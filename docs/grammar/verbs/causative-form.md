# Causative Form in Japanese
## 〜せる / 〜させる — Make / Let Someone Do Something {#causative}
---
## Quick Summary {#quick-summary}
### Mental Model
• “Cause someone to do something” → make / let / have
### Core Idea
• Adds a second person (the doer)
• Subject becomes the one **causing** the action
---
## Core Meaning {#core-meaning}
• Expresses:
  - making someone do something
  - letting someone do something
  - having someone do something
> <ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>に<ruby>野菜<rt>やさい</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べさせる。
**Natural English:** Make the child eat vegetables.  
**Literal English:** Cause the child to eat vegetables.
---
## How It Works {#how-it-works}
### Structure
| Role | Particle | Example |
|------|----------|--------|
| Causer | は / が | <ruby>母<rt>はは</rt></ruby>は |
| Person acting | に | <ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>に |
| Action | causative verb | <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べさせる |
---
## Formation Rules {#formation}
### Ichidan Verbs (る verbs)
• Drop る → add させる
| Base | Causative |
|------|-----------|
| <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べる | <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べさせる |
| <ruby>見<rt>み</rt></ruby>る | <ruby>見<rt>み</rt></ruby>させる |
---
### Godan Verbs (う verbs)
• Change final sound to あ-row + せる
| Base | Causative |
|------|-----------|
| <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>く | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>かせる |
| <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>く | <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かせる |
| <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>す | <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>させる |
---
### Irregular Verbs
| Base | Causative |
|------|-----------|
| する | させる |
| <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>る | <ruby>来<rt>こ</rt></ruby>させる |
---
## Nuance Comparison {#nuance-comparison}
### Make vs Let
| Meaning | Example | Nuance |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Make | <ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>させる | Obligation / pressure |
| Let | <ruby>遊<rt>あそ</rt></ruby>ばせる | Permission / freedom |
> Context determines whether it means “make” or “let”
---
### Micro Contrast
• <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>けばいい → suggestion  
• <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かせる → causative (make/let go)
---
## Grammar Analysis {#grammar-analysis}
### Function in Context
> <ruby>母<rt>はは</rt></ruby>は<ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>に<ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>させた。
• Introduces a second actor  
• Subject becomes controller of action  
• Action becomes indirect  
---
### Particle Behavior
| Pattern | Meaning |
|--------|--------|
| に | person being made to act |
| を | object of the action |
---
## Pattern Expansion {#pattern-expansion}
### Common Builds
• 〜させている → making someone do (ongoing)  
• 〜させておく → let someone do in advance  
• 〜させられる → be made to do (causative-passive)
---
### Upgrade / Evolution Path
• Can form causative and understand “make/let” meaning  
• Still requires effort to interpret nuance from context  
• Becomes automatic through exposure at N2+
---
## Common Mistakes
### Incorrect particle
❌ <ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>を<ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>させる  
✔ <ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>に<ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>させる  
• The person doing the action takes に
---
### Overusing “force”
• Not always “force” — often neutral or permissive
---
## Register and Formality
### Casual
> <ruby>やらせる</ruby>  
Natural: Make / let them do  
Shift: Direct, everyday
---
### Polite
> <ruby>やらせます</ruby>  
Natural: I will have them do it  
Shift: Neutral polite
---
### Extra Polite
> <ruby>やらせていただきます</ruby>  
Natural: I will humbly do it  
Shift: Formal / business
---
### Humble
> <ruby>させていただきます</ruby>  
Natural: I will (humbly) do  
Shift: Deferential
---
## Examples {#examples}
> <ruby>先生<rt>せんせい</rt></ruby>は<ruby>学生<rt>がくせい</rt></ruby>に<ruby>本<rt>ほん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>ませた。  
Natural: The teacher made the students read a book.  
Literal: The teacher caused the students to read a book.  
Context: Instruction
---
> <ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>に<ruby>好<rt>す</rt></ruby>きなことをやらせている。  
Natural: I let my child do what they like.  
Literal: I am letting the child do what they like.  
Context: Parenting
---
> <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>に<ruby>説明<rt>せつめい</rt></ruby>させた。  
Natural: I had him explain it.  
Literal: I caused him to explain it.  
Context: Delegation
---
## Hidden Points
• Causative ≠ always “force”  
• Often expresses control, permission, or delegation  
• Very common in instruction and parenting  
• Frequently appears in combined forms
