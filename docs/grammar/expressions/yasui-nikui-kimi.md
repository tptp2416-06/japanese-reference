# Ease and Difficulty — 〜やすい・〜にくい

## <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい・<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい・<ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じがたい

---

## JLPT Level {#jlpt-level}

!!! abstract "JLPT Level — N4 Core / Lower N3 Extended"
    〜やすい and 〜にくい are foundational N4 patterns — verb-stem adjectives that describe inherent ease or difficulty of an action. But the full ecosystem of "difficulty" in Japanese (〜にくい vs. 〜づらい vs. 〜がたい) is solidly N3, and learners often conflate them or overuse <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい where a stem-adjective would be more natural.

    | Tier | Pattern | Description | Example |
    |------|---------|-------------|---------|
    | N5 — Foundation | <ruby>簡単<rt>かんたん</rt></ruby>だ / <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい | Objective difficulty (standalone adjectives) | この<ruby>本<rt>ほん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい。 |
    | N5 — Foundation | <ruby>上手<rt>じょうず</rt></ruby>だ / <ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>だ | Personal skill level | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐのが<ruby>上手<rt>じょうず</rt></ruby>。 |
    | **N4 — Core ← You are here** | **〜やすい / 〜にくい** | **Inherent ease/difficulty of an action** | <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい。<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい。 |
    | N3 — Extended | **〜づらい** | **Physical or psychological discomfort in doing** | <ruby>持<rt>も</rt></ruby>ちづらい。<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いづらい。 |
    | N3 — Extended | **〜がたい** | **Emotional resistance; nearly impossible** | <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じがたい。<ruby>許<rt>ゆる</rt></ruby>しがたい。 |
    | N3 — Extended | **〜かねる** | **Hesitation due to position / inability to bring oneself to do** | <ruby>承諾<rt>しょうだく</rt></ruby>しかねる。 |

## Quick Summary {#quick-summary}

!!! tip "At a glance"
    - 〜やすい attaches to a verb stem to create an i-adjective meaning "easy to [verb]." <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>む → <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい (easy to drink). It describes the action as low-friction.
    - 〜にくい attaches to a verb stem to create an i-adjective meaning "hard to [verb]." <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>む → <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みにくい (hard to drink). It describes the action as high-friction.
    - These forms describe **the inherent property of the action or object**, not the person's ability. <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい means "difficult to understand" (the explanation is unclear); <ruby>理解力<rt>りかいりょく</rt></ruby>がない means "lacking comprehension ability" (the person is at fault).
    - At N3, the difficulty spectrum expands: 〜にくい (general friction), 〜づらい (physical/mental discomfort), 〜がたい (emotional resistance bordering on impossibility).
    - <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい and <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい are among the most frequently used collocations in all of Japanese — worth learning as single vocabulary items.

## Core Meaning {#core-meaning}

!!! note "Core Rule"
    Use 〜やすい when an action proceeds smoothly due to the nature of the object, method, or circumstances. Use 〜にくい when an action meets resistance due to the same. These are **property descriptions**, not ability judgments.

| このワインは<ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい。 | This wine is easy to drink [smooth, approachable]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: This wine drink-easy is.* |
| | *Context: Describing the wine's inherent quality. Not "I am good at drinking wine" but "The wine goes down easily."* |

| この<ruby>説明書<rt>せつめいしょ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい。 | This manual is hard to understand [poorly written]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: This manual understand-hard is.* |
| | *Context: Describing the manual's clarity. The fault lies in the document, not necessarily the reader.* |

| このペンは<ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい。 | This pen is easy to write with [good grip, smooth flow]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: This pen write-easy is.* |
| | *Context: The pen's physical properties make writing smooth. A judgment about the tool, not the writer.* |

## The Difficulty Spectrum: N4 to N3 {#the-difficulty-spectrum}

Japanese does not have one word for "difficult." It has a **gradient of friction** that depends on whether the resistance is physical, emotional, or logical.

| Form | Core Meaning | Type of Difficulty | Example | JLPT |
|------|------------|-------------------|---------|------|
| 〜やすい | Easy to do | Low friction | <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい | N4 |
| 〜にくい | Hard to do | General resistance; inherent difficulty | <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みにくい | N4 |
| 〜づらい | Hard to do | Physical strain or social/psychological awkwardness | <ruby>持<rt>も</rt></ruby>ちづらい | N3 |
| 〜がたい | Hard to do | Emotional resistance; nearly impossible | <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じがたい | N3 |
| 〜かねる | Cannot bring oneself to do | Position-based hesitation | <ruby>承諾<rt>しょうだく</rt></ruby>しかねる | N3 |
| <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい | Difficult (standalone adj) | Complex, high-skill | <ruby>問題<rt>もんだい</rt></ruby>が<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい | N5 |
| <ruby>簡単<rt>かんたん</rt></ruby>だ | Simple (standalone adj) | Low complexity | <ruby>問題<rt>もんだい</rt></ruby>が<ruby>簡単<rt>かんたん</rt></ruby>だ | N5 |

### The Critical Distinction

| この<ruby>料理<rt>りょうり</rt></ruby>は<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べにくい。 | This dish is hard to eat [texture, temperature, size]. |
|---|---|
| | *Describes the dish's physical properties. Maybe it's too hot, too tough, or awkwardly shaped.* |

| この<ruby>料理<rt>りょうり</rt></ruby>は<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べづらい。 | This dish is awkward/uncomfortable to eat [socially or physically]. |
|---|---|
| | *Describes discomfort. Maybe you're wearing white clothes and it's messy, or the setting is formal and the food requires messy hands.* |

| この<ruby>料理<rt>りょうり</rt></ruby>は<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べがたい。 | This dish is unthinkable to eat [emotional revulsion]. |
|---|---|
| | *Extreme. Maybe it contains something sacred or taboo to the eater. Emotional impossibility.* |

| この<ruby>料理<rt>りょうり</rt></ruby>は<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい。 | This dish is difficult [to make]. |
|---|---|
| | *If applied to eating, sounds odd — <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい describes complexity, not eating friction.* |

## In Real Life {#in-real-life}

!!! example "In Real Life"
    **Situation:** ユキ is at an electronics store with her friend ハル. They're looking at smartphones. Yuki picks one up, swipes the screen, types a few characters, then sets it down and picks up another.

    &gt; ユキ：「<ruby>前<rt>まえ</rt></ruby>のは<ruby>画面<rt>がめん</rt></ruby>が<ruby>小<rt>ちい</rt></ruby>さくて<ruby>押<rt>お</rt></ruby>しにくかった。これは<ruby>文字<rt>もじ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>大<rt>おお</rt></ruby>きくて<ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすい。」

    *Yui: "The previous one had a small screen and was hard to press. This one has large text and is easy to read."*

    **Why 〜やすい / 〜にくい fits here:** Yuki is evaluating the devices as tools. She is not saying she is bad at typing (<ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>) or that the phone is complex (<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい). She is describing the physical interaction: the buttons resist or yield. This is exactly the domain of stem-adjectives.

    **What <ruby>押<rt>お</rt></ruby>しにくい captures:** It means "hard to press" — maybe the buttons are too small, too stiff, or too close together. The difficulty is in the object's design.

    **What <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすい captures:** It means "easy to read" — the font, contrast, and size make reading smooth. The ease is in the display's properties.

    **What makes this natural:** Japanese product reviews, cooking instructions, and UI feedback rely heavily on these forms. They are the standard grammar of tool evaluation.

## How It Works {#how-it-works}

〜やすい and 〜にくい are suffixes that attach to the **verb stem** (the form used before ます). The result is a fully functional i-adjective that conjugates like <ruby>高<rt>たか</rt></ruby>い or <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい.

**Layer 1 — Verb stem:** Remove the る from ichidan verbs, or shift godan verbs to their i-stem (the form before ます).

**Layer 2 — Suffix attachment:** Add やすい or にくい directly to the stem.

**Layer 3 — Adjective grammar:** The resulting word is an i-adjective. It can take です, drop the copula in casual speech, be adverbialized with く, and be nominalized with の or こと.

| Verb Type | Dictionary | Stem | + やすい | + にくい |
|-----------|-----------|------|----------|----------|
| Godan く | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>く | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>き | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きにくい |
| Godan ぐ | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐ | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎ | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎやすい | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎにくい |
| Godan す | <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>す | <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>し | <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>しやすい | <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>しにくい |
| Godan つ | <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>つ | <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ち | <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ちやすい | <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ちにくい |
| Godan ぬ | <ruby>死<rt>し</rt></ruby>ぬ | <ruby>死<rt>し</rt></ruby>に | <ruby>死<rt>し</rt></ruby>にやすい | <ruby>死<rt>し</rt></ruby>ににくい |
| Godan ぶ | <ruby>遊<rt>あそ</rt></ruby>ぶ | <ruby>遊<rt>あそ</rt></ruby>び | <ruby>遊<rt>あそ</rt></ruby>びやすい | <ruby>遊<rt>あそ</rt></ruby>びにくい |
| Godan む | <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>む | <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>み | <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすい | <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みにくい |
| Godan る | <ruby>帰<rt>かえ</rt></ruby>る | <ruby>帰<rt>かえ</rt></ruby>り | <ruby>帰<rt>かえ</rt></ruby>りやすい | <ruby>帰<rt>かえ</rt></ruby>りにくい |
| Ichidan | <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べる | <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べ | <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べやすい | <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べにくい |
| Irregular する | する | し | しやすい | しにくい |
| Irregular くる | くる | き | <ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>やすい | <ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>にくい |

!!! warning "する and くる are slightly irregular"
    する → しやすい (not さやすい). くる → <ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>やすい (not きやすい in the expected godan pattern — it follows the <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>る conjugation).

### Real Speech vs. Textbook Form

Textbooks present these as straightforward adjective formation. In real speech, certain collocations are so common they function as independent vocabulary.

| Form | Frequency | Note |
|------|-----------|------|
| <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい / <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい | Extremely high | The canonical pair for clarity |
| <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすい / <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みにくい | Very high | Book and font evaluation |
| <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい / <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みにくい | High | Food and beverage reviews |
| <ruby>使<rt>つか</rt></ruby>いやすい / <ruby>使<rt>つか</rt></ruby>いにくい | Very high | Tool and software evaluation |
| <ruby>聞<rt>き</rt></ruby>きやすい / <ruby>聞<rt>き</rt></ruby>きにくい | High | Voice, music, acoustics |
| <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いやすい / <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いにくい | High | Difficulty of expression |
| <ruby>生<rt>い</rt></ruby>きにくい | Moderate | "Hard to live" — social commentary |
| <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じにくい | Moderate | Skepticism |
| ありにくい / なりにくい | Low | Rare; usually expressed other ways |

## Grammar Analysis {#grammar-analysis}

### Function in Context

- このペンは<ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい: このペンは marks the topic. <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい is the predicate i-adjective. No copula needed in casual speech; です added in polite.
- <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ちにくかった: Past tense of <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ちにくい. As an i-adjective, past is formed with かった.
- <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすくない: Negative of <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい. The くない follows i-adjective rules.
- <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくくて、<ruby>困<rt>こま</rt></ruby>った: Te-form of the i-adjective (くて) connecting to a result. "It was hard to understand, so I was troubled."

| この<ruby>本<rt>ほん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすくて、<ruby>面白<rt>おもしろ</rt></ruby>い。 | This book is easy to read and interesting. |
|---|---|
| | *The やすくて connects the ease of reading to the interest. Both are properties of the book.* |

| <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>の<ruby>話<rt>はなし</rt></ruby>は<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくかった。 | What he said was hard to understand. |
|---|---|
| | *Past tense. The difficulty is attributed to his speech, not the listener's intelligence.* |

### Construction {#construction}

| Form | Result | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Present affirmative | stem + やすい / にくい | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい |
| Present negative | stem + やすくない / にくくない | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすくない |
| Past affirmative | stem + やすかった / にくかった | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすかった |
| Past negative | stem + やすくなかった / にくくなかった | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすくなかった |
| Adverbial | stem + やすく / にくく | やすく<ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>ける |
| Nominalized | stem + やすさ / にくさ | <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすさ |

!!! note "The さ nominalization"
    やすさ and にくさ turn the adjective into a noun: <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすさ = "readability / ease of reading." This is common in product specs and design discussions.

## Japanese Mindset {#japanese-mindset}

Japanese tool-evaluation grammar avoids blaming the user. When a manual is unclear, Japanese says <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりにくい (hard to understand) — a property of the text. English might say "This is confusing" or even "I don't get it," which locates the problem in the reader or in a vague atmosphere.

This reflects a broader tendency to **attribute friction to the interaction or the object rather than the person**. 〜やすい and 〜にくい are the grammar of **user experience before the concept existed** — they evaluate how the world meets the body and mind.

- **Object-centered evaluation.** <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい evaluates the wine. <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい<ruby>人<rt>ひと</rt></ruby> would evaluate the drinker (unusual). The default is to describe the world, not the self.
- **Friction as inherent property.** Difficulty is not a challenge to overcome (as in English "difficult but rewarding"); it is a property to note and possibly avoid. <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べにくい is a warning, not a gauntlet.
- **The body in grammar.** <ruby>持<rt>も</rt></ruby>ちづらい (hard to hold) and <ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>りにくい (hard to sit in) embed physical experience into adjectives. Japanese grammar does not separate the observer from the observed as cleanly as English.

## Nuance Comparison {#nuance-comparison}

| Grammar Point | Core Meaning | Type of Resistance | Target | JLPT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 〜やすい | Easy to do | Low friction | Action / object property | N4 |
| 〜にくい | Hard to do | General resistance (physical, logical, design) | Action / object property | N4 |
| 〜づらい | Hard to do | Physical strain or social awkwardness | Action / body position | N3 |
| 〜がたい | Hard to do | Emotional resistance; nearly impossible | Action / moral boundary | N3 |
| 〜かねる | Hesitate to do | Position, propriety, inability to commit | Social action | N3 |
| <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい | Difficult | Complex, high skill requirement | Object / task | N5 |
| <ruby>簡単<rt>かんたん</rt></ruby>だ | Simple | Low complexity | Object / task | N5 |
| <ruby>上手<rt>じょうず</rt></ruby>だ | Skilled | High personal ability | Person | N5 |
| <ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>だ | Unskilled | Low personal ability | Person | N5 |

**〜にくい vs. <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい:** この<ruby>問題<rt>もんだい</rt></ruby>は<ruby>解<rt>と</rt></ruby>きにくい = "This problem is hard to solve [the setup is awkward]." この<ruby>問題<rt>もんだい</rt></ruby>は<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい = "This problem is difficult [high intellectual demand]." The first is about solving friction; the second is about complexity.

**〜にくい vs. 〜づらい:** <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べにくい = "hard to eat [texture, size]." <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べづらい = "awkward to eat [position, social context, physical discomfort]." The overlap is large, but づらい emphasizes the eater's discomfort more than the food's property.

**〜にくい vs. 〜がたい:** <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じにくい = "hard to believe [the evidence is weak]." <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じがたい = "unthinkable to believe [emotional shock]." The first is skeptical; the second is devastated.

## Alternatives {#alternatives}

### <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい / <ruby>簡単<rt>かんたん</rt></ruby>だ (standalone adjectives)

Objective complexity, not interaction friction.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | この<ruby>数学<rt>すうがく</rt></ruby>の<ruby>問題<rt>もんだい</rt></ruby>は<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい。 |
| **English** | "This math problem is difficult." |
| **Intent** | The problem requires high skill or knowledge. |
| **vs. 〜にくい** | <ruby>解<rt>と</rt></ruby>きにくい would mean the problem is awkwardly formulated, not necessarily advanced. |

### <ruby>上手<rt>じょうず</rt></ruby>だ / <ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>だ (personal skill)

Ability of the person, not property of the object.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐのが<ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>だ。 |
| **English** | "He's bad at swimming." |
| **Intent** | The person lacks skill. |
| **vs. 〜にくい** | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎにくい would mean the water/current makes swimming hard, not that the swimmer is unskilled. |

### 〜づらい (N3 — physical/social discomfort)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | この<ruby>椅子<rt>いす</rt></ruby>は<ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>りづらい。 |
| **English** | "This chair is uncomfortable to sit in." |
| **Intent** | Physical or postural awkwardness. |
| **vs. 〜にくい** | <ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>りにくい focuses on the act of sitting being difficult (maybe too low). <ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>りづらい focuses on sustained discomfort (bad back support, social visibility). |

### 〜がたい (N3 — emotional impossibility)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>の<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>うことを<ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じがたい。 |
| **English** | "I find what he says almost impossible to believe." |
| **Intent** | Emotional resistance. The facts may support it, but the heart rejects it. |
| **vs. 〜にくい** | <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じにくい is skeptical. <ruby>信<rt>しん</rt></ruby>じがたい is wounded or shocked. |

## Register and Formality {#register-and-formality}

The forms are register-neutral. Politeness is carried by the copula and context.

=== ":material-chat-outline: Casual"
    このビール、<ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすい。

    *"This beer goes down easy."*

    **Shift:** No copula. Direct property description. Common in casual reviews.

=== ":material-tie: Basic Polite"
    このビールは<ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすいです。

    *"This beer is easy to drink."*

    **Shift:** です added. Standard polite evaluation.

=== ":material-briefcase-outline: Extra Polite"
    この<ruby>商品<rt>しょうひん</rt></ruby>はお<ruby>客様<rt>きゃくさま</rt></ruby>に<ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすくご<ruby>用意<rt>ようい</rt></ruby>しております。

    *"We have prepared this product to be easy for our customers to drink."*

    **Shift:** お<ruby>客様<rt>きゃくさま</rt></ruby> (honorific), ご<ruby>用意<rt>ようい</rt></ruby>しております (humble). The <ruby>飲<rt>の</rt></ruby>みやすく is adverbialized to modify the preparation.

## Examples {#examples}

| このペンは<ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい。 | This pen is easy to write with. |

## Common Mistakes {#common-mistakes}

### 1. Confusing 〜にくい with personal inability

!!! failure "Attribution error"
    <ruby>私<rt>わたし</rt></ruby>は<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎにくい。

    ~This sounds like "I am hard to swim" — a property of your body, not your skill. For personal inability, use <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐのが<ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>だ or <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎ<ruby>方<rt>かた</rt></ruby>がわからない.~

!!! success "Correct"
    このプールは<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぎにくい。 (The pool is hard to swim in.)
    <ruby>私<rt>わたし</rt></ruby>は<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐのが<ruby>下手<rt>へた</rt></ruby>だ。 (I'm bad at swimming.)

### 2. Using <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい where 〜にくい is more natural

!!! failure "Register mismatch"
    このボタンは<ruby>押<rt>お</rt></ruby>すのが<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい。

    ~<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい describes complexity or high skill. A button is not intellectually demanding — it is physically resistant or poorly placed. Use <ruby>押<rt>お</rt></ruby>しにくい.~

!!! success "Correct"
    このボタンは<ruby>押<rt>お</rt></ruby>しにくい。

### 3. Overusing 〜がたい for ordinary difficulty

!!! failure "Emotional overkill"
    この<ruby>漢字<rt>かんじ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みがたい。

    ~がたい carries emotional or moral weight. For ordinary reading difficulty, use <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みにくい or <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい.~

!!! success "Correct"
    この<ruby>漢字<rt>かんじ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みにくい。

### 4. Wrong stem formation with godan verbs

!!! failure "Stem error"
    <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>くやすい。

    ~Godan verbs require the i-stem (<ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>き), not the dictionary form.~

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすい。

## Hidden Points {#hidden-points}

- **<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい is often used as praise for people, not just documents.** A teacher is <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい. A presenter is <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい. This extends the "object property" metaphor to people — their explanations have the quality of clarity.
- **〜やすい can imply vulnerability.** <ruby>病気<rt>びょうき</rt></ruby>になりやすい means "prone to illness" — easy for disease to take hold. <ruby>間違<rt>まちが</rt></ruby>えやすい means "prone to mistakes." The "ease" is not always positive.
- **〜にくい can imply resilience positively.** <ruby>腐<rt>くさ</rt></ruby>りにくい means "resistant to rotting" — a good property for food. <ruby>壊<rt>こわ</rt></ruby>れにくい means "durable." The "difficulty" can be a benefit.
- **The さ nominalization is common in tech and design.** <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>みやすさ (readability), <ruby>使<rt>つか</rt></ruby>いやすさ (usability), <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きやすさ (writability) are standard professional terms. They turn subjective experience into measurable properties.
- **<ruby>生<rt>い</rt></ruby>きにくい carries social weight.** In discussions of mental health, social pressure, or economic hardship, <ruby>生<rt>い</rt></ruby>きにくい is the standard grammar for "hard to live [in this society]." It is stronger than <ruby>大変<rt>たいへん</rt></ruby> because it locates the difficulty in the structure of life itself.
- **<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いにくい is the grammar of taboo.** When a topic is embarrassing, socially risky, or emotionally loaded, Japanese speakers say <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いにくいんだけど… before broaching it. The form functions as a social warning signal.
- **〜やすい / 〜にくい can be used adverbially.** やすく<ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>ける ("can write easily") and にくく<ruby>壊<rt>こわ</rt></ruby>れる ("breaks with difficulty / is hard to break") are standard. The adverbial form modifies the verb that follows.

## Learner Stage {#learner-stage}

| Stage | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Now — N4** | Produce 〜やすい and 〜にくい correctly from verb stems; distinguish them from <ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい / <ruby>簡単<rt>かんたん</rt></ruby>だ; use common collocations (<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい, <ruby>使<rt>つか</rt></ruby>いやすい) naturally; avoid attributing them to persons |
| **Developing — N4→N3** | Deploy 〜づらい for physical/social discomfort; use 〜がたい for emotional resistance; choose between にくい and づらい based on type of friction; nominalize with 〜やすさ / にくさ |
| **Automatic — N3→N2** | The difficulty spectrum becomes intuitive — you feel why <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べにくい is about food design, <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べづらい is about social context, and <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べがたい is about moral revulsion. You no longer translate "difficult" but select the right Japanese friction type. |
| **Mastery** | You control the full ease/difficulty architecture: やすい (smooth), にくい (resistant), づらい (awkward), がたい (unthinkable), かねる (hesitant). You know when to praise with <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりやすい, when to warn with <ruby>壊<rt>こわ</rt></ruby>れにくい, and when to mourn with <ruby>生<rt>い</rt></ruby>きにくい. |
