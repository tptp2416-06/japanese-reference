# Embedded Questions — 〜か / 〜かどうか

## <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか、<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。

---

## JLPT Level {#jlpt-level}

!!! abstract "JLPT Level — Upper N4 / Lower N3"
    The basic pattern of embedding a plain question with 〜か is N4. But the full system — distinguishing 〜か from 〜かどうか, knowing when particles drop, and controlling the scope of uncertainty — is solidly lower N3. Native speakers make these choices automatically; learners often stumble over whether to include どうか or whether が should stay.

    | Tier | Pattern | Description | Example |
    |------|---------|-------------|---------|
    | N5 — Foundation | Plain questions | Standalone questions with か | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ますか？ |
    | N4 — Foundation | 〜か (embedded) | Embedding a question as a noun clause | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 |
    | **N4 — Core ← You are here** | **〜かどうか** | **Explicit yes/no embedding — "whether or not"** | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 |
    | N3 — Extended | Particle dropping | が/は often omitted in embedded clauses | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか… (very casual) |
    | N3 — Extended | Double embedding | 〜か〜か + どうか in lists | <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>くか<ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かないか、<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 |
    | N2 — Awareness | 〜かのように | "As if…" — hypothetical embedding | <ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>っているかのように<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>す。 |

## Quick Summary {#quick-summary}

!!! tip "At a glance"
    - 〜か turns a question into a noun-like clause that can be inserted into a larger sentence — as the object of <ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>る, <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かる, <ruby>聞<rt>き</rt></ruby>く, <ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>する, etc.
    - 〜かどうか explicitly embeds a yes/no question, marking both alternatives: "whether [X] or not." It is clearer, more explicit, and often preferred in formal contexts.
    - With question words (<ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>, <ruby>誰<rt>だれ</rt></ruby>, どこ, etc.), only 〜か is used — never 〜かどうか. <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるかどうか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らない is ungrammatical because "what" is not a yes/no question.
    - With yes/no questions, both 〜か and 〜かどうか work, but the nuance differs: 〜か is neutral and compact; 〜かどうか is explicit about the uncertainty between two outcomes.
    - Particles が and は are often dropped in embedded clauses, especially in casual speech. <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか becomes <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか in fast conversation.

## Core Meaning {#core-meaning}

!!! note "Core Rule"
    Use 〜か to embed any question (wh- or yes/no) into a larger statement. Use 〜かどうか when you want to explicitly mark that a yes/no question is being embedded — emphasizing the uncertainty between the positive and negative outcome.

| <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 | I don't know if he'll come [embedded yes/no]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: He come-question don't-know.* |
| | *Context: Neutral embedding. The か alone carries the yes/no ambiguity.* |

| <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 | I don't know whether he'll come or not [explicit yes/no]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: He come-question-or-not don't-know.* |
| | *Context: The どうか makes the two alternatives explicit. Slightly more formal and clearer than か alone.* |

| <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>が<ruby>起<rt>お</rt></ruby>きたか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>りたい。 | I want to know what happened [embedded wh-question]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: What happened-question want-to-know.* |
| | *Context: Wh-question embedding. Only か is possible here — かどうか cannot follow <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>.* |

| <ruby>日本<rt>にっぽん</rt></ruby><ruby>語<rt>ご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるかどうか<ruby>聞<rt>き</rt></ruby>いてください。 | Please ask whether [they] can speak Japanese or not. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: Japanese speak-can-question-or-not ask-please.* |
| | *Context: Explicit yes/no embedding in a request. The かどうか makes the binary nature of the inquiry clear.* |

## In Real Life {#in-real-life}

!!! example "In Real Life"
    **Situation:** ユイ is organizing a weekend picnic. She needs to know if her friend ケン is coming because she has to buy food. She texts another friend who might know.

    > ケンが<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか、<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない？

    *"Do you know whether Ken is coming or not?"*

    **Why かどうか fits here:** Yui could have said ケンが<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない — grammatically fine, but slightly less explicit. The かどうか makes the binary nature of the uncertainty clear: he either comes or he doesn't, and she needs to know which. The comma pause after かどうか creates a small space of suspense before the main question.

    **What the どうか adds:** It transforms a vague "if" into a clear "whether or not." This is especially useful when the listener needs to understand that both outcomes matter — not just the positive one.

    **What makes this natural:** In planning contexts, かどうか is extremely common because plans require binary decisions. The explicitness prevents misunderstanding.

## How It Works {#how-it-works}

Embedding a question in Japanese is structurally simple but pragmatically layered. The question clause is nominalized by か (and optionally どうか), then placed in a grammatical slot that normally takes a noun — typically as the object of a verb like <ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>る, <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かる, <ruby>聞<rt>き</rt></ruby>く, <ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>する, <ruby>決<rt>き</rt></ruby>める, or <ruby>考<rt>かんが</rt></ruby>える.

**Layer 1 — The embedded clause:** Take a plain-form question (not the polite 〜ますか form) and replace the question mark with か. <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ますか？ → <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか.

**Layer 2 — The explicit marker (optional):** For yes/no questions, add どうか after か to make both alternatives explicit: <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか.

**Layer 3 — The main clause:** The embedded clause functions as the object (or sometimes subject) of the main predicate. <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか + <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない.

| <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか | Whether he comes [compact] |
|---|---|
| | *The か alone carries the uncertainty. The listener understands it's a yes/no question from context.* |

| <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか | Whether he comes or not [explicit] |
|---|---|
| | *The どうか spells out the negative alternative. More formal, more precise, more planning-friendly.* |

### Real Speech vs. Textbook Form

Textbooks often present 〜か and 〜かどうか as interchangeable for yes/no questions. In real speech, the choice is meaningful: 〜か is neutral and compact; 〜かどうか is explicit and often carries a sense of "I need a definitive answer."

| Form | Example | Note |
|------|---------|------|
| Textbook (neutral) | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 | Standard example — grammatically correct, fully natural |
| Textbook (explicit) | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 | Standard example — grammatically correct, fully natural |
| Real speech (wh-embedded) | <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>が<ruby>起<rt>お</rt></ruby>きたか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>りたい。 | Wh-question — only か possible |
| Real speech (particle drop) | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか… | Casual particle drop — が omitted |
| Real speech (double form) | <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>くか<ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かないか、<ruby>決<rt>き</rt></ruby>めていない。 | Both alternatives stated explicitly |

## Grammar Analysis {#grammar-analysis}

### Function in Context

- ケンが<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか is the embedded clause functioning as the object of <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない. The comma after かどうか is optional but common — it marks the boundary between the embedded question and the main predicate.
- <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない is the main predicate — "don't know." It requires a noun-like object: "I don't know [the answer to this question]."
- The どうか explicitly marks the negative alternative: "come or not-come." Without it, the negative alternative is implied but not stated.
- The question clause uses plain form (<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>る), not polite form (<ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ます). Embedding 〜ますか is ungrammatical: ケンが<ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ますか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない is wrong.
- Without かどうか: ケンが<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない is softer and more compact. It doesn't emphasize the binary nature as strongly. In casual speech, this is often preferred.

| これが<ruby>正<rt>ただ</rt></ruby>しいか<ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>してください。 | Please check whether this is correct. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: This correct-question confirm-please.* |
| | *Context: 〜か alone is sufficient because the context makes the yes/no nature obvious. Adding どうか would be redundant but not wrong.* |

### Construction {#construction}

| Question Type | Embedded Form | Example | かどうか Possible? |
|---------------|---------------|---------|------------------|
| Yes/no (plain) | verb-plain + か | <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか | Optional |
| Yes/no (explicit) | verb-plain + かどうか | <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか | Yes |
| Yes/no (negative explicit) | verb-plain + か + verb-nai + か | <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>来<rt>こ</rt></ruby>ないか | Yes (literary) |
| Wh-question (<ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>) | <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby> + predicate + か | <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるか | No |
| Wh-question (<ruby>誰<rt>だれ</rt></ruby>) | <ruby>誰<rt>だれ</rt></ruby> + predicate + か | <ruby>誰<rt>だれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか | No |
| Wh-question (どこ) | どこ + predicate + か | どこに<ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>くか | No |
| Wh-question (いつ) | いつ + predicate + か | いつ<ruby>帰<rt>かえ</rt></ruby>るか | No |
| Wh-question (どれ) | どれ + predicate + か | どれがいいか | No |
| Wh-question (なぜ) | なぜ + predicate + か | なぜ<ruby>怒<rt>おこ</rt></ruby>ったか | No |

!!! warning "かどうか is only for yes/no"
    かどうか cannot follow question words (<ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>, <ruby>誰<rt>だれ</rt></ruby>, どこ, etc.) because those are not yes/no questions. <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるかどうか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らない is ungrammatical.

!!! warning "Plain form required"
    The embedded clause must use plain form, not polite 〜ます form. <ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ますか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない is ungrammatical. The politeness of the main sentence is carried by the main verb: <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりません (polite).

### Particle Dropping in Embedded Clauses

In embedded questions, が and は are frequently dropped — especially in casual speech.

| Full | Casual / Fast Speech |
|------|----------------------|
| <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby><ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか |
| <ruby>雨<rt>あめ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>降<rt>ふ</rt></ruby>るかどうか | <ruby>雨<rt>あめ</rt></ruby><ruby>降<rt>ふ</rt></ruby>るかどうか |
| <ruby>彼女<rt>かのじょ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>ったか | <ruby>彼女<rt>かのじょ</rt></ruby><ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby><ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>ったか |

!!! note "The drop is optional"
    Omitting particles is casual but not ungrammatical in standard Japanese. In formal writing, particles are usually retained.

## Japanese Mindset {#japanese-mindset}

Japanese is a language that **nominalizes freely**. Where English uses "whether" or "if" as conjunctions, Japanese turns the entire question into a noun-like block and drops it into a sentence slot. This reflects a broader pattern: Japanese prefers **clause nominalization** over **conjunction chaining**.

The かどうか pattern also reveals a cultural preference for **explicit binary framing**. In planning, decision-making, and formal contexts, Japanese speakers often want to mark both alternatives clearly — not just "if yes" but "whether yes or no." This prevents the ambiguity of English "if," which can mean both "whether" and "in the event that."

- **Questions as objects.** In Japanese, a question is not just an utterance act — it can be a grammatical object, a thing to be known, decided, or checked. <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか is "the matter of whether [someone] comes." This reification is natural in Japanese but takes adjustment for English speakers.
- **Binary explicitness as clarity.** かどうか marks both poles of the uncertainty. In contexts where miscommunication would be costly (plans, medical questions, legal matters), this explicitness is preferred.
- **Particle drop as intimacy.** Dropping が in embedded questions signals casualness and trust. The listener can parse the clause without grammatical scaffolding. This is a subtle intimacy marker.

## Nuance Comparison {#nuance-comparison}

| Grammar Point | Core Meaning | Explicitness / Nuance | Trigger Context | JLPT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 〜か (embedded) | "Whether…" / "if…" [embedded] | Neutral, compact; leaves alternatives implied | General embedding; wh-questions; casual yes/no | N4 |
| 〜かどうか | "Whether… or not" | Explicit binary; both alternatives marked | Yes/no questions where clarity matters; formal contexts | N4 |
| 〜か〜ないか | "Whether… or not" [literary] | Both alternatives stated in full | Written Japanese; emphatic; literary | N3 |
| 〜かのように | "As if…" | Hypothetical embedding; pretend knowledge | Describing behavior that assumes something | N2 |
| 〜かと<ruby>思<rt>おも</rt></ruby>った | "I wondered if…" | Past self-questioning; relief or surprise | Reacting to something that resolved an uncertainty | N3 |
| 〜かもしれない | "Maybe…" | Possibility, not embedding | Expressing uncertainty directly (not as object) | N4 |

The closest lookalike is 〜かもしれない. But かもしれない is a sentence-ending possibility marker (<ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかもしれない — "He might come"), not an embedded clause. The grammatical function is completely different.

## Alternatives {#alternatives}

### 〜か (compact embedding)

The minimal form. Same function, less explicit about the binary.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。 |
| **English** | "I don't know if he'll come." |
| **Intent** | Neutral, compact, sufficient for most contexts |
| **vs. かどうか** | Less explicit. In casual speech, often preferred. Can feel slightly less formal or less urgent. |

!!! tip "When to use this instead"
    Use 〜か alone when the yes/no nature is obvious from context, when speaking casually, or when brevity matters. It is never wrong for yes/no embedding.

### 〜か〜ないか (literary double form)

States both alternatives explicitly with full verbs.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>くか<ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かないか、<ruby>決<rt>き</rt></ruby>めていない。 |
| **English** | "I haven't decided whether to go or not." |
| **Intent** | Literary, emphatic, written style |
| **vs. かどうか** | More formal and weighty than かどうか. Common in novels, essays, and dramatic speech. Sounds heavy in casual conversation. |

### 〜かもしれない (possibility marker)

Not an embedding pattern — a sentence-ending expression of possibility.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかもしれない。 |
| **English** | "He might come." |
| **Intent** | Direct expression of uncertainty |
| **vs. embedded か** | This is a main-clause pattern, not an embedded clause. It cannot function as the object of <ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>る or <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かる. |

## Register and Formality {#register-and-formality}

The explicitness of かどうか makes it slightly more formal than か alone, but both are acceptable across most registers.

=== ":material-chat-outline: Casual"
    ケン<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。

    *"Don't know if Ken's coming."*

    **Shift:** Particle drop (が omitted), か alone. Ultra-casual, text-message friendly.

=== ":material-chat-outline: Casual (explicit)"
    ケンが<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか、<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>ってる？

    *"Do you know whether Ken is coming or not?"*

    **Shift:** かどうか adds explicitness even in casual speech. The comma pause is common. The <ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>ってる is casual.

=== ":material-tie: Basic Polite"
    ケンさんが<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか、<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かりますか。

    *"Do you know whether Ken is coming or not?"*

    **Shift:** Standard polite. The かどうか is appropriate here. The main verb <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かります carries the politeness.

=== ":material-briefcase-outline: Extra Polite"
    ケン<ruby>様<rt>さま</rt></ruby>がご<ruby>出席<rt>しゅっせき</rt></ruby>されるかどうか、ご<ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>いただけますでしょうか。

    *"Might we ask you to confirm whether Ken will be in attendance or not?"*

    **Shift:** Full keigo. <ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>る → ご<ruby>出席<rt>しゅっせき</rt></ruby>される (respectful); <ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>する → ご<ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>いただけますでしょうか (humble request). The かどうか is retained even in ultra-formal contexts — it is not casual.

## Common Mistakes {#common-mistakes}

### 1. Using かどうか with wh-questions

!!! failure "Ungrammatical"
    <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるかどうか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らない。

    *<ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby> is a wh-word, not a yes/no question. かどうか cannot follow wh-questions. Use <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らない.*

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らない。

    *"I don't know what [they] will eat."* — Wh-embedding with か only.

### 2. Using polite form inside the embedded clause

!!! failure "Ungrammatical"
    <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ますか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。

    *Embedded clauses must use plain form. <ruby>来<rt>き</rt></ruby>ますか is a standalone polite question, not an embeddable clause.*

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。

    *"I don't know if he'll come."* — Plain form inside the embedding; politeness is carried by the main verb.

### 3. Confusing embedded か with sentence-ending かもしれない

!!! failure "Structural error"
    <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るか<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らないかもしれない。

    *This tries to embed <ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>らないかもしれない ("might not know"), which is possible but means something different. If the intent is "I don't know if he might come," the structure is wrong.*

!!! success "Correct (for intended meaning)"
    <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない。

    *"I don't know whether he'll come or not."* — Clear embedding.

| Collocation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 〜か<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない | "Don't know whether…" — the most common embedding pattern |
| 〜かどうか<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>からない | "Don't know whether… or not" — explicit uncertainty |
| 〜か<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>りたい | "Want to know whether/what…" — curiosity embedding |
| 〜か<ruby>聞<rt>き</rt></ruby>く | "Ask whether…" — inquiry embedding |
| 〜か<ruby>確認<rt>かくにん</rt></ruby>する | "Check whether…" — verification embedding |
| 〜か<ruby>決<rt>き</rt></ruby>める | "Decide whether…" — decision embedding |

## Hidden Points {#hidden-points}

- **〜か can embed statements that aren't literally questions.** <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>が<ruby>来<rt>く</rt></ruby>るかどうか is not a direct question — it's a nominalized clause. But it retains the semantic shape of a question: uncertainty, alternatives, information gap.
- **The comma after かどうか is prosodic.** It represents a breath between the uncertainty and the main predicate. In fast speech it may disappear; in careful speech it marks the clause boundary clearly.
- **〜かどうか can be used sarcastically.** <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>くかどうか、<ruby>知<rt>し</rt></ruby>ったことじゃない ("Whether I go or not is none of your business") uses the explicit binary to create distance and finality.
- **Double embedding with lists:** <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>くか<ruby>留<rt>と</rt></ruby>まるか、<ruby>決<rt>き</rt></ruby>めていない ("I haven't decided whether to go or stay") embeds two alternatives with two か markers. This is standard for multiple-choice embedding.
- **〜か can embed exclamations rhetorically.** <ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>て<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>ったか<ruby>忘<rt>わす</rt></ruby>れた ("I forgot what [you] said") embeds a question the speaker once knew. The か marks the information as previously questioned, now lost.
- **The plain-form requirement is absolute.** Even in ultra-formal keigo, the embedded clause remains plain: ご<ruby>出席<rt>しゅっせき</rt></ruby>されるかどうか (not ご<ruby>出席<rt>しゅっせき</rt></ruby>されますかどうか). The politeness is always on the main predicate, never inside the embedding.

## Learner Stage {#learner-stage}

| Stage | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Now — N4** | Produce 〜か and 〜かどうか correctly; distinguish wh-embedding from yes/no embedding; use plain form inside embedded clauses; avoid かどうか with question words |
| **Developing — N4→N3** | Drop particles naturally in casual embedded clauses; choose between か and かどうか based on explicitness needs; use double-form 〜か〜ないか in writing |
| **Automatic — N3→N2** | Embedded questions arrive as single units — you no longer parse them as "question + か." The choice between か and かどうか becomes intuitive. |
| **Mastery** | You deploy the full embedding spectrum: か (compact), かどうか (explicit), か〜ないか (literary), かのように (hypothetical). You know when particle dropping sounds natural and when it sounds sloppy. You never put ます inside an embedded clause. |
