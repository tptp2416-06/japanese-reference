# Unattended Continuation — 〜っぱなし

#### Reference Sentence: 〜っぱなし — "Left Doing" / "Left in the State of Doing"

---

## Quick Summary {#quick-summary}

!!! tip "At a glance"
    - 〜っぱなし attaches to the **masu-stem** of a verb and means "to leave something in an ongoing state" — the action continues (or its result persists) without the attention, intervention, or closure that would normally be expected.
    - It carries a strong nuance of **neglect, forgetfulness, or failure to complete the proper cycle** of an action. Something is on, open, running, or undone when it should have been stopped, closed, or finished.
    - In the reference sentence — テレビが<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしです — the TV has been left on (it is in the "turned-on" state, and someone should have turned it off but didn't).
    - っぱなし is distinct from 〜たまま, which neutrally describes a state persisting, and from 〜ている, which simply states an ongoing action or resultant state without the judgment of neglect.

---

## Core Meaning — "Left in an Inappropriate State" {#core-meaning}

!!! note "Core Rule"
    Use 〜っぱなし when you want to say that an action or its result **continues without being properly closed or attended to** — usually because someone forgot, neglected, or failed to complete the expected follow-up.

| テレビが<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしです。 | The TV has been left on. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: TV turned-on-left-alone is.* |
| | *Someone should have turned it off; the state of being on is continuing unattended.* |

## The Reference Sentence — Full Breakdown {#reference-breakdown}

### The Sentence

> テレビが<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしです。

### Natural Translation
*"The TV has been left on."* / *"The TV is still on (and shouldn't be)."*

### Component Breakdown

| Component | Form | Function |
|-----------|------|----------|
| テレビ | Noun | Television |
| が | Subject particle | Marks the TV as the thing in the state |
| <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>け | Masu-stem of <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>ける (to turn on) | The base to which っぱなし attaches |
| っぱなし | Suffix | "Left in the state of doing" — adds neglect nuance |
| です | Polite copula | Makes the sentence polite |

### Why Not 〜ている or 〜たまま?

| Form | Sentence | Nuance |
|------|----------|--------|
| 〜ている | テレビが<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>いている。 | "The TV is on." (neutral state; no judgment) |
| 〜たまま | テレビが<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けたままだ。 | "The TV is on (and was left that way)." (persistent state; can be neutral or neglectful depending on context) |
| **〜っぱなし** | テレビが<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしだ。 | **"The TV is left on (and someone should have turned it off)."** (strong neglect nuance) |

---

## How 〜っぱなし Works {#how-it-works}

### The Grammar

**Verb (masu-stem) + っぱなし**

Remove ます from the polite form to get the stem, then attach っぱなし directly. っぱなし functions as a noun (it can be followed by だ/です). It comes from the verb <ruby>放<rt>はな</rt></ruby>す (はなす, to let go / to release), with the same root as <ruby>放<rt>はな</rt></ruby>し<ruby>飼<rt>が</rt></ruby>い (free-range) — the idea is that the action has been "released" and left to run.

| Verb | Masu-stem | + っぱなし | Meaning |
|------|-----------|------------|---------|
| <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>ける (to turn on) | <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>け | <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなし | Left on |
| <ruby>開<rt>あ</rt></ruby>ける (to open) | <ruby>開<rt>あ</rt></ruby>け | <ruby>開<rt>あ</rt></ruby>けっぱなし | Left open |
| <ruby>出<rt>だ</rt></ruby>す (to take out) | <ruby>出<rt>だ</rt></ruby>し | <ruby>出<rt>だ</rt></ruby>しっぱなし | Left out (not put away) |
| <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>く (to write) | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>き | <ruby>書<rt>か</rt></ruby>きっぱなし | Left half-written; left as-is without finishing |
| <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>う (to say) | <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>い | <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いっぱなし | Left as just words; said and then abandoned without follow-up |
| <ruby>立<rt>た</rt></ruby>つ (to stand) | <ruby>立<rt>た</rt></ruby>ち | <ruby>立<rt>た</rt></ruby>ちっぱなし | Left standing (without sitting) |
| <ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>る (to sit) | <ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>り | <ruby>座<rt>すわ</rt></ruby>りっぱなし | Left sitting (without moving) |

!!! warning "Only attaches to the masu-stem"
    You cannot attach っぱなし to the dictionary form, te-form, or any other conjugation. Only the masu-stem (the part before ます) is used.

## The Neglect Nuance — The Core of っぱなし {#neglect-nuance}

っぱなし always implies that something **should have been done but wasn't** — the state is not just continuing; it's continuing *inappropriately*. This is what distinguishes it from neutral state descriptions.

| Sentence | Nuance |
|----------|--------|
| <ruby>窓<rt>まど</rt></ruby>が<ruby>開<rt>あ</rt></ruby>いている。 | The window is open. (No judgment — maybe it's open for fresh air.) |
| <ruby>窓<rt>まど</rt></ruby>が<ruby>開<rt>あ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしだ。 | The window has been left open. (Someone should have closed it — it's cold, it's insecure, etc.) |
| <ruby>扇風機<rt>せんぷうき</rt></ruby>が<ruby>回<rt>まわ</rt></ruby>っている。 | The fan is running. (Neutral.) |
| <ruby>扇風機<rt>せんぷうき</rt></ruby>が<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしだ。 | The fan has been left on. (Someone forgot to turn it off; waste of electricity.) |

The speaker using っぱなし is **criticising** the situation, even if mildly. It's not just observation — it's observation with a "this shouldn't be the case" judgment.

---

## 〜っぱなし vs. 〜たまま — A Critical Distinction {#vs-mama}

Both describe a state continuing, but the nuance differs sharply.

| | 〜っぱなし | 〜たまま |
|--|-----------|----------|
| **Nuance** | Neglect, oversight, inappropriateness — the state should have been resolved. | Persistence — the state simply hasn't changed yet; neutral or context-dependent. |
| **Judgment** | Inherently critical or regretful. | Can be neutral, practical, or sometimes critical if context adds it. |
| **Typical use** | Complaints, noticing forgotten things. | Describing posture, position, or unchanged situation. |
| **Example** | <ruby>電気<rt>でんき</rt></ruby>が<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしだ。 "The light has been left on." (Wasteful; someone forgot.) | <ruby>電気<rt>でんき</rt></ruby>が<ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>いたままだ。 "The light is still on." (Just stating; maybe it's intentional.) |
| **Example** | <ruby>靴<rt>くつ</rt></ruby>を<ruby>脱<rt>ぬ</rt></ruby>ぎっぱなしだ。 "The shoes have been left lying around." (Tidy them up!) | <ruby>靴<rt>くつ</rt></ruby>を<ruby>脱<rt>ぬ</rt></ruby>いだままだ。 "The shoes are still off." (Maybe the person is still inside; no criticism implied.) |

!!! tip "Rule of thumb"
    If you're annoyed that something hasn't been put right, use **っぱなし**. If you're simply describing that a state hasn't changed, use **まま**.

## Common Mistakes {#common-mistakes}

### 1. Using っぱなし for neutral or positive continuation

っぱなし is inherently negative — it implies neglect or something left undone. Using it for a neutral or intentional state sounds accusatory or odd.

!!! failure "Incorrect nuance"
    <ruby>音楽<rt>おんがく</rt></ruby>をかけっぱなしで<ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>するのが<ruby>好<rt>す</rt></ruby>きです。

    ~"I like studying with the music left on." Sounds like you're complaining about the music being on, not enjoying it.~

!!! success "More natural"
    <ruby>音楽<rt>おんがく</rt></ruby>をかけたまま<ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>するのが<ruby>好<rt>す</rt></ruby>きです。

    ~"I like studying with the music playing." まま is neutral; no neglect nuance.~

### 2. Attaching っぱなし to the wrong form

っぱなし only attaches to the masu-stem. Using the dictionary form, te-form, or past tense is incorrect.

!!! failure "Incorrect"
    <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けるっぱなし ❌
    <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けったっぱなし ❌

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなし ✅ (masu-stem of <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>ける)

### 3. Confusing っぱなし with 〜たまま for all contexts

Though they overlap, っぱなし adds judgment. Using it when simply describing a persistent state without criticism can make the speaker sound unfairly harsh.

!!! failure "Overly critical"
    <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>若<rt>わか</rt></ruby>いままだ。 → <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>若<rt>わか</rt></ruby>いっぱなしだ ❌

    ~"He remains young." っぱなし with i-adjectives isn't standard; "left young" makes no sense. まま is correct.~

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>彼<rt>かれ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>若<rt>わか</rt></ruby>いままだ。

## Quick Reference Table {#quick-reference}

| Pattern | Meaning | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Masu-stem + っぱなし** | Left doing / left in a state (neglect) | <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなし "left on" |
| **Masu-stem + っぱなし + だ／です** | Polite/Negative statement about the state | <ruby>付<rt>つ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしです |
| **〜っぱなしにする** | To leave something in that state (deliberate or causal) | <ruby>開<rt>あ</rt></ruby>けっぱなしにする "leave it open" |
| **〜っぱなしにしないで** | Don't leave it in that state | <ruby>出<rt>だ</rt></ruby>しっぱなしにしないで "Don't leave it out" |

## See Also {#see-also}

### Similar Patterns

- **〜たまま** — Persistent state, neutral. "The window is still open (as it was)." No neglect nuance.
- **〜ている** — Ongoing action or resultant state. "The TV is on." Pure state description.
- **〜ずにいる** — "To remain without doing." Negative action remaining undone, but without the "left" judgment.
- **〜<ruby>放題<rt>ほうだい</rt></ruby>（ほうだい）** — "All you can / as much as you like." Different nuance — freedom, not neglect. <ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べ<ruby>放題<rt>ほうだい</rt></ruby>, <ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>いたい<ruby>放題<rt>ほうだい</rt></ruby>.

### Contrast With

- **〜っぱなし** vs. **〜たまま** — Neglect vs. neutral persistence. See the comparison section above.
