# Change of State — 〜ように・〜ようになる

## From inability to ability, from unknown to known

---

## JLPT Level {#jlpt-level}

!!! abstract "JLPT Level — Upper N4 / Lower N3"
    The base pattern 〜ようにする is N4 — "try to do" or "make it so that." But the extended usage 〜ようになる for change-over-time, especially the transformative shift from inability to ability, is a lower-N3 pragmatic layer that textbooks under-teach. Native speakers use ようになる constantly; learners often miss the "gradual emergence" nuance.

    | Tier | Pattern | Description | Example |
    |------|---------|-------------|---------|
    | N5 — Foundation | 〜できる | Direct potential — innate or acquired ability | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げる。*"I can swim."* |
    | N4 — Foundation | 〜ようにする | Volitional effort — "try to make it so that" | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐようにする。*"I'll try to swim every day."* |
    | **N4 — Core ← You are here** | **〜ようになる** | **Change-over-time — gradual emergence of ability or state** | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになった。*"I became able to swim."* |
    | N3 — Extended | 〜ようにしている | Sustained effort — maintaining a practice | <ruby>毎朝<rt>まいあさ</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>はし</rt></ruby>るようにしている。*"I make it a point to run every morning."* |
    | N3 — Extended | 〜ようにしてくれる | Request for accommodation — "please do it so that…" | <ruby>静<rt>しず</rt></ruby>かにするようにしてくれる？ |
    | N2 — Awareness | 〜ようになっている | Designed state — "is set up to…" | <ruby>自動<rt>じどう</rt></ruby>で<ruby>止<rt>と</rt></ruby>まるようになっている。 |

---

## Quick Summary {#quick-summary}

!!! tip "At a glance"
    - 〜ようになる describes a **change-over-time** — a shift from one state to another, especially from inability to ability, from unawareness to awareness, or from non-habit to habit.
    - Unlike 〜できる which states current ability flatly, 〜ようになる narrates the **emergence** of that ability. It tells a story: there was a time I couldn't, and now I can.
    - The よう nominalizes the preceding clause; the なる adds the change. Together: "It has come to be that [clause]."
    - This pattern is the grammar of **growth narratives** — how Japanese speakers talk about learning, maturing, adapting, and transforming.

---

## Core Meaning {#core-meaning}

!!! note "Core Rule"
    Use 〜ようになる when you want to express that a state has changed over time — especially that someone has gained ability, awareness, or a new pattern. The change is typically gradual, earned, or observed over a period.

| <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げる。 | I can swim [current ability, flat statement]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: Swim-can.* |
| | *Context: Answering "Can you swim?" Neutral, present-tense capability.* |

| <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになった。 | I've become able to swim [change over time, narrative of growth]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: Swim-can way became.* |
| | *Context: Telling someone about your progress. The ようになった adds the story: there was a before (couldn't swim) and an after (can swim now).* |

| <ruby>日本語<rt>にほんご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになりたい。 | I want to become able to speak Japanese [aspirational change]. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: Japanese speak-can way become-want.* |
| | *Context: Expressing a goal. The ようになりたい frames the desire as a transformation, not just a skill acquisition.* |

## The Two Patterns: 〜ようにする vs. 〜ようになる {#the-two-patterns}

These two patterns are often taught together but serve fundamentally different functions. Confusing them is one of the most persistent N4 errors.

| Pattern | Structure | Core Meaning | Subject | Example |
|---------|-----------|--------------|---------|---------|
| 〜ようにする | [plain/potential] + ようにする | Volitional effort — "I will make it so that…" | The person making effort | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>するようにする。*"I'll try to study every day."* |
| 〜ようになる | [plain/potential] + ようになる | Observed change — "It has come to be that…" | The situation or person changing | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>勉強<rt>べんきょう</rt></ruby>するようになった。*"I've come to study every day [it became a habit]."* |

!!! warning "The agency distinction"
    〜ようにする requires human agency — someone is trying to make something happen. 〜ようになる describes change that may or may not involve effort — the focus is on the result, not the will.

| <ruby>早<rt>はや</rt></ruby>く<ruby>起<rt>お</rt></ruby>きるようにしている。 | I'm trying to wake up early [making an effort]. |
|---|---|
| | *The subject is actively working at this. It may or may not be happening yet.* |

| <ruby>早<rt>はや</rt></ruby>く<ruby>起<rt>お</rt></ruby>きるようになった。 | I've come to wake up early [change observed]. |
|---|---|
| | *The change has happened. Whether through effort or naturally, the result is now real.* |

## How 〜ようになる Works {#how-it-works}

### The Nominalization Layer (よう)

The よう in this pattern is a nominalizer — it turns the preceding clause into a noun-like concept. Unlike こと (which nominalizes actions as abstract entities) or の (which nominalizes more loosely), よう specifically nominalizes **manner, way, or state**.

When attached to a potential form (<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるよう), it nominalizes the state of being able to swim. When attached to a plain verb (<ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かるよう), it nominalizes the state of understanding.

### The Change Layer (なる)

なる is the intransitive verb of change — "to become." It describes natural, observed, or gradual transformation. This is crucial: 〜ようになる is never sudden or forced. It is the grammar of **emergence**.

| <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かる。 | I understand [right now]. |
|---|---|
| | *Static state. No narrative.* |

| <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かるようになった。 | I've come to understand [over time]. |
|---|---|
| | *Narrative of emergence. The understanding developed gradually.* |

### What Precedes ように

The clause before ように can be:

| Form | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| Potential verb | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるように | "In a way that can swim" → "able to swim" |
| Plain verb | <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かるように | "In a way that understands" → "understanding" |
| Negative verb | <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かないように | "In a way that doesn't go" → "not going" |
| Adjective + く | <ruby>早<rt>はや</rt></ruby>くなるように | "In a way that becomes early/fast" |

!!! note "The potential form preference"
    〜ようになる most commonly attaches to potential verbs (<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げる, <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せる, <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>める) because the pattern's core meaning is **ability emerging**. But it also attaches to plain verbs for habit formation (<ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>走<rt>はし</rt></ruby>るようになった — "came to run every day").

## In Real Life {#in-real-life}

!!! example "Growth narrative"
    **Situation:** ユイ is catching up with a friend she hasn't seen in a year. Last year, she was struggling with Japanese; now she's conversational.

    > <ruby>去年<rt>きょねん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>全然<rt>ぜんぜん</rt></ruby><ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せなかったけど、<ruby>今<rt>いま</rt></ruby>は<ruby>日常会話<rt>にちじょうかいわ</rt></ruby>くらい<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになったよ。

    *"Last year I couldn't speak at all, but now I've become able to handle daily conversation."*

    **Why ようになった fits here:** The sentence explicitly contrasts past inability (<ruby>全然<rt>ぜんぜん</rt></ruby><ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せなかった) with present ability. The ようになった is the bridge — it narrates the transformation. Without it, <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるよ would be a flat statement of current ability; with it, the ability is framed as earned growth.

    **What the よ adds:** The sentence-final よ marks this as shareable news — something the speaker is pleased to report. Combined with ようになった, it creates a small celebration of progress.

!!! example "Habit formation"
    **Situation:** ケンタ is explaining to his doctor how his lifestyle has changed.

    > <ruby>医者<rt>いしゃ</rt></ruby>さんに<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>われたから、<ruby>野菜<rt>やさい</rt></ruby>を<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べるようになったんです。

    *"Because the doctor told me to, I've come to eat vegetables."*

    **Why ようになった fits here:** The vegetables are now part of Kenta's diet — a change from before. The ようになった captures that this wasn't always true. The んです adds explanatory grounding — he's answering an implied "Why?" or "How come?"

!!! example "Natural change"
    **Situation:** ハルコ is talking about her aging father.

    > <ruby>父<rt>ちち</rt></ruby>は<ruby>最近<rt>さいきん</rt></ruby>、<ruby>夜<rt>よる</rt></ruby><ruby>中<rt>なか</rt></ruby>に<ruby>目<rt>め</rt></ruby>が<ruby>覚<rt>さ</rt></ruby>めるようになった。

    *"My father has recently come to wake up in the middle of the night."*

    **Why ようになった fits here:** The waking is new, observed, and not deliberately caused. The ようになる pattern accommodates natural, even unwanted, changes — not just earned abilities.

## Grammar Analysis {#grammar-analysis}

### Function in Context

- <ruby>去年<rt>きょねん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>全然<rt>ぜんぜん</rt></ruby><ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せなかった establishes the "before" state — complete inability. The けど (but) sets up the contrast.
- <ruby>今<rt>いま</rt></ruby>は marks the "after" state — present time frame.
- <ruby>日常<rt>にちじょう</rt></ruby><ruby>会話<rt>かいわ</rt></ruby>くらい scopes the ability — "daily conversation level." The くらい softens the claim, making it modest.
- <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになった is the core transformation. <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せる (potential) + ように (nominalization) + なった (past change).
- よ closes the sentence as shareable news. The speaker is not just stating ability; they're reporting growth.

| <ruby>子供<rt>こども</rt></ruby>の<ruby>頃<rt>ころ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>苦手<rt>にがて</rt></ruby>だったけど、<ruby>今<rt>いま</rt></ruby>は<ruby>納豆<rt>なっとう</rt></ruby>が<ruby>食<rt>た</rt></ruby>べられるようになった。 | I used to be bad with it as a kid, but now I've become able to eat natto. |
|---|---|
| | *Literal: Child time disliked-was but, now natto eat-can way became.* |
| | *Context: The classic Japanese "acquired taste" narrative. The ようになった marks the palate's maturation.* |

### Construction {#construction}

| Preceding Form | Structure | Example |
|----------------|-----------|---------|
| Potential verb (できる) | [potential] + ようになる | <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになる |
| Plain verb | [plain] + ようになる | <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かるようになる |
| Negative plain | [nai-form] + ようになる | <ruby>行<rt>い</rt></ruby>かないようになる |
| Adjective (i) | [stem] + く + ようになる | <ruby>強<rt>つよ</rt></ruby>くなるようになる |
| Adjective (na) | [stem] + に + ようになる | <ruby>静<rt>しず</rt></ruby>かになるようになる |
| Noun + の | [noun] + のようになる | <ruby>大人<rt>おとな</rt></ruby>のようになる |

!!! warning "The なる vs. する distinction"
    〜ようになる (intransitive) describes natural or observed change. 〜ようにする (transitive) describes deliberate effort. Do not confuse them.

| Intransitive (observed) | Transitive (deliberate) |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| <ruby>早<rt>はや</rt></ruby>く<ruby>起<rt>お</rt></ruby>きるようになった | <ruby>早<rt>はや</rt></ruby>く<ruby>起<rt>お</rt></ruby>きるようにした |
| "I've come to wake up early" | "I made an effort to wake up early" |
| Change observed, result-focused | Effort made, process-focused |

## The Full Spectrum of よう {#the-full-spectrum-of-よう}

The よう particle serves multiple functions across Japanese grammar. Understanding how 〜ように/〜ようになる fits into this spectrum prevents confusion.

| Pattern | Function | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| 〜ようにする | Volitional effort | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>運動<rt>うんどう</rt></ruby>するようにする。*"I'll try to exercise every day."* |
| 〜ようにしている | Sustained effort | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>運動<rt>うんどう</rt></ruby>するようにしている。*"I make it a point to exercise every day."* |
| 〜ようになる | Change over time | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>運動<rt>うんどう</rt></ruby>するようになった。*"I've come to exercise every day."* |
| 〜ようにしてくれる | Request for accommodation | <ruby>静<rt>しず</rt></ruby>かにするようにしてくれる？*"Can you try to be quiet?"* |
| 〜ように<ruby>言<rt>ゆ</rt></ruby>う | Indirect instruction | <ruby>早<rt>はや</rt></ruby>く<ruby>来<rt>こ</rt></ruby>いように<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>った。*"I told him to come early."* |
| 〜ように<ruby>見<rt>み</rt></ruby>える | Appearance | <ruby>分<rt>わ</rt></ruby>かるように<ruby>見<rt>み</rt></ruby>える。*"It looks like [you] understand."* |
| 〜ようになっている | Designed state | <ruby>自動<rt>じどう</rt></ruby>で<ruby>止<rt>と</rt></ruby>まるようになっている。*"It's set up to stop automatically."* |

## Japanese Mindset {#japanese-mindset}

English talks about ability with can/could — static, present-oriented. Japanese talks about ability with 〜ようになる — narrative, change-oriented. This reflects a broader cultural preference for **process over state**, **emergence over possession**.

When a Japanese speaker says <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになった, they are not just saying "I can swim." They are saying "There was a process, and now swimming is part of me." The grammar demands a story.

- **Growth as narrative.** 〜ようになる requires a before and after. Even when unstated, the listener supplies it. The pattern activates a temporal frame automatically.
- **Effort as invisible.** Unlike 〜ようにする which foregrounds effort, 〜ようになる backgrounds it. The change simply emerges. This is humbler — the speaker doesn't claim credit for their growth, they merely observe it.
- **Naturalness as virtue.** Changes described with 〜ようになる often feel inevitable, organic, even welcomed. The pattern carries a subtle positive valence — things coming into their proper state.

## Nuance Comparison {#nuance-comparison}

| Grammar Point | Core Meaning | Emotional / Pragmatic Nuance | Trigger Context | JLPT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 〜ようになる | Change over time — ability/state emerges | Narrative of growth; natural emergence; humble observation | Describing acquired abilities, habits, or natural changes | N4 |
| 〜ようにする | Volitional effort — "try to make it so" | Active willpower; self-discipline; resolution | Setting intentions; making plans; self-improvement goals | N4 |
| 〜ようにしている | Sustained effort — maintaining practice | Ongoing discipline; established routine | Describing current maintained habits | N3 |
| 〜できる | Direct potential — current ability | Flat statement; no narrative | Answering ability questions; neutral capability | N5 |
| 〜られる (passive/potential) | Potential (formal) or passive | More formal than できる; can be ambiguous | Written Japanese; formal speech | N4 |
| 〜<ruby>始<rt>はじ</rt></ruby>める | Begin to do | Active start; deliberate initiation | Starting new actions | N4 |
| 〜<ruby>出<rt>だ</rt></ruby>す | Start to do (sudden) | Sudden emergence; often emotional or physical | Sudden changes in state | N4 |
| 〜てくる | Change approaching speaker | Gradual approach; "has come to be" | Changes noticed over time, approaching present | N4 |

The closest lookalike is 〜<ruby>始<rt>はじ</rt></ruby>める. Both describe change over time, but 〜<ruby>始<rt>はじ</rt></ruby>める emphasizes the starting point ("I started swimming"), while 〜ようになる emphasizes the resulting state ("I've become able to swim"). 〜<ruby>始<rt>はじ</rt></ruby>める is about action; 〜ようになる is about capacity.

## Alternatives {#alternatives}

### 〜できる (direct potential)

The flat statement of ability. No narrative, no change.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>日本語<rt>にほんご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せる。 |
| **English** | "I can speak Japanese." |
| **Intent** | Neutral capability statement |
| **vs. ようになる** | Removes the growth story. Appropriate when the "before" state is irrelevant or unknown. |

!!! tip "When to use this instead"
    Use できる when you simply want to state current ability without framing it as achievement. Use ようになる when the journey matters.

### 〜ようにする (deliberate effort)

The active counterpart. Emphasizes willpower over result.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>日本語<rt>にほんご</rt></ruby>を<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>すようにする。 |
| **English** | "I'll try to speak Japanese every day." |
| **Intent** | Setting an intention; making a plan |
| **vs. ようになる** | ようにする is about effort; ようになる is about result. They often appear in sequence: first you ようにする, then you ようになる. |

### 〜てくる (change approaching)

Describes change noticed over time, approaching the present.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Example** | <ruby>最近<rt>さいきん</rt></ruby>、<ruby>日本語<rt>にほんご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せてくる。 |
| **English** | "Recently, I've been becoming able to speak Japanese." |
| **Intent** | Change approaching the speaker; ongoing emergence |
| **vs. ようになる** | 〜てくる emphasizes the approach toward the speaker (temporal proximity); 〜ようになる emphasizes the state change itself. Both can describe the same phenomenon with slightly different framing. |

## Register and Formality {#register-and-formality}

The change-of-state meaning is preserved across registers, but the packaging shifts.

=== ":material-chat-outline: Casual"
    <ruby>去年<rt>きょねん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>全然<rt>ぜんぜん</rt></ruby><ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せなかったけど、<ruby>今<rt>いま</rt></ruby>は<ruby>日常会話<rt>にちじょうかいわ</rt></ruby>くらい<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになったよ。

    *"Last year I couldn't speak at all, but now I've become able to handle daily conversation."*

    **Shift:** Input example. Casual, narrative, celebratory. The よ marks shareable news.

=== ":material-tie: Basic Polite"
    <ruby>去年<rt>きょねん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>全然<rt>ぜんぜん</rt></ruby><ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せませんでしたが、<ruby>今<rt>いま</rt></ruby>は<ruby>日常会話<rt>にちじょうかいわ</rt></ruby>くらい<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになりました。

    *"Last year I could not speak at all, but now I have become able to handle daily conversation."*

    **Shift:** Past negative <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せなかった → <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せませんでした; なった → なりました. The narrative structure is identical; the politeness is elevated.

=== ":material-briefcase-outline: Extra Polite"
    <ruby>昨年<rt>さくねん</rt></ruby>は<ruby>全く<rt>まったく</rt></ruby><ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>すことができませんでしたが、<ruby>現在<rt>げんざい</rt></ruby>は<ruby>日常会話<rt>にちじょうかいわ</rt></ruby>レベルまで<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになっております。

    *"Last year I was entirely unable to speak, but at present I have come to be able to speak at a daily conversation level."*

    **Shift:** <ruby>去年<rt>きょねん</rt></ruby> → <ruby>昨年<rt>さくねん</rt></ruby>; <ruby>全然<rt>ぜんぜん</rt></ruby> → <ruby>全<rt>まった</rt></ruby>く; <ruby>今<rt>いま</rt></ruby> → <ruby>現在<rt>げんざい</rt></ruby>; なった → なっております (humble progressive). The core pattern 〜ようになる is preserved even in ultra-formal register.

| | *Literal: Every day practiced because, kanji read-can way became.* |

~Context: Explicit effort → result. The から makes the causal chain explicit, but the ようになった still frames the result as gradual emergence.~

|---|---|
| | *Literal: Japan came-after-from, sashimi eat-can way became.* |

~Context: Environmental influence. The change is attributed to context (being in Japan), not personal effort. The ようになる is neutral about cause.~

| | *Literal: Forcibly early wake-up way doing-was, naturally early wake-up way became.* |

## Common Mistakes {#common-mistakes}

### 1. Confusing 〜ようにする and 〜ようになる

The most common N4 error. Learners produce <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐようにした when they mean <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになった, or vice versa.

!!! failure "Wrong pattern"
    <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>いで、<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようにした。

    *"I swam every day and tried to make it so I could swim."* — Bizarre. The effort and result are conflated.

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>毎日<rt>まいにち</rt></ruby><ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>ぐようにしてたら、<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになった。

    *"I tried to swim every day, and then I became able to swim."* — Effort (ようにする) leads to result (ようになる).

### 2. Using 〜ようになる for sudden changes

!!! failure "Pragmatic mismatch"
    <ruby>事故<rt>じこ</rt></ruby>で<ruby>足<rt>あし</rt></ruby>が<ruby>動<rt>うご</rt></ruby>かないようになった。

    *"Due to an accident, my legs became unable to move."* — Grammatically possible, but <ruby>動<rt>うご</rt></ruby>かなくなった is more natural for sudden traumatic change. 〜ようになる prefers gradual emergence.

!!! success "Better"
    <ruby>事故<rt>じこ</rt></ruby>で<ruby>足<rt>あし</rt></ruby>が<ruby>動<rt>うご</rt></ruby>かなくなった。

    *"My legs stopped moving due to an accident."* — Direct state change, more appropriate for sudden events.

### 3. Overusing 〜ようになる for current ability

!!! failure "Narrative overkill"
    <ruby>私<rt>わたし</rt></ruby>は<ruby>英語<rt>えいご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになっている。

    *"I have become able to speak English [and remain so]."* — Grammatically odd. 〜ようになっている is for designed states, not personal abilities. Use <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せる or <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せます.

!!! success "Correct"
    <ruby>私<rt>わたし</rt></ruby>は<ruby>英語<rt>えいご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せます。

    *"I can speak English."* — Simple, appropriate.

## Hidden Points {#hidden-points}

- **〜ようになる can describe loss of ability.** <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げなくなった ("became unable to swim") uses the negative potential + なる. The pattern accommodates decline as well as growth — though the valence is usually negative when describing loss.
- **The "before" state is often ellipsed.** Japanese speakers frequently say <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになった without explicitly stating <ruby>前<rt>まえ</rt></ruby>は<ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げなかった. The pattern itself activates the "before" frame — listeners automatically supply it.
- **〜ようになる + たい is aspirational grammar.** なりたい ("want to become") + ようになる creates the goal-state construction: <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せるようになりたい. This is more humble and process-oriented than <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>したい ("want to speak").
- **Simile よう and nominalization よう are related but distinct.** <ruby>大人<rt>おとな</rt></ruby>のようになる ("become like an adult") uses simile よう; <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げるようになる uses nominalization よう. The grammar looks identical but the semantics differ. Context disambiguates.
- **〜ようになっている is a hidden N2 gem.** It describes designed or expected states: <ruby>自動<rt>じどう</rt></ruby>で<ruby>止<rt>と</rt></ruby>まるようになっている ("It's set up to stop automatically"). The ている adds "is currently in the state of being designed to…" This is common in technical descriptions and instructions.

## Learner Stage {#learner-stage}

| Stage | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Now — N4** | Produce 〜ようになる correctly with potential verbs; distinguish from 〜ようにする; narrate simple ability acquisition |
| **Developing — N4→N3** | Deploy naturally in growth narratives; use with plain verbs for habit formation; recognize 〜ようにしている as sustained effort counterpart |
| **Automatic — N3→N2** | The pattern becomes a single conceptual unit — "emergence." You hear ようになった and immediately register a before/after frame without conscious parsing. |
| **Mastery** | You control the full よう spectrum: ようにする (effort), ようになる (emergence), ようにしている (sustained practice), ようにしてくれる (request), ようになっている (designed state). You know when できる is flat and when ようになる is narrative. You also recognize simile よう vs. nominalization よう instantly. |

## See Also {#see-also}

### Same Family

- 〜ようにする — Volitional effort; "try to make it so"
- 〜ようにしている — Sustained effort; maintaining a practice
- 〜ようになっている — Designed state; "is set up to"

### Commonly Confused With

- 〜ようにする — Deliberate effort vs. observed change
- 〜できる — Static potential vs. narrative emergence
- 〜<ruby>始<rt>はじ</rt></ruby>める — Active beginning vs. gradual emergence
- 〜てくる — Change approaching speaker vs. state change
- 〜になる — Simple becoming vs. manner/state becoming

### Builds From

- 〜なる (N5) — Intransitive change
- 〜ように (N4) — Manner/nominalization
- Potential forms (N4) — <ruby>泳<rt>およ</rt></ruby>げる, <ruby>話<rt>はな</rt></ruby>せる, <ruby>読<rt>よ</rt></ruby>める
- Plain verb forms (N5) — For habit formation usage

### Unlocks Next

- 〜ようにしてくれる (N3) — Requesting accommodation
- 〜ように<ruby>言<rt>い</rt></ruby>う (N3) — Indirect reported commands
- 〜ようになっている (N2) — Designed/expected states
- 〜ようとする (N3) — Attemptive; "try to [do]"
- 〜ようとしない (N3) — Refusal; "not try to [do]"
