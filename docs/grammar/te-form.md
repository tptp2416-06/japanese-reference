# <ruby>て<rt></rt></ruby>-form

The te-form is one of the most productive forms in Japanese — it connects clauses, makes requests, signals permission and prohibition, and stacks with dozens of auxiliaries.

  <h2>【Translation】</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><span class="nat-eng">Natural English:</span> Thanks for waiting.</li>
    <li><span class="label">Literal English:</span> Having-waited for-me, thank you.</li>
    <li><span class="label">The Gap:</span>
      <p class="ind">• English "thanks for waiting" is neutral. Japanese <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ってくれて encodes that the wait was done <em>as a favor to me</em> — the speaker is acknowledging a social debt.</p>
      <p class="ind">• The te-form chain (って + くれて) packages two grammatical moves — punctual completion + benefactive — into one smooth unit that English collapses into a single word.</p>
    </li>
  </ul>

  <hr>

  <h2>【Grammar Stacking】</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>
      <div class="chain-title">Chain 1 · Benefactive Gratitude Stack: <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ってくれてありがとう</div>

      <div class="chain-layer">① <span class="chain-term"><ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>つ</span> ➝ Base verb "to wait." <span class="tag">⟦Godan · intransitive⟧</span>
        <p class="ind">• The actor doing the waiting is the listener, not the speaker.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="chain-layer">② <span class="chain-term"><ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>って</span> ➝ Te-form connector. <span class="tag">⟦te-form⟧</span>
        <p class="ind">• Links the waiting action to the next element — here, the benefactive auxiliary.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="chain-layer">③ <span class="chain-term"><ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>ってくれて</span> ➝ Benefactive: the listener did the waiting <em>for the speaker.</em> <span class="tag">⟦te-form + くれる benefactive⟧</span>
        <p class="ind">• くれる marks an inward favor — something done for me or my in-group.</p>
        <p class="ind">• Without くれて, ありがとう would still work but loses the explicit acknowledgment of social debt.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="chain-layer">④ <span class="chain-term">ありがとう</span> ➝ Gratitude expression. <span class="tag">⟦set phrase⟧</span>
        <p class="ind">• The whole te-form chain before it becomes the reason for gratitude.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="chain-pin">📌 Compare: <ruby>待<rt>ま</rt></ruby>っててくれてありがとう — adding ている (→ てて) shifts from punctual to progressive: "thanks for having kept waiting." Acknowledges sustained, accumulated waiting rather than a single wait.</div>

      <div class="assembly-label">Full Assembly</div>
      <div class="assembly-line"><b>Logical order:</b> [Listener waited (for me)] → [thank you for that]</div>

      <div class="assembly-line"><b>Literal:</b> Having-waited-for-me, thank you.</div>
