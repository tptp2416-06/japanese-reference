    N5-Foundation additive mo particles reviewed

    The Particle も — Also, Even, and Everything In Between¶

    From "me too" to "not even once"¶

    JLPT Level¶

    JLPT Level — N5 Foundation through N3 Extended

    The additive も is N5 — one of the first particles learners encounter. But the scalar, emphatic, conditional, and negative-polarity uses spread across N4 and N3, forming a dense web that native speakers navigate effortlessly and learners often underuse.

    Tier Pattern Description Example
    N5 — Foundation Noun + も Additive — "also / too" 私わたしも行いく。
    N5 — Foundation AもBも Listing — "both A and B" 猫ねこも犬いぬも好すき。
    N4 — Foundation Quantity + も Scalar — "as much as / a whole / not even" 三さん時間じかんも待まった。
    N4 — Foundation Wh-word + も + negative Negative polarity — "nothing / nobody / nowhere" 何なんも食たべない。
    N4 — Core ← You are here 〜ても / 〜でも Conditional — "even if / even though" 高たかくても買かう。
    N3 — Extended Noun + でも Extreme — "even [noun]" 子供こどもでもできる。
    N3 — Extended 〜もしない / 〜もある Minimal / maximal — "not even / there is also" 一いち度ども行いかない。
    N2 — Awareness 〜も〜ば〜も Correlative — "both… and… / neither… nor…" 行いくも行いかないも自由じゆうだ。

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • も is a multipurpose particle that replaces は, が, or を on a noun to add it to a set. 私わたしは行いく → 私わたしも行いく ("I too will go").
    • Beyond simple addition, も scales: attached to quantities it means "as much as / a whole" (三さん時間じかんも); attached to minimal quantities with negative verbs it means "not even" (千せん円えんもない).
    • With wh-words (何なん, 誰だれ, どこ) and negative verbs, も creates universal negatives: 何なんも食たべない = "I eat nothing."
    • With the te-form (〜ても / 〜でも), も creates conditional extremes: "even if / even though."
    • With bare nouns in extreme contexts, も means "even": 子供こどもでもできる = "Even a child can do it."
    • The core logic unifying all uses: も marks an item as at the edge of a set — either adding to the set (additive), pushing the boundary of the set (scalar), or exhausting the set entirely (universal / negative polarity).

    Core Meaning¶

    Core Rule

    Use も when you want to mark an item as belonging to a set in a way that emphasizes its inclusion, its extremity, or its exhaustiveness. The particle absorbs and replaces whatever case particle (は, が, を) would normally mark that item.

    私わたしも行いく。 I will go too [additive — I am added to the set of goers].
    も replaces が or は. The speaker joins an existing group.
    三さん時間じかんも待まった。 I waited a whole three hours [scalar — the quantity is emphasized as large].
    も attaches to the quantity. The three hours are marked as notable, burdensome, or impressive.
    何なんも食たべない。 I eat nothing [negative polarity — the wh-word + も exhausts the set of possible objects].
    も attaches to 何なん in a negative context, creating universal negation.
    高たかくても買かう。 Even if it's expensive, I'll buy it [conditional extreme].
    も attaches to the te-form. The condition (expensive) is marked as extreme but insufficient to prevent the action.
    子供こどもでもできる。 Even a child can do it [extreme — the noun is at the low end of capability].
    も attaches to a noun directly (or to the copula contraction でも). The child represents the minimal expected capability.

    The Six Functions of も¶

    1. Additive も — "Also / Too / As Well"¶

    The foundational use. も replaces は, が, or を on a noun to include it in a set already under discussion.

    Pattern Example Meaning
    Noun + も 私わたしも。 Me too.
    Noun + も + predicate 彼かれも来きた。 He came too.
    Noun + も + particle + predicate 東京とうきょうにも行いった。 I went to Tokyo too.
    Noun + も + noun + も 猫ねこも犬いぬも好すき。 I like both cats and dogs.

    も absorbs particles

    You cannot double-mark. 私わたしはも and 私わたしもは are both ungrammatical. も replaces the topic/subject/object marker entirely. For other particles (に, で, から, etc.), も attaches after them: 東京とうきょうにも, 学校がっこうでも.

    彼かれにも言いった。 I told him too [に + も].
    The に marks the recipient; the も adds him to the set of people told.
    昨日きのうも雨あめだった。 It rained yesterday too [temporal + も].
    も attaches to time expressions to add them to a temporal set.

    2. Scalar も — "As Much As / A Whole / Not Even"¶

    When も attaches to quantities, it pushes them to the edge of expectation. The direction (large or small) depends on context and polarity.

    Large / Surprising: | 千せん円えんもした。 | It cost as much as 1000 yen [surprisingly expensive]. | |---|---| | 三さん時間じかんも待まった。 | I waited a whole three hours [surprisingly long]. | | 十じゅう人にんも来きた。 | As many as ten people came [surprisingly many]. |

    Small / Dismissive (with negative or downplaying predicates): | 千せん円えんもない。 | I don't even have 1000 yen [dismally little]. | |---|---| | 三さん時間じかんも寝ねていない。 | I haven't even slept three hours [insufficient]. | | 一人ひとりもいなかった。 | There wasn't even one person [universal negative]. |

    The scalar flip

    The same quantity + も can mean "as much as" (positive, surprising) or "not even" (negative, dismissive). Context and the main predicate determine which reading applies.

    3. Wh-word + も — Universal and Negative Polarity¶

    When も attaches to question words (wh-words), it creates universal or exhaustive reference. With negative predicates, this becomes universal negation.

    Wh-word + Negative Meaning + Affirmative Meaning
    何なんも 何なんも食たべない eat nothing (rare/non-standard) —
    誰だれも 誰だれも来こなかった nobody came (rare) —
    どこも どこも行いかない go nowhere どこも素敵すてきだ everywhere is lovely
    いつも (rare with neg) — いつも忙いそがしい always busy
    どれも どれも好すきじゃない none of them liked どれも美味おいしい all of them delicious
    いくらも いくらもない not much at all いくらもある there is plenty

    いつも is lexicalized

    いつも means "always" and is almost always affirmative. It does not follow the negative polarity pattern. For "never," use 一いち度ども〜ない or 全然ぜんぜん〜ない.

    何なんも with affirmative verbs is non-standard

    何なんも食たべた is ungrammatical in standard Japanese. For "I ate something," use 何なんか食たべた. For "I ate everything," use 全部ぜんぶ食たべた or 何なんでも食たべた.

    4. Conditional ても / でも — "Even If / Even Though"¶

    も attaches to the te-form of verbs, i-adjectives, and the de-form of nouns/na-adjectives to create extreme conditions.

    Type Form Example Meaning
    Verb (te-form) 〜ても 行いっても無駄むだだ Even if [you] go, it's useless
    i-Adjective 〜くても 高たかくても買かう Even if expensive, [I] buy
    na-Adjective 〜でも 静しずかでも聞きこえる Even if quiet, [I] can hear
    Noun 〜でも 雨あめでも出でかける Even if rain / even rain, [I] go out
    Negative verb 〜なくても 行いかなくてもいい Even if [you] don't go, it's fine

    でも has three faces

    1. Conditional even: 雨あめでも出でかける (even if rain)
    2. Extreme even: 子供こどもでもできる (even a child)
    3. Sentence-initial but: でも、高たかいよ (but, it's expensive) — this is a different word entirely, a contraction of それでも.

    5. Extreme も — "Even [Noun]"¶

    When も attaches directly to nouns (or noun+copula) without a conditional te/de, it marks the noun as an extreme or unexpected member of a category.

    子供こどもも知しっている。 Even children know [surprisingly basic].
    彼女かのじょも来くるって。 Even she's coming [surprisingly, she too].
    百ひゃく円えんもあれば足たりる。 Even 100 yen would be enough [surprisingly little].

    Extreme も vs. Additive も

    The line is blurry. 彼女かのじょも来くる can mean "She's coming too" (additive) or "Even she's coming" (surprising extreme). Intonation and context disambiguate. Falling intonation = additive; rising or emphatic intonation = extreme.

    6. Minimal / Maximal も — 〜もしない / 〜もある¶

    も marks the edge of verbal action — either the absence of even minimal action, or the presence of additional action.

    一いち度ども行いったことがない。 I've never gone even once [minimal].
    驚おどろくこともある。 There are also surprising things [maximal/additive].
    何なんもしない。 Do nothing [universal negative].
    笑わらうこともできない。 Can't even laugh [extreme inability].

    In Real Life¶

    In Real Life

    Situation: ユイ and ケン are planning a weekend outing. They've been discussing restaurants.

    > ユイ：寿司すしも食たべたいな。 > ケン：私わたしも。でも、高たかいよ。 > ユイ：千せん円えんもしないよ。ランチなら。 > ケン：子供こどもでも行いける？ > ユイ：誰だれでも行いける。何なんも心配しんぱいしないで。

    Yui: "I want to eat sushi too." Ken: "Me too. But it's expensive." Yui: "It won't even cost 1000 yen. If it's lunch." Ken: "Can even children go?" Yui: "Anyone can go. Don't worry about anything."

    The も density: This short exchange contains five distinct も functions: 1. 寿司すしも — additive (sushi too, in addition to other foods) 2. 私わたしも — additive (me too) 3. 千せん円えんもしない — scalar negative (not even 1000 yen) 4. 子供こどもでも — extreme conditional (even if [you're] a child / even a child) 5. 誰だれでも — extreme universal (anyone, no matter who) 6. 何なんも — negative polarity (nothing)

    What makes this natural: Native speakers switch between も functions unconsciously. The particle is a Swiss Army knife that scales from simple addition to universal quantification. The density in this exchange is completely normal.

    Grammar Analysis¶

    The Particle Replacement Rule¶

    も does not stack with は, が, or を. It replaces them. For other particles, it stacks after them.

    Normal Particle + も Example
    は (topic) も (replaces) 私わたしは → 私わたしも
    が (subject) も (replaces) 彼かれが → 彼かれも
    を (object) も (replaces) 本ほんを → 本ほんも
    に (direction) にも 東京とうきょうに → 東京とうきょうにも
    で (location/means) でも 学校がっこうで → 学校がっこうでも
    から (origin) からも 日本にっぽんから → 日本にっぽんからも
    へ (direction) へも 家いえへ → 家いえへも
    と (with) とも 友達ともだちと → 友達ともだちとも
    まで (until) までも 夜よるまで → 夜よるまでも

    でも is overloaded

    学校がっこうでも can mean: 1. "At school too" (で + も, additive) 2. "Even at school" (で + も, extreme) 3. "Even if at school" (conditional, though rare with concrete nouns)

    Context resolves the ambiguity.

    Scalar も with Numbers¶

    The scalar function has precise pragmatic rules:

    Pattern Reading Example
    Number + counter + も + affirmative "As many as / a whole" 三さん時間じかんも待まった
    Number + counter + も + negative "Not even" 三さん時間じかんも寝ねていない
    一いち + counter + も + negative "Not even one" 一いち度ども行いかない
    少すこしも + negative "Not even a little" 少すこしも分わからない
    ちっとも + negative "Not at all" ちっとも知しらない

    The number one is special

    一いち度ども, 一人ひとりも, 一ひとつも with negative verbs create the strongest universal negation. They mean "not even once / not even one person / not even one thing."

    Wh-word + も Grammar¶

    Wh-word Negative Form Meaning Affirmative Form Meaning
    何なんも 何なんも〜ない nothing / not anything (non-standard) —
    誰だれも 誰だれも〜ない nobody / not anyone 誰だれもが知しる everyone knows (literary)
    どこも どこも〜ない nowhere / not anywhere どこも賑にぎやか everywhere is lively
    いつも (rare) — いつも〜 always
    どれも どれも〜ない none どれも〜 all of them
    いくらも いくらも〜ない not much いくらも〜 plenty

    何なんか vs. 何なんも

    何なんか (something) is the affirmative counterpart to 何なんも (nothing). They are not interchangeable: - 何なんか食たべた (I ate something) - 何なんも食たべなかった (I ate nothing)

    Japanese Mindset¶

    English tends to add information through new subjects and verbs: "I went. She went too. He also went." Japanese tends to add information through particle manipulation on a stable predicate: 私わたしが行いった。彼女かのじょも行いった。彼かれも行いった。 The predicate stays; the participants accumulate.

    This reflects a broader Japanese preference for stable frames with modular participants. も is the perfect tool for this — it allows the sentence skeleton to remain while new elements are clipped on.

    The scalar uses reveal another preference: quantities as emotional events. 三さん時間じかんも待まった doesn't just state the duration; it evaluates it. The も turns a measurement into an experience. English requires separate words ("a whole three hours," "as much as three hours") to achieve this; Japanese builds it into the particle.

    • Inclusion as social harmony. Additive も is the grammar of solidarity. Saying 私わたしも is not just information — it's alignment. You are joining the other's frame.
    • Extremity as rhetoric. Scalar も pushes quantities to the edge of believability. It makes numbers feel large, small, shocking, or pitiful.
    • Exhaustion as negation. Wh-word + も + negative doesn't just deny — it empties the universe of possibilities. 何なんもない is stronger than ない because it denies the existence of any possible thing.

    Nuance Comparison¶

    Grammar Point Core Meaning Emotional / Pragmatic Nuance Trigger Context JLPT
    も (additive) Also / too Solidarity, inclusion, joining a set Adding oneself or others to an established group N5
    も (scalar) As much as / not even Emphasis on quantity as surprising or insufficient Quantities with emotional loading N4
    も (wh-negative) Nothing / nobody / nowhere Universal negation; emptiness Negative sentences with wh-words N4
    ても / でも Even if / even though Extreme condition insufficient to prevent action Conditional sentences pushing boundaries N4
    Noun + でも Even [noun] Extreme member of a category Surprising capability or inclusion N3
    さえ / すら Even More extreme than も; often negative or literary "Not even" with stronger dismissal; literary N3
    だけ Only Exclusionary; limits a set "Nothing but" contexts N4
    しか〜ない Only [negative frame] "Nothing except"; more emphatic than だけ Negative exclusivity N4
    でも (sentence-initial) But Contrastive conjunction (different from particle も) Changing topic, pushing back N5

    も vs. さえ: 子供こどもでもできる means "Even a child can do it" (surprising but neutral). 子供こどもさえできる means "Even a child can do it" (dismissive of adult difficulty, or emphasizing how easy it is). さえ is sharper and more evaluative.

    も vs. だけ: 私わたしも行いく ("I too will go" — inclusive) vs. 私わたしだけ行いく ("Only I will go" — exclusive). They are semantic opposites but structurally similar in replacing particles.

    Alternatives¶

    だけ (only — exclusive)¶

    The opposite of additive も. Limits rather than expands.
    Example 私わたしだけ行いく。
    English "Only I will go."
    Intent Exclusion — others are not going
    vs. も も adds to a set; だけ subtracts from it.

    さえ (even — extreme)¶

    More emphatic and often more critical than scalar も.
    Example 子供こどもさえ知しっている。
    English "Even children know [so why don't you?]"
    Intent Sharp emphasis; can carry reproach
    vs. も さえ sounds more pointed. も sounds more neutral or inclusive.

    しか〜ない (only — negative frame)¶

    Exclusive, like だけ, but grammatically negative.
    Example 私わたししか行いかない。
    English "Only I will go / Nobody but me is going."
    Intent Strong exclusion with negative framing
    vs. も も is positive/inclusive addition; しか〜ない is negative/exclusive limitation.

    ても vs. とても (completely different)¶

    Visual similarity trap

    高たかくても (even if expensive) and とても高たかい (very expensive) share ても/とても but are unrelated. Do not confuse them.

    Register and Formality¶

    も is neutral and appears across all registers. The formality shifts come from the main predicate, not from も itself.

    私わたしも行いく。千せん円えんもしないし。

    "I'm going too. And it won't even cost 1000 yen."

    Shift: Plain form, additive + scalar. The し adds "and what's more."

    私わたしも行いきます。千せん円えんもいたしません。

    "I will go as well. It will not even amount to 1000 yen."

    Shift: です／ます on the main verbs. も itself is unchanged. しない → いたしません (humble negative of する).

    私わたしどもも参さんります。いかなる場合ばあいでも対応たいおういたします。

    "We too will attend. We will respond in any and all circumstances."

    Shift: 私わたしどもも (humble plural + additive も). いかなる場合ばあいでも (literary/extreme conditional + も). も survives even in the most formal registers.

    Grammar Lineage¶

    Step Pattern What It Adds Example
    N5 — Foundation Noun + も Additive — "also / too" 私わたしも行いく。
    N5 — Foundation AもBも Listing — "both A and B" 猫ねこも犬いぬも好すき。
    N4 — Foundation Quantity + も Scalar — "as much as / not even" 三さん時間じかんも待まった。
    N4 — Foundation Wh-word + も + negative Negative polarity 何なんも食たべない。
    N4 — Core ← You are here 〜ても / 〜でも Conditional extreme — "even if" 高たかくても買かう。
    N3 — Extended Noun + でも Extreme — "even [noun]" 子供こどもでもできる。
    N3 — Extended 〜もしない Minimal — "not even" 一いち度ども行いかない。
    N2 — Awareness 〜も〜ば〜も Correlative — "both… and…" 行いくも行いかないも自由じゆうだ。

    Examples¶

    彼女かのじょも来くるって。 I heard she's coming too [additive, possibly surprising].
    Context: Adding her to the set of attendees. The って marks hearsay.
    三さん時間じかんも待まったのに、来こなかった。 I waited a whole three hours, and yet he didn't come [scalar + のに].
    Context: Scalar も emphasizes the wasted duration; のに adds the frustrated contrast.
    誰だれも知しらない。 Nobody knows [universal negative].
    Context: Complete absence of knowers. Stronger than 知しらない人ひとがいない (double negative).
    高たかくても買かう。 Even if it's expensive, I'll buy it [conditional extreme].
    Context: The expense is acknowledged as extreme but insufficient to stop the purchase.
    子供こどもでもできる。 Even a child can do it [extreme noun].
    Context: The child represents the lower boundary of capability. The task is therefore easy.
    どこも素敵すてきだった。 Everywhere was lovely [universal affirmative].
    Context: どこも with affirmative verb means "everywhere." With negative it would mean "nowhere."
    何なんも心配しんぱいない。 There's nothing to worry about [universal negative].
    Context: Reassurance through total negation. The も exhausts the set of worries.
    一いち度ども行いったことがない。 I've never gone even once [minimal scalar].
    Context: 一いち度ども with negative creates the strongest "never." The minimal quantity is denied.
    百ひゃく円えんもあれば足たりる。 Even 100 yen would be enough [extreme minimal].
    Context: The small amount is marked as sufficient — surprisingly little is needed.
    寿司すしも天てんぷらも食たべた。 I ate both sushi and tempura [listing].
    Context: Multiple items, each marked with も. The predicate applies to the entire set.

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Double-marking with は or が¶

    Incorrect

    私わたしはも行いく。

    ~も replaces は/が. Stacking them is ungrammatical.~

    Correct

    私わたしも行いく。

    2. Using 何なんも with affirmative verbs¶

    Incorrect

    何なんも食たべた。

    ~何なんも requires a negative predicate in standard Japanese. For affirmative "something," use 何なんか. For affirmative "everything," use 全部ぜんぶ or 何なんでも.~

    Correct

    何なんか食たべた。 (I ate something.) 何なんも食たべなかった。 (I ate nothing.)

    3. Confusing sentence-initial でも with conditional でも¶

    Ambiguous misread

    でも、高たかいよ。

    ~This is the conjunction "but," unrelated to the particle も. It comes from それでも.~

    Understanding

    雨あめでも出でかける = "Even if it rains, I'll go out" (conditional particle でも). でも、高たかいよ = "But it's expensive" (sentence-initial conjunction).

    4. Using いつも with negative verbs for "never"¶

    Pragmatically odd

    いつも行いかない。

    ~This means "I always don't go" (habitual non-attendance), not "I never go." For "never," use 一いち度ども行いかない or 全然ぜんぜん行いかない.~

    Correct

    一いち度ども行いかない。 (I never go — not even once.)

    5. Assuming scalar も always means "a lot"¶

    Context error

    千せん円えんもある。 = "I have as much as 1000 yen" [surprisingly much]. 千せん円えんもない。 = "I don't even have 1000 yen" [dismally little].

    ~The same scalar も flips meaning based on predicate polarity. Learners sometimes miss the flip.~

    Collocation Notes¶

    Collocation Meaning
    私わたしも "Me too" — the universal additive
    〜も〜も "Both… and…" — listing pattern
    何なんも〜ない "Not… anything" — universal negative
    誰だれも〜ない "Nobody…" — universal negative
    どこも〜ない "Nowhere…" — universal negative
    一いち度ども〜ない "Never…" — minimal scalar negative
    少すこしも〜ない "Not even a little…" — scalar negative
    ちっとも〜ない "Not at all…" — emphatic scalar negative
    いくらも〜ない "Not much…" — scalar negative
    千せん円えんもする "Costs as much as 1000 yen" — large scalar
    三さん時間じかんも "A whole three hours" — temporal scalar
    子供こどもでも "Even a child" — extreme capability
    誰だれでも "Anyone" — universal extreme
    何なんでも "Anything / everything" — universal affirmative
    いつも "Always" — lexicalized temporal universal
    どこでも "Anywhere / everywhere" — locative universal
    〜てもいい "It's okay even if…" — permission pattern
    〜てもかまわない "I don't mind even if…" — permissive conditional
    〜なくてもいい "Don't have to…" — negative permission
    〜てもしょうがない "Even if… it's no use" — resigned conditional
    〜ても無駄むだだ "Even if… it's useless" — futility

    ~These collocations are worth learning as complete units. 何なんも〜ない, 〜てもいい, and 誰だれでも are especially high-frequency.~

    Hidden Points¶

    • も can stack on multiple items in a single predicate. 猫ねこも犬いぬも鳥とりも好すき is perfectly natural — three も-marked items with one predicate. English would need "I like cats, dogs, and birds" or "I like cats and dogs and even birds."
    • も replaces を, but the object reading remains. When you say 本ほんも読よむ, the も tells you the book is added to a set, but the underlying grammatical role (object) is unchanged. This is why も is sometimes called a "case-marking particle" rather than a "sentence particle."
    • Scalar も with time can be nostalgic or burdensome. 三さん時間じかんも待まった sounds long and burdensome. 三さん時間じかんも話はなした sounds long and possibly intimate (we talked for a whole three hours). The emotional valence depends on the verb.
    • 何なんでも and 誰だれでも are affirmative universals. Unlike 何なんも/誰だれも which require negatives, 何なんでも食たべる means "I eat anything" and 誰だれでも来きていい means "Anyone may come." The でも here is the conditional/extreme particle, not the conjunction.
    • いつも is the lexicalized exception. It behaves like an adverb meaning "always" and does not require negative polarity. It is essentially a different word that happens to share the spelling.
    • 〜てもいい is a permission pattern with hidden complexity. 行いってもいい ("You may go") uses も to mark the action as extreme — "Even if you go, it's fine." Over time this has grammaticalized into the standard permission pattern. But the scalar origin is still audible: the speaker is saying "Going is at the edge of acceptable actions, and I'm okay with it."
    • 〜もしない with positive verbs means "not even." 笑わらいもしない ("doesn't even laugh") is stronger than 笑わらわない ("doesn't laugh"). The も exhausts the set of possible reactions.
    • The "both… and…" pattern (AもBも) implies exhaustiveness. When you say 猫ねこも犬いぬも好すき, you imply these are the relevant categories. Adding 鳥とりも changes the implied set. The listener unconsciously tracks what も marks as "the complete relevant group."

    Learner Stage¶

    Stage What It Looks Like
    Now — N5 Use additive も correctly (私わたしも, AもBも); avoid double-marking with は/が; recognize いつも as "always"
    Developing — N5→N4 Deploy scalar も with quantities (三さん時間じかんも, 千せん円えんも); master 何なんも/誰だれも/どこも + negative; use 〜ても/〜でも for conditional extremes
    Automatic — N4→N3 Distinguish additive も from scalar も from extreme も in real time; use 子供こどもでも naturally; understand 〜てもいい as permission; produce 一いち度ども〜ない without thinking
    Mastery You control the full も spectrum — adding items, scaling quantities, exhausting universes, and pushing conditional boundaries. You know when さえ would be too sharp, when だけ would be too exclusive, and when も is exactly right. You also hear the scalar origin in てもいい permission and the lexicalized drift in いつも.
