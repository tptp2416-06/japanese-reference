    Questions Inside Sentences¶

    〜か & 〜かどうか — Embedding a Question in a Larger Statement¶

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • Japanese can embed a full question inside a larger sentence using the particle か (for both yes/no and wh-questions) or かどうか (exclusively for yes/no questions).
    • かどうか explicitly signals "whether or not" — it turns a yes/no alternative into a noun-like clause that can serve as the object, subject, or topic of a sentence.
    • In the reference sentence — 週末しゅうまつ、旅行りょこうに行いくかどうか決きめましょう — the embedded question "whether or not we go on a trip this weekend" is the thing being decided.
    • When embedding, the copula だ is dropped before か. Plain forms are used; politeness is expressed on the main-clause verb.
    • Embedded questions commonly appear with verbs of decision (決きめる), knowledge (知しっている, わかる), inquiry (調しらべる, 聞きく), and communication (教おしえる, 伝つたえる).

    The Core Distinction — Direct vs. Embedded Questions¶

    Two Ways a Question Appears

    A question can be the main sentence itself (direct) or a piece of a larger sentence (embedded).

    Type Function Example
    Direct question The sentence is the question. Ends with か and rising intonation. 週末しゅうまつ、旅行りょこうに行いきますか？ "Will you go on a trip this weekend?"
    Embedded question The question is a component inside a statement, command, or another question. The main sentence does something with that question — decides it, knows it, checks it. 週末しゅうまつ、旅行りょこうに行いくかどうか決きめましょう。 "Let's decide whether to go on a trip this weekend."

    In the reference sentence, the speaker is not asking a direct question. They are proposing that the group decide a particular question. That question — "go or not go" — is packaged as the object of 決きめる.

    Yes/No Embedded Questions — かどうか & か¶

    A yes/no question is embedded in one of two ways:

    Pattern Structure Nuance
    Plain form + かどうか 行いくかどうか Explicitly presents the yes/no alternative: "whether or not [X]." This is the standard, unambiguous choice.
    Plain form + か 行いくか Implies the yes/no alternative without spelling out the "or not." Slightly more compact, but can sometimes be ambiguous with wh-questions if context isn't clear.

    Both are grammatical, but かどうか is more common and safer for yes/no embeddings, especially in formal or careful speech.

    Formation — かどうか¶

    Verb / Adjective / Noun Plain Form + かどうか
    Verb (affirmative) 行いく 行いくかどうか
    Verb (negative) 行いかない 行いかないかどうか
    i-adjective 高たかい 高たかいかどうか
    na-adjective 便利べんり∅ 便利べんりかどうか
    Noun 休やすみ∅ 休やすみかどうか

    だ is dropped before かどうか

    For na-adjectives and nouns, the copula だ is never used before か or かどうか.

    • 便利べんりだかどうか ❌
    • 便利べんりかどうか ✅
    • 休やすみだかどうか ❌
    • 休やすみかどうか ✅

    The Reference Sentence — かどうか¶

    週末しゅうまつ、旅行りょこうに行いくかどうか決きめましょう。

    Component Meaning
    週末しゅうまつ "Weekend" — time context
    旅行りょこうに "On a trip" — destination and purpose
    行いく "Go" — plain present, the action being questioned
    かどうか "Whether or not" — turns 行いく into an embedded yes/no question
    決きめましょう "Let's decide" — the main verb that takes the embedded question as its object

    The embedded clause 旅行りょこうに行いくかどうか functions as a single noun-like unit. It is the thing being decided. You can think of it as: "Let's decide [the question of whether we'll go on a trip this weekend]."

    Wh- Embedded Questions — か Only¶

    Wh-questions (who, what, where, when, why, how) are embedded with か alone. かどうか is not used — the wh-word already specifies the unknown.

    Wh-word + plain clause + か Meaning
    誰だれが来くるか Who will come
    いつ出発しゅっぱつするか When (we) will depart
    何なにを食たべるか What (we) will eat
    どこに泊とまるか Where (we) will stay
    どうやって行いくか How (we) will go
    なぜ遅おくれたか Why (someone) was late
    彼かれがどこに住すんでいるか知しっていますか？ Do you know where he lives?
    The embedded question is "where he lives." The main question is "do you know?"

    The Copula Drop — だ Before か¶

    When embedding a question that ends in a noun or na-adjective, the copula だ is systematically dropped before か and かどうか. This is a hard grammatical rule, not a stylistic choice.

    Plain Statement Embedded Question (だ→∅) Meaning
    これは本ほんだ。 これが本ほん∅かどうか調しらべる。 Check whether this is a book.
    あの店みせは有名ゆうめいだ。 あの店みせが有名ゆうめい∅かどうか知しっている？ Do you know whether that shop is famous?
    明日あしたは休やすみだ。 明日あしたが休やすみ∅かどうか確認かくにんする。 Confirm whether tomorrow is a day off.

    Why this happens

    The copula だ evolved from である, and in embedded contexts, the attributive form of the copula (である/な) is used historically. The だ is a sentence-final form and is incompatible with subordination markers like か. The underlying structure is closer to 〜であるか, but in modern Japanese the ある is also dropped, leaving just the bare noun/adjective stem + か.

    Particles After Embedded Questions¶

    The embedded question functions as a noun-like element. It can be followed by case particles like を, が, に, で, と, or by the topic marker は. However, the accusative を and nominative が are often omitted in natural speech, especially in casual register.

    Particle Function Example
    を (often omitted) Marks the embedded question as the direct object of verbs like 決きめる, 調しらべる, 聞きく, 確認かくにんする 行いくかどうか(を)決きめる。
    が (often omitted) Marks the embedded question as the subject, typically with verbs like わかる, 決きまる, 問題もんだいだ 誰だれが来くるか(が)重要じゅうようだ。
    は Topic-marks the embedded question for contrast or emphasis 行いくかどうかはまだ決きめていない。
    によって "Depending on" — the embedded question is the factor 天気てんきがいいかどうかによって決きめよう。
    週末しゅうまつ、旅行りょこうに行いくかどうかを決きめましょう。 Same meaning as the reference sentence, with the を explicitly marking the object. Slightly more formal.

    In the reference sentence, を is omitted. This is the most natural pattern in everyday speech.

    Verb Types That Take Embedded Questions¶

    Certain verb families routinely take embedded questions as their complements. Learning these as collocations helps with natural production.

    Category Verbs Example
    Decision / Determination 決きめる, 決きまる, 決断けつだんする, 判断はんだんする 行いくかどうか決きめる
    Knowledge / Understanding 知しっている, わかる, 理解りかいする, 把握はあくする 誰だれが来くるか知しっている
    Investigation / Confirmation 調しらべる, 確認かくにんする, 検査けんさする, 調査ちょうさする 本当ほんとうかどうか調しらべる
    Inquiry 聞きく, 尋たずねる, 質問しつもんする, 伺うかがう どこにいるか聞きく
    Memory 覚おぼえている, 思おもい出だす どこに置おいたか覚おぼえている
    Communication 教おしえる, 伝つたえる, 話はなす, 報告ほうこくする, 説明せつめいする いつ着つくか教おしえる
    Relevance / Importance 問題もんだいだ, 重要じゅうようだ, 関係かんけいある, 次第しだいだ 誰だれがやるかが問題もんだいだ
    彼女かのじょがこのことを知しっているかどうか確認かくにんして。 Check whether she knows about this.
    確認かくにんする is an investigation verb. The embedded yes/no question is its object.

    Politeness and Register in Embedded Clauses¶

    The embedded clause is typically in plain form, even when the main clause is polite. The politeness level is marked on the main-clause verb only.

    Main Clause Embedded Clause Correct/Incorrect
    Polite Polite ❌ 行いきますかどうか決きめましょう
    Polite Plain ✅ 行いくかどうか決きめましょう
    Polite Plain (negative) ✅ 行いかないかどうか決きめましょう

    Do not use です・ます inside the embedded question

    The embedded clause is grammatically subordinate. Politeness markers like です and ます appear only in the main clause (the outermost verb). Using them inside the embedded clause is a common learner error.

    • 彼かれが来くるかどうか知しっていますか？ ✅
    • 彼かれが来きますかどうか知しっていますか？ ❌

    However, in very formal written Japanese (e.g., academic papers, formal reports), である may appear inside embedded questions: 〜であるかどうか. This is a different register and not the norm for everyday business or conversational Japanese.

    かどうか vs. か — Nuance and Preference¶

    For yes/no embedded questions, both かどうか and bare か are grammatical. The choice is a matter of clarity, register, and rhythm.

    かどうか か
    Clarity Unambiguous — explicitly means "whether or not." Can be ambiguous, especially if a wh-word could be implied.
    Formality Slightly more formal or careful. Preferred in writing and business. Slightly more casual or spoken. Common in fast conversation.
    Emphasis Emphasises the binary alternative. Neutral; the "or not" is implied from context.
    With negative embedded 行いかないかどうか → explicit: "whether or not [I] don't go" 行いかないか → could sound like "the question of not going" without the alternative
    彼かれが来くるかどうか知しらない。 I don't know whether he'll come (or not).
    彼かれが来くるか知しらない。 I don't know whether he'll come. (Same meaning, slightly shorter, common in speech.)

    When to prefer かどうか

    • In any written communication (emails, reports, messages to superiors).
    • When the yes/no alternative is the key point you're deliberating.
    • After negative predicates (行いかないかどうか) for clarity.
    • When learning: it's safer and clearer.

    When bare か is fine: - Fast casual conversation with clear context. - When the verb itself already implies a binary (決きめる, 確認かくにんする) and the alternative is obvious.

    かどうか with the Negative — "Whether or Not I Don't…"¶

    Sometimes the embedded question itself is negative — the speaker is unsure whether the negative state is the case.

    彼かれが来こないかどうか確認かくにんして。 Check whether he's not coming.
    The question is not "will he come?" but "is it the case that he won't come?" The negative is inside the embedded question.

    This is distinct from the positive embedded question. The nuance: the speaker suspects he might not come and wants confirmation of that suspicion.

    Embedded Questions as Topics — Using は and も¶

    The embedded question can be made the topic of the sentence with は, typically when discussing the status of the decision or knowledge itself.

    行いくかどうかはまだ決きめていない。 As for whether I'll go or not — I haven't decided yet.
    誰だれがやるかは後あとで話はなし合あおう。 As for who will do it — let's discuss that later.
    成功せいこうするかどうかは努力次第どりょくしだいだ。 Whether we succeed or not depends on effort.

    Embedded Questions in the Subject Position — が¶

    When the embedded question is the grammatical subject, が is the particle used, though it is often omitted in speech. This occurs especially with predicates like わかる, 問題もんだいだ, 重要じゅうようだ, 決きまる (intransitive "to be decided").

    いつ始はじまるか(が)まだわからない。 I still don't know when it starts.
    誰だれがリーダーか(が)問題もんだいだ。 Who the leader is — that's the issue.

    Direct Quote と vs. Embedded Question か¶

    A common point of confusion: と marks a direct or indirect quote (content of speech or thought), while か marks an embedded question. They are not interchangeable.

    と — Quote か — Embedded Question
    彼かれは「行いく」と言いった。 彼かれが行いくか聞きいた。
    He said, "I'll go." I asked whether he would go.
    Marks the actual words or content of communication. Marks a question being asked, decided, checked, etc.

    Some verbs (聞きく, 言いう, 伝つたえる) can take either, with a difference in meaning:

    彼かれに行いくか聞きいた。 I asked him whether he would go. (The question "will you go?" was posed to him.)
    彼かれに行いくと聞きいた。 I heard from someone that he would go. (The information "he will go" came from him or through him.)

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Keeping だ before か／かどうか¶

    Incorrect

    彼かれが学生がくせいだかどうか知しらない。

    ~The copula だ must be dropped before かどうか.~

    Correct

    彼かれが学生がくせい∅かどうか知しらない。

    ~Nouns and na-adjectives attach directly to かどうか without だ.~

    2. Using かどうか with wh-words¶

    Incorrect

    どこに行いくかどうか決きめよう。

    ~かどうか is only for yes/no questions. どこ already fills the unknown slot.~

    Correct

    どこに行いくか決きめよう。

    ~Wh-questions use か alone.~

    3. Using polite forms inside the embedded clause¶

    Incorrect

    彼かれが来きますかどうか知しっていますか？

    ~Polite ます does not belong inside the subordinate clause.~

    Correct

    彼かれが来くるかどうか知しっていますか？

    ~Plain form in the embedded clause; politeness only on the main verb 知しっています.~

    4. Confusing と and か with verbs like 聞きく¶

    Incorrect

    彼かれに来くると聞きいた (intending "I asked him whether he's coming")

    ~With と, 聞きく means "I heard that he's coming" — the inquiry meaning is lost.~

    Correct

    彼かれに来くるか聞きいた。 (I asked whether he's coming.)

    ~か marks the question being asked.~

    Hidden Points¶

    • かどうか literally means "or not." The どう corresponds to "how / in what way" and か is the question marker. Together they form "whether or not." The etymology helps remember why it only works for binary questions.
    • The particle を after embedded questions is dropped roughly 80% of the time in spoken Japanese. Including it sounds slightly more formal or emphatic. Learners who always include を can sound stiff; learners who never include it may struggle in formal writing where it is expected.
    • When the main verb is 決きまっている (it has been decided), the embedded question takes the subject marker が. 行いくかどうかがもう決きまっている。 "Whether we go or not has already been decided." The intransitive verb 決きまる assigns the が role.
    • Embedded questions can nest indefinitely. 誰だれがいつどこで何なんをしたかどうかを調しらべる — "to investigate the matter of who did what, when, and where." Stylistically heavy but grammatically possible.
    • The boundary between bare か and かどうか is blurring in casual speech. Some speakers use か for yes/no embeddings more freely than textbooks suggest. Context almost always disambiguates.

    Extended Examples — かどうか & か in Action¶

    Japanese English
    明日あしたのパーティーに行いくかどうかまだ迷まよってる。 I'm still undecided about whether to go to tomorrow's party.
    迷まよう (to hesitate / be undecided) takes the embedded question as its object.
    彼かれが本当ほんとうのことを言いっているかどうかわからない。 I don't know whether he's telling the truth.
    わかる (to understand/know) is intransitive. The embedded question is the subject of understanding.
    このボタンを押おせば動うごくかどうか試ためしてみよう。 Let's try (pressing this button) to see whether it works.
    試ためす (to test/try) takes the embedded question as the thing being tested.
    電車でんしゃが時間通じかんどおりに来くるか心配しんぱいだ。 I'm worried about whether the train will come on time.
    心配しんぱいだ (to be worried) takes an embedded question as the source of worry.
    誰だれが責任者せきにんしゃか確認かくにんしておきます。 I'll confirm who is the person in charge.
    Wh-question with noun predicate (責任せきにん者しゃ). Note: だ is absent before か.
