    N4-Upper humble-language keigo kenjougo

    Extra Modest Expressions — 謙譲語けんじょうご¶

    おる & お・ご〜する¶

    JLPT Level¶

    JLPT Level — Upper N4 / Lower N3

    The individual patterns (おる, お〜する, ご〜する) are N4 grammar. But deploying them correctly — knowing which verbs have special humble forms, which use お vs. ご, and when extra modesty becomes excessive — is solidly lower N3. This is the first major keigo hurdle where learners must abandon one-to-one translation and think in social layers.

    Tier Pattern Description Example
    N5 — Foundation です／ます Basic polite forms — distance without humility 行いきます。"I go [polite]."
    N4 — Foundation 謙譲語けんじょうご I — お〜する Humble prefix + stem + する for group 1 verbs 持もつ → 持もちます → 持もち致いたす → お持もちします
    N4 — Core ← You are here 謙譲語けんじょうご II — Special verbs & おる Verbs with dedicated humble forms; いる→おる 私わたしは社長室しゃちょうしつに居おります。
    N3 — Extended お〜する vs. ご〜する Choosing prefix based on verb origin (和語わご vs. 漢語かんご) 連絡れんらくする → ご連絡れんらくする
    N3 — Extended 謙譲語けんじょうご overuse & avoidance When humility sounds servile or when 丁寧語ていねいご is safer 食たべる → 頂いただく (humble receive) vs. お食たべする (unnatural)
    N2 — Awareness 謙譲語けんじょうご + 受身形うけみけい (double keigo) お持もちいただく — humble + receive 資料しりょうをお持もちいただきます。

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • 謙譲語けんじょうご (kenjougo / humble language) lowers the speaker's status relative to the listener or a third party. Unlike 丁寧語ていねいご (polite language) which simply adds distance, humble language actively degrades the speaker's actions.
    • Special humble verbs replace common verbs entirely: いる→居おる, 行いく・来くる→参まいる, する→致いたす, 言いう→申もうす, 食たべる・飲のむ→頂いただく, 知しる→存ぞんじる, 見みる→拝見はいけんする.
    • お〜する (for wago / native Japanese verbs) and ご〜する (for kango / Sino-Japanese nouns/verbs) create humble forms for verbs that lack special dedicated forms.
    • おる is the humble form of いる — not merely "to be" but "to humbly be." Using it positions the speaker as occupying space in the listener's world at the listener's tolerance.
    • The boundary between appropriate humility and servile overuse is thin. Native speakers often prefer 丁寧語ていねいご (ます) over 謙譲語けんじょうご when the social distance is unclear.

    Core Meaning¶

    Core Rule

    Use 謙譲語けんじょうご when describing your own actions (or your in-group's actions) in the presence of someone whose status is higher than yours, or when you want to show deference through self-deprecation. The key principle: lower yourself, elevate the other.

    私わたしは明日あした参まいります。 I will come/go tomorrow [humble].
    Literal: I tomorrow humbly-come/go [polite].
    Context: Speaking to a superior about visiting them. 参まいる replaces both 行いく and 来くる in humble contexts.
    私わたしは社長室しゃちょうしつに居おります。 I am in the president's office [humbly occupying space].
    Literal: I president's-office-in humbly-am [polite].
    Context: The input pattern. おる replaces いる. The nuance: "I am present here at the company's tolerance, in the president's space."
    資料しりょうをお持もちします。 I will bring the materials [humbly performing the bringing].
    Literal: Materials [object] humble-bringing do [polite].
    Context: Speaking to a superior. 持もつ→お持もちする. The お elevates the action; the する degrades the agent.

    The Two Systems: Special Verbs & お・ご〜する¶

    謙譲語けんじょうご operates through two parallel systems. Mastering both — and knowing when each applies — is the core N4/N3 keigo challenge.

    System 1: Special Humble Verbs¶

    Certain common verbs have dedicated humble forms that must be memorized. These are not derived from the original verb; they are separate lexical items with built-in humility.

    Plain Humble Meaning Nuance
    いる 居おる (おる) to be, to exist (animate) Humble existence — occupying space at others' tolerance
    行いく / 来くる / いる 参まいる to go / to come / to be Humble movement — approaching or being in another's space
    する 致いたす to do Humble action — performing at another's service
    言いう 申もうす to say Humble speech — speaking to a superior
    食たべる / 飲のむ 頂いただく to eat / to drink / to receive Humble receiving — accepting what is given from above
    知しる 存ぞんじる to know Humble knowledge — knowing as a lesser person
    見みる 拝見はいけんする to see / to look Humble seeing — viewing what is above one's station
    聞きく 伺うかがう to hear / to ask / to visit Humble hearing/asking — receiving information from above
    あげる 差さし上あげる to give (to superior) Humble giving — offering upward
    もらう 頂いただく / 戴いただく to receive (from superior) Humble receiving — accepting from above
    知しっている 存ぞんじて上あげる to know (humble progressive) Progressive humble knowledge

    おる is not casual

    In some dialects (especially Kansai), おる is used casually as a plain form equivalent to いる. In standard keigo, however, おる is strictly humble. Using it casually in Tokyo sounds dialectal or rough; using it formally without keigo intent sounds bizarre.

    System 2: お〜する & ご〜する¶

    For verbs without special humble forms, create one with prefix + stem + する.

    Pattern Applies To Example
    お + [verb stem] + する Wago (和語わご / native Japanese verbs) 持もつ → お持もちする
    ご + [noun/verb] + する Kango (漢語かんご / Sino-Japanese) 連絡れんらくする → ご連絡れんらくする

    お vs. ご — the origin rule

    This is the single most common keigo error. お attaches to native Japanese (和語わご) verb stems. ご attaches to Sino-Japanese (漢語かんご) nouns and verbs. Saying お連絡れんらくする or ご持もちする is ungrammatical.

    Correct Incorrect Why
    お持もちする ご持もちする 持もつ is wago — takes お
    ご連絡れんらくする お連絡れんらくする 連絡れんらく is kango — takes ご
    お書かきする ご書かきする 書かく is wago — takes お
    ご紹介しょうかいする お紹介しょうかいする 紹介しょうかい is kango — takes ご

    The wago/kango test

    If the verb can be written in pure hiragana without 漢字かんじ (持もつ→もつ), it's likely wago and takes お. If it's already in 漢字かんじ and feels "compound-y" (連絡れんらく, 紹介しょうかい, 説明せつめい), it's likely kango and takes ご. This is a rough heuristic but works for most N4/N3 vocabulary.

    In Real Life¶

    In Real Life

    Situation: 田中たなか (Tanaka), a junior sales representative at a trading company, is speaking to 山本部長やまもとぶちょう (Yamamoto, the department head). Tanaka has been summoned to the director's office and is now reporting back.

    私わたしは今いま、社長室しゃちょうしつの前まえに居おります。お呼よびでしょうか。

    "I am now humbly positioned in front of the president's office. Might this be a summons?"

    Why おる fits here: Tanaka could have said います — grammatically correct, socially acceptable. But おります signals awareness of hierarchy: he is in the president's space, at the president's building, on the president's time. His very existence there is contingent. The おる acknowledges this power geometry.

    Why お呼よび: 呼よぶ (to call/summon) has no special humble form, so Tanaka uses お〜する. But he doesn't complete the pattern — he says お呼よびでしょうか ("Is it a calling?"), using the noun form + copula rather than the full verb (お呼よびしますでしょうか would be ungrammatical). This is a common keigo compression: the humble prefix elevates the noun, and the copula handles the predication.

    What makes this natural: The combination of おる (special humble) and お呼よび (derived humble) shows 田中たなか operating in two keigo systems simultaneously. Native speakers do this automatically; learners must build the pattern recognition.

    How It Works¶

    謙譲語けんじょうご is not merely "polite Japanese." It is a status negotiation system where every verb choice repositions the speaker relative to the listener. Understanding this repositioning is more important than memorizing forms.

    Layer 1 — Self-deprecation: The speaker describes their own actions as lesser, smaller, or performed in service. This is not self-hatred; it is social grace. By lowering yourself, you create space to elevate the other.

    Layer 2 — Elevation of other: The 謙譲形けんじょうけいimplies a 受取人うけとりにん or 観察者かんさつしゃ who is 高たかい. お持もちしますdoesn't just mean "I bring" — it means "I bring [for you, who are above me]." The other 人ひと is built into the 文法ぶんぽう.

    Layer 3 — Spatial humility: Many humble verbs encode physical or social space. 居おる (to humbly be) implies occupying space at another's tolerance. 参まいる (to humbly go/come) implies moving within another's sphere. 伺うかがう (to humbly hear/ask/visit) implies approaching another's presence.

    私わたしが行いきます。 I will go [polite, neutral].
    The speaker is going. The destination and purpose are neutral.
    私わたしが参まいります。 I will go/come [humble, deferential].
    The speaker's movement is framed as approaching a superior's space. The going is an act of service or obligation.
    私わたしはここに居おります。 I am here [humbly existing].
    The speaker's presence is contingent, tolerated, perhaps even intrusive. The おる acknowledges this.

    Real Speech vs. Textbook Form¶

    Textbooks present keigo as a fixed system: "Use 謙譲語けんじょうご for your own actions, 尊敬語そんけいご for others' actions." Real speechスピーチ is messier — speakers often avoid keigo entirely when uncertain, or mix levels within a single utterance.

    Form Example Note
    Textbook (pure keigo) 私わたしは明日あした参まいります。 Clean, correct, slightly stiff
    Real speech (keigo + polite) 明日あした、お邪魔じゃまさせていただきます。 "I will humbly receive the favor of disturbing you tomorrow" — 謙譲語けんじょうご + 使役しえき + 受身形うけみけい, extremely common in business
    Real speech (keigo avoidance) 明日あした行いきますね。 Neutral polite — when the relationship is close or the keigo boundary unclear
    Real speech (overkeigo) 私わたしがお食たべいたします。 Excessive — 食たべるhas special form 頂いただく; お食たべするis unnatural and sounds like parody

    Grammar Analysis¶

    Function in Context¶

    • 私わたしは marks the speaker as topic — standard. In keigo contexts, first-person pronouns are often dropped because the humble verb itself implies "I."
    • 社長室しゃちょうしつの前まえに locates the speaker spatially. The location (in front of the president's office) is high-status space; the おる acknowledges this.
    • おります is the humble form of いる, conjugated to polite ます-form. The stem is おり; the ます attaches to the i-stem (おり → おります). This is a regular Godan conjugation despite the verb's special status.
    • お呼よびでしょうか uses the derived humble pattern incompletely — 呼よぶ→お呼よび (stem + prefix), then treated as a noun with copula でしょうか. This is a common keigo compression: rather than "Will you summon me?" (direct, potentially demanding), the speaker asks "Is this a summons?" (indirect, self-effacing).
    • Without おる: 私わたくしは社長室しゃちょうしつの前まえにいます would be a neutral location report. With おる: the location becomes a statement of social position — "I am positioned here, aware of my place."
    • Without お呼よび: 呼よばれるんですか ("Will I be called?") uses passive and sounds passive-aggressive or anxious. お呼よびでしょうか is proactively humble.
    社長しゃちょう、お戻もどりですか。 President, have you returned [to your esteemed presence]?
    Literal: President, humble-return is?
    Context: Staff to president. 戻もどる→お戻もどり, then nominalized with copula. The staff does not ask "Did you come back?" — they ask about the state of the president's presence.

    Construction¶

    Special Humble Verbs — Conjugation¶

    Plain Humble Stem Polite (ます) Te-form Past
    いる 居おり (おり) 居おります 居おって 居おった
    行いく / 来くる 参まいり (まいり) 参まいります 参まいりまして 参まいりました
    する 致いたし (いたし) 致いたします 致いたしまして 致いたしました
    言いう 申もうし (もうし) 申もうします 申もうしまして 申もうしました
    食たべる / 飲のむ 頂いただき (いただき) 頂いただきます 頂いただきまして 頂いただきました
    知しる 存ぞんじ (ぞんじ) 存ぞんじます 存ぞんじまして 存ぞんじました

    おる conjugation is Godan

    Despite being a special humble verb, おる conjugates as a standard Godan verb: おらない, おろう, おれば. Do not treat it as irregular.

    お〜する / ご〜する — Formation¶

    Verb Type Dictionary Stem Prefix Humble Form
    Wago Godan 持もつ 持もち お お持もちする
    Wago Godan 書かく 書かき お お書かきする
    Wago Ichidan 見みる 見み お お見みする (but 拝見はいけんする preferred)
    Kango noun 連絡れんらく 連絡れんらく ご ご連絡れんらくする
    Kango noun 説明せつめい 説明せつめい ご ご説明せつめいする

    Not all verbs take お〜する

    Verbs with special humble forms (行いく→参まいる, 見みる→拝見はいけんする) should use those forms instead of お〜する. Saying お見みする or お行いきする is unnatural — not ungrammatical, but like wearing a tuxedo t-shirt.

    Japanese Mindset¶

    Keigo is not a costume you put on for formal occasions. It is a social positioning system that operates constantly, even unconsciously. The choice between います and おります is not about politeness level — it is about acknowledging that you exist in a web of relationships where some positions are higher than others.

    The humble speaker does not say "I am less than you" (which would be self-abasement). They say "I am performing a role in which my actions are oriented toward you." The humility is relational, not personal.

    • Space as social geometry. おる is humble because it implies occupying space that belongs to another. When Tanaka says 社長室しゃちょうしつの前まえにおります, he is not saying "I am small" — he is saying "I am in the president's space, positioned by his authority." The humility is spatial and relational.
    • Action as service. お持もちします doesn't mean "I am a lowly being who brings." It means "My bringing is an act performed in your service." The action is redefined by its orientation toward the superior.
    • The paradox of keigo fluency. Native speakers often claim keigo is "difficult" even while using it flawlessly. This is because keigo is not a set of rules but a social intuition — the forms are easy, but the calibration is hard. Knowing when おります is respectful and when it is servile requires reading the room, not the textbook.

    Grammar Point Core Meaning Emotional / Pragmatic Nuance Trigger Context JLPT

    ご〜する Derived humble (kango) Standard humility for Sino-Japanese vocabulary Most Sino-Japanese nouns/verbs in humble contexts N4

    Alternatives¶

    丁寧語ていねいご (neutral polite) — ます／です¶

    The safe default when keigo boundaries are unclear. No status negotiation, just distance.
    Example 私わたしは明日あした行いきます。
    English "I will go tomorrow [polite]."
    Intent Neutral, respectful, no implication of hierarchy
    vs. humble Removes the self-deprecation. Safe when you don't know if the listener expects keigo or would find it excessive.

    When to use this instead

    Use 丁寧語ていねいご when the social relationship is unclear, when speaking to strangers of unknown status, or when you want to be polite without implying hierarchy. Many Japanese prefer this in ambiguous situations.

    尊敬語そんけいご (respectful language) — いらっしゃる¶

    Elevates the other person's actions, not your own. Often paired with 謙譲語けんじょうご in the same sentence.
    Example 社長しゃちょうは今いまお戻もどりになりました。
    English "The president has now returned [respectful]."
    Intent Elevates the president's action. Contrast with 私わたしは戻もどりました (humble) or 社長しゃちょうは戻もどりました (neutral).
    vs. humble 尊敬語そんけいご and 謙譲語けんじょうご are complementary, not alternatives. Use both in the same sentence: 私わたしがお持もちします (humble self) + 社長しゃちょうがお戻もどりになります (respectful other).

    使役受身形しえきうけみけい (causative-passive) — させていただく¶

    Extreme humility — "I will humbly receive the favor of being allowed to do X."
    Example 説明せつめいさせていただきます。
    English "I will take the liberty of explaining [humble + receive permission]."
    Intent The speaker frames their action as something they are being allowed to do by a superior. Ultra-humble.
    vs. お〜する ご説明せつめいします is humble. 説明せつめいさせていただきます is ultra-humble — the speaker is not even the agent, just the recipient of permission.

    Register and Formality¶

    謙譲語けんじょうご is by definition formal, but its intensity varies. The key is matching the humility level to the social gap.

    私わたし、明日あした行いくよ。

    "I'm going tomorrow, okay?"

    Shift: Plain form, no keigo. Appropriate for friends, family, peers. Using 参まいる here would be bizarre — like bowing to your roommate.

    私わたしは明日あした行いきます。

    "I will go tomorrow [polite]."

    Shift: です／ます only. No keigo. Appropriate for most workplace interactions where hierarchy is present but not rigid, or when unsure of keigo boundaries.

    私わたしは明日あした参まいります。

    "I will humbly go/come tomorrow."

    Shift: 行いく→参まいる. Appropriate for speaking to clear superiors or in formal service contexts. The humility is explicit but not excessive.

    明日あした、お邪魔じゃまさせていただけますでしょうか。

    "Might I be permitted to humbly disturb you tomorrow?"

    Shift: 邪魔じゃまする→お邪魔じゃまさせていただく (humble + causative + receive). The speaker is not even the agent of the action — they are the recipient of permission to perform it. Appropriate for highest-stakes formal requests.

    N4 — Core お〜する / ご〜する Derived humble pattern for verbs without special forms お持もちします。
    N3 — Extended お vs. ご selection Choosing prefix based on wago/kango origin ご連絡れんらくする
    N3 — Extended 謙譲語けんじょうご overuse avoidance Knowing when 丁寧語ていねいご is safer than 謙譲語けんじょうご 食たべる → 頂いただく (ok) vs. お食たべする (avoid)
    N2 — Awareness 使役受身形しえきうけみけい (causative-passive humble) させていただく — receiving permission to act 説明せつめいさせていただきます。
    N2 — Awareness 謙譲語けんじょうご + 尊敬語そんけいご mixing Using both systems in same sentence for full keigo 私わたしがお持もちします。社長しゃちょうはいらっしゃいますか。

    Examples¶

    私わたしは会議室かいぎしつに居おります。 I am in the meeting room [humbly present].
    Literal: I meeting-room-in humbly-am [polite].

    ~Context: Speaking to a superior who is looking for you. The おる acknowledges that your presence is at their disposal.*~

    資料しりょうをお持もちします。 I will bring the materials [humbly].
    Literal: Materials [object] humble-bringing do [polite].

    ~Context: To a superiorしゅぺりおる or clientくらいえんと. 持もつ→お持もちする. The お elevates the materialsまてりある; the する degrades the agentえーじぇんと.*~

    明日あした、ご連絡れんらくします。 I will contact you tomorrow [humbly].
    Literal: Tomorrow, humble-contact do [polite].

    ~Context: To a superior or customer. 連絡れんらくする→ご連絡れんらくする. Kango takes ご, not お.*~

    社長しゃちょう、お戻もどりですか。 President, have you returned [to your esteemed presence]?
    Literal: President, humble-return is?

    ~Context: Staff to president. 戻もどる→お戻もどり, nominalized with copula. The question is about the state of the president's presence, not the action of returning.*~

    何なにかご質問しつもんがございましたら、お申もうし付つけください。 If you have any questions, please humbly command me [to answer them].
    Literal: Something questions if-humbly-exist, humble-saying-attach please.

    ~Context: Ultra-formal service language. 質問しつもんがある→ご質問しつもんがございましたら (humble existence); 言ゆう→申もうす (humble speech); 付つける→お申もうし付つけください (humble command). The speaker positions themselves as entirely at the listener's disposal.*~

    私わたしどもでございますが… It is we [humble we] who…
    Literal: We-humble [humble copula] but…

    ~Context: Formal self-introduction by company representative. 私わたくしども (humble "we") + でございます (humble copula). The entire self-reference is wrapped in humility.*~

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Using お〜する for verbs with special humble forms¶

    The most common keigo error — creating derived humble forms when dedicated ones exist.

    Unnatural

    お行いきします

    ~行いく has special humble form 参まいる. お行いきします sounds like a learner's overcorrection — grammatically comprehensible but socially off-key.~

    Correct

    参まいります

    ~Use the special form. Memorize the list: 行いく/来くる→参まいる, する→致いたす, 言いう→申もうす, 食たべる/飲のむ→頂いただく, 見みる→拝見はいけんする, 知しる→存ぞんじる.~

    2. Confusing お and ご prefixes¶

    Mixing wago and kango prefixes produces ungrammatical forms that immediately mark the speaker as non-native.

    Incorrect

    お連絡れんらくする

    ~連絡れんらく is kango (Sino-Japanese) — must take ご, not お.~

    Correct

    ご連絡れんらくする

    ~Kango → ご prefix. The wago/kango distinction is non-negotiable in standard keigo.~

    Incorrect

    ご持もちする

    ~持つ is wago (native Japanese) — must take お, not ご.~

    Correct

    お持もちする

    ~Wago → お prefix.~

    3. Overusing 謙譲語けんじょうご in inappropriate contexts¶

    Excessive humility can sound servile, sarcastic, or simply foreign.

    Socially awkward

    友達ともだちに会あいに参まいります。

    "I will humbly go to meet my friend." — Using 参まいる for peer relationships sounds bizarre, like bowing to your cat.

    Appropriate

    友達ともだちに会あいに行いきます。

    "I'm going to meet a friend." — Neutral polite. Keigo is for hierarchical relationships, not intimacy.

    Collocation Notes¶

    Collocation Meaning
    私わたしは〜に居おります "I am [humbly] at/in ~" — formal self-location
    お〜します / ご〜します "I [humbly] do ~" — the standard derived humble pattern
    お〜致いたします "I [humbly] do ~ [extreme]" — お〜する + 致いたす double humble
    何なにかございましたら "If there is anything [humble existence]" — formal service opener
    お申もうし付つけください "Please command me [humbly]" — ultra-deferential request for instruction
    お気きを付つけて下くださいませ "Please take care [humbly]" — formal farewell
    私わたしども "We [humble]" — formal company/organization self-reference
    でございます "[Humbly] is" — humble copula replacing です

    ~These collocations are worth memorizing as complete units. 何なにかございましたら and お申もうし付つけください especially are service-industry staples.~

    Hidden Points¶

    • おる carries dialectal baggage. In Kansai and some other regions, おる is plain-form casual (equivalent to standard いる). A Kyoto shopkeeper saying おるで is not being humble — they're being local. In Tokyo standard, おる is keigo only. This regional split causes genuine confusion among native speakers too.
    • 参まいる means both "go" and "come" — but only in humble contexts. It replaces 行いく when the movement is toward a superior's space, and 来くる when the movement is from the speaker's perspective toward the listener. The directionality is determined by social geometry, not physical direction.
    • 頂いただく (いただく) is the Swiss Army knife of humility. It means "to humbly eat," "to humbly drink," "to humbly receive," and even "to humbly be allowed to do" (〜させていただく). Its semantic range is vast because the core meaning is simply "to accept from above."
    • 存ぞんじる has two readings. 存ぞんじる (ぞんじる) for "to know" and 存ぞんじ上あげる (ぞんじあげる) for "to know [about you, respectfully]." ご存知ぞんじです (そんちです) is actually 尊敬語そんけいご (respectful), not humble — it elevates the listener's knowledge. The 存ぞんじ family is a keigo minefield.
    • お〜する can be split by particles. You can say お持もちします (continuous) or お持もちいたします (humble + extreme humble). You can also nominalize: お持もちすること (the act of humbly bringing). The pattern is productive once internalized.
    • The "humble we" (私わたくしども) vs. "humble I" (私わたくし). Using 私わたくしども for a single speaker sounds archaic or ultra-formal in some contexts, but standard in company representatives. The ども (共とも / "together") historically meant "companions" but now functions as a humble pluralizer.

    Stage What It Looks Like
    Now — N4 Memorize special humble verbs (おるおる, 参まいる, 致いたす, 申もうす, 頂いただく, 拝見はいけんする, 存ぞんじる, 伺うかがう); produce お〜する and ご〜する correctly; distinguish wago from kango for prefix selection
    Developing — N4→N3 Deploy 敬語けいご naturally in service and workplace contexts; know when 丁寧語ていねいご is safer than 謙譲語けんじょうご; avoid overkeigo; begin mixing 謙譲語けんじょうご and 尊敬語そんけいご in same utterance
    Automatic — N3→N2 Keigo choices become automatic — you feel the social distance and the right form arrives without analysis. You recognize when someone uses おる dialectally vs. humbly.
    Mastery You control the full keigo spectrum from casual plain form through double keigo (させていただく). You know when humility sounds sincere and when it sounds servile. You can read the social geometry of any room and calibrate accordingly.
