    N4-Core adjective-formation ease-difficulty yasui-nikui

    Ease and Difficulty — 〜やすい・〜にくい¶

    飲のみやすい・分わかりにくい・信しんじがたい¶

    JLPT Level¶

    JLPT Level — N4 Core / Lower N3 Extended

    〜やすい and 〜にくい are foundational N4 patterns — verb-stem adjectives that describe inherent ease or difficulty of an action. But the full ecosystem of "difficulty" in Japanese (〜にくい vs. 〜づらい vs. 〜がたい) is solidly N3, and learners often conflate them or overuse 難むずかしい where a stem-adjective would be more natural.

    Tier Pattern Description Example
    N5 — Foundation 簡単かんたんだ / 難むずかしい Objective difficulty (standalone adjectives) この本ほんは難むずかしい。
    N5 — Foundation 上手じょうずだ / 下手へただ Personal skill level 彼かれは泳およぐのが上手じょうず。
    N4 — Core ← You are here 〜やすい / 〜にくい Inherent ease/difficulty of an action 飲のみやすい。分わかりにくい。
    N3 — Extended 〜づらい Physical or psychological discomfort in doing 持もちづらい。言いいづらい。
    N3 — Extended 〜がたい Emotional resistance; nearly impossible 信しんじがたい。許ゆるしがたい。
    N3 — Extended 〜かねる Hesitation due to position / inability to bring oneself to do 承諾しょうだくしかねる。

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • 〜やすい attaches to a verb stem to create an i-adjective meaning "easy to [verb]." 飲のむ → 飲のみやすい (easy to drink). It describes the action as low-friction.
    • 〜にくい attaches to a verb stem to create an i-adjective meaning "hard to [verb]." 飲のむ → 飲のみにくい (hard to drink). It describes the action as high-friction.
    • These forms describe the inherent property of the action or object, not the person's ability. 分わかりにくい means "difficult to understand" (the explanation is unclear); 理解力りかいりょくがない means "lacking comprehension ability" (the person is at fault).
    • At N3, the difficulty spectrum expands: 〜にくい (general friction), 〜づらい (physical/mental discomfort), 〜がたい (emotional resistance bordering on impossibility).
    • 分わかりやすい and 分わかりにくい are among the most frequently used collocations in all of Japanese — worth learning as single vocabulary items.

    Core Meaning¶

    Core Rule

    Use 〜やすい when an action proceeds smoothly due to the nature of the object, method, or circumstances. Use 〜にくい when an action meets resistance due to the same. These are property descriptions, not ability judgments.

    このワインは飲のみやすい。 This wine is easy to drink [smooth, approachable].
    Literal: This wine drink-easy is.
    Context: Describing the wine's inherent quality. Not "I am good at drinking wine" but "The wine goes down easily."
    この説明書せつめいしょは分わかりにくい。 This manual is hard to understand [poorly written].
    Literal: This manual understand-hard is.
    Context: Describing the manual's clarity. The fault lies in the document, not necessarily the reader.
    このペンは書かきやすい。 This pen is easy to write with [good grip, smooth flow].
    Literal: This pen write-easy is.
    Context: The pen's physical properties make writing smooth. A judgment about the tool, not the writer.

    The Difficulty Spectrum: N4 to N3¶

    Japanese does not have one word for "difficult." It has a gradient of friction that depends on whether the resistance is physical, emotional, or logical.

    Form Core Meaning Type of Difficulty Example JLPT
    〜やすい Easy to do Low friction 飲のみやすい N4
    〜にくい Hard to do General resistance; inherent difficulty 飲のみにくい N4
    〜づらい Hard to do Physical strain or social/psychological awkwardness 持もちづらい N3
    〜がたい Hard to do Emotional resistance; nearly impossible 信しんじがたい N3
    〜かねる Cannot bring oneself to do Position-based hesitation 承諾しょうだくしかねる N3
    難むずかしい Difficult (standalone adj) Complex, high-skill 問題もんだいが難むずかしい N5
    簡単かんたんだ Simple (standalone adj) Low complexity 問題もんだいが簡単かんたんだ N5

    The Critical Distinction¶

    この料理りょうりは食たべにくい。 This dish is hard to eat [texture, temperature, size].
    Describes the dish's physical properties. Maybe it's too hot, too tough, or awkwardly shaped.
    この料理りょうりは食たべづらい。 This dish is awkward/uncomfortable to eat [socially or physically].
    Describes discomfort. Maybe you're wearing white clothes and it's messy, or the setting is formal and the food requires messy hands.
    この料理りょうりは食たべがたい。 This dish is unthinkable to eat [emotional revulsion].
    Extreme. Maybe it contains something sacred or taboo to the eater. Emotional impossibility.
    この料理りょうりは難むずかしい。 This dish is difficult [to make].
    If applied to eating, sounds odd — 難むずかしい describes complexity, not eating friction.

    In Real Life¶

    In Real Life

    Situation: ユキ is at an electronics store with her friend ハル. They're looking at smartphones. Yuki picks one up, swipes the screen, types a few characters, then sets it down and picks up another.

    > ユキ：「前まえのは画面がめんが小ちいさくて押おしにくかった。これは文字もじが大おおきくて読よみやすい。」

    Yui: "The previous one had a small screen and was hard to press. This one has large text and is easy to read."

    Why 〜やすい / 〜にくい fits here: Yuki is evaluating the devices as tools. She is not saying she is bad at typing (下手へた) or that the phone is complex (難むずかしい). She is describing the physical interaction: the buttons resist or yield. This is exactly the domain of stem-adjectives.

    What 押おしにくい captures: It means "hard to press" — maybe the buttons are too small, too stiff, or too close together. The difficulty is in the object's design.

    What 読よみやすい captures: It means "easy to read" — the font, contrast, and size make reading smooth. The ease is in the display's properties.

    What makes this natural: Japanese product reviews, cooking instructions, and UI feedback rely heavily on these forms. They are the standard grammar of tool evaluation.

    How It Works¶

    〜やすい and 〜にくい are suffixes that attach to the verb stem (the form used before ます). The result is a fully functional i-adjective that conjugates like 高たかい or 難むずかしい.

    Layer 1 — Verb stem: Remove the る from ichidan verbs, or shift godan verbs to their i-stem (the form before ます).

    Layer 2 — Suffix attachment: Add やすい or にくい directly to the stem.

    Layer 3 — Adjective grammar: The resulting word is an i-adjective. It can take です, drop the copula in casual speech, be adverbialized with く, and be nominalized with の or こと.

    Verb Type Dictionary Stem + やすい + にくい
    Godan く 書かく 書かき 書かきやすい 書かきにくい
    Godan ぐ 泳およぐ 泳およぎ 泳およぎやすい 泳およぎにくい
    Godan す 話はなす 話はなし 話はなしやすい 話はなしにくい
    Godan つ 待まつ 待まち 待まちやすい 待まちにくい
    Godan ぬ 死しぬ 死しに 死しにやすい 死しににくい
    Godan ぶ 遊あそぶ 遊あそび 遊あそびやすい 遊あそびにくい
    Godan む 読よむ 読よみ 読よみやすい 読よみにくい
    Godan る 帰かえる 帰かえり 帰かえりやすい 帰かえりにくい
    Ichidan 食たべる 食たべ 食たべやすい 食たべにくい
    Irregular する する し しやすい しにくい
    Irregular くる くる き 来きやすい 来きにくい

    する and くる are slightly irregular

    する → しやすい (not さやすい). くる → 来きやすい (not きやすい in the expected godan pattern — it follows the 来くる conjugation).

    Real Speech vs. Textbook Form¶

    Textbooks present these as straightforward adjective formation. In real speech, certain collocations are so common they function as independent vocabulary.

    Form Frequency Note
    分わかりやすい / 分わかりにくい Extremely high The canonical pair for clarity
    読よみやすい / 読よみにくい Very high Book and font evaluation
    飲のみやすい / 飲のみにくい High Food and beverage reviews
    使つかいやすい / 使つかいにくい Very high Tool and software evaluation
    聞ききやすい / 聞ききにくい High Voice, music, acoustics
    言いいやすい / 言いいにくい High Difficulty of expression
    生いきにくい Moderate "Hard to live" — social commentary
    信しんじにくい Moderate Skepticism
    ありにくい / なりにくい Low Rare; usually expressed other ways

    Grammar Analysis¶

    Function in Context¶

    • このペンは書かきやすい: このペンは marks the topic. 書かきやすい is the predicate i-adjective. No copula needed in casual speech; です added in polite.
    • 待まちにくかった: Past tense of 待まちにくい. As an i-adjective, past is formed with かった.
    • 飲のみやすくない: Negative of 飲のみやすい. The くない follows i-adjective rules.
    • 分わかりにくくて、困こまった: Te-form of the i-adjective (くて) connecting to a result. "It was hard to understand, so I was troubled."
    この本ほんは読よみやすくて、面白おもしろい。 This book is easy to read and interesting.
    The やすくて connects the ease of reading to the interest. Both are properties of the book.
    彼かれの話はなしは分わかりにくかった。 What he said was hard to understand.
    Past tense. The difficulty is attributed to his speech, not the listener's intelligence.

    Construction¶

    Form Result Example
    Present affirmative stem + やすい / にくい 書かきやすい
    Present negative stem + やすくない / にくくない 書かきやすくない
    Past affirmative stem + やすかった / にくかった 書かきやすかった
    Past negative stem + やすくなかった / にくくなかった 書かきやすくなかった
    Adverbial stem + やすく / にくく やすく書かける
    Nominalized stem + やすさ / にくさ 読よみやすさ

    The さ nominalization

    やすさ and にくさ turn the adjective into a noun: 読よみやすさ = "readability / ease of reading." This is common in product specs and design discussions.

    Japanese Mindset¶

    Japanese tool-evaluation grammar avoids blaming the user. When a manual is unclear, Japanese says 分わかりにくい (hard to understand) — a property of the text. English might say "This is confusing" or even "I don't get it," which locates the problem in the reader or in a vague atmosphere.

    This reflects a broader tendency to attribute friction to the interaction or the object rather than the person. 〜やすい and 〜にくい are the grammar of user experience before the concept existed — they evaluate how the world meets the body and mind.

    • Object-centered evaluation. 飲のみやすい evaluates the wine. 飲のみやすい人ひと would evaluate the drinker (unusual). The default is to describe the world, not the self.
    • Friction as inherent property. Difficulty is not a challenge to overcome (as in English "difficult but rewarding"); it is a property to note and possibly avoid. 食たべにくい is a warning, not a gauntlet.
    • The body in grammar. 持もちづらい (hard to hold) and 座すわりにくい (hard to sit in) embed physical experience into adjectives. Japanese grammar does not separate the observer from the observed as cleanly as English.

    Nuance Comparison¶

    Grammar Point Core Meaning Type of Resistance Target JLPT
    〜やすい Easy to do Low friction Action / object property N4
    〜にくい Hard to do General resistance (physical, logical, design) Action / object property N4
    〜づらい Hard to do Physical strain or social awkwardness Action / body position N3
    〜がたい Hard to do Emotional resistance; nearly impossible Action / moral boundary N3
    〜かねる Hesitate to do Position, propriety, inability to commit Social action N3
    難むずかしい Difficult Complex, high skill requirement Object / task N5
    簡単かんたんだ Simple Low complexity Object / task N5
    上手じょうずだ Skilled High personal ability Person N5
    下手へただ Unskilled Low personal ability Person N5

    〜にくい vs. 難むずかしい: この問題もんだいは解ときにくい = "This problem is hard to solve [the setup is awkward]." この問題もんだいは難むずかしい = "This problem is difficult [high intellectual demand]." The first is about solving friction; the second is about complexity.

    〜にくい vs. 〜づらい: 食たべにくい = "hard to eat [texture, size]." 食たべづらい = "awkward to eat [position, social context, physical discomfort]." The overlap is large, but づらい emphasizes the eater's discomfort more than the food's property.

    〜にくい vs. 〜がたい: 信しんじにくい = "hard to believe [the evidence is weak]." 信しんじがたい = "unthinkable to believe [emotional shock]." The first is skeptical; the second is devastated.

    Alternatives¶

    難むずかしい / 簡単かんたんだ (standalone adjectives)¶

    Objective complexity, not interaction friction.
    Example この数学すうがくの問題もんだいは難むずかしい。
    English "This math problem is difficult."
    Intent The problem requires high skill or knowledge.
    vs. 〜にくい 解ときにくい would mean the problem is awkwardly formulated, not necessarily advanced.

    上手じょうずだ / 下手へただ (personal skill)¶

    Ability of the person, not property of the object.
    Example 彼かれは泳およぐのが下手へただ。
    English "He's bad at swimming."
    Intent The person lacks skill.
    vs. 〜にくい 泳およぎにくい would mean the water/current makes swimming hard, not that the swimmer is unskilled.

    〜づらい (N3 — physical/social discomfort)¶
    Example この椅子いすは座すわりづらい。
    English "This chair is uncomfortable to sit in."
    Intent Physical or postural awkwardness.
    vs. 〜にくい 座すわりにくい focuses on the act of sitting being difficult (maybe too low). 座すわりづらい focuses on sustained discomfort (bad back support, social visibility).

    〜がたい (N3 — emotional impossibility)¶
    Example 彼かれの言いうことを信しんじがたい。
    English "I find what he says almost impossible to believe."
    Intent Emotional resistance. The facts may support it, but the heart rejects it.
    vs. 〜にくい 信しんじにくい is skeptical. 信しんじがたい is wounded or shocked.

    Register and Formality¶

    The forms are register-neutral. Politeness is carried by the copula and context.

    このビール、飲のみやすい。

    "This beer goes down easy."

    Shift: No copula. Direct property description. Common in casual reviews.

    このビールは飲のみやすいです。

    "This beer is easy to drink."

    Shift: です added. Standard polite evaluation.

    この商品しょうひんはお客様きゃくさまに飲のみやすくご用意よういしております。

    "We have prepared this product to be easy for our customers to drink."

    Shift: お客様きゃくさま (honorific), ご用意よういしております (humble). The 飲のみやすく is adverbialized to modify the preparation.

    Step Pattern What It Adds Example

    Examples¶

    このペンは書かきやすい。 This pen is easy to write with.
    Context: Evaluating a writing tool. The grip, weight, and flow make writing smooth.
    この薬くすりは飲のみにくい。 This medicine is hard to take [taste, size, texture].
    Context: Describing the medicine's physical properties. Not "I am bad at taking medicine."
    先生せんせいの説明せつめいはいつも分わかりやすい。 The teacher's explanations are always easy to understand.
    Context: Praising teaching clarity. One of the most common collocations in educational contexts.
    このフォントは読よみにくい。 This font is hard to read.
    Context: Design criticism. The font's properties impede reading.
    彼かれの話はなしは信しんじにくい。 What he says is hard to believe [the evidence is weak].
    Context: Skepticism. Not emotional rejection — just analytical doubt.
    彼かれの言いい分ぶんは信しんじがたい。 His claim is almost impossible to believe [emotional shock].
    Context: Deeper emotional resistance. Maybe betrayal or implausible excuse.
    この箱はこは持もちづらい。 This box is awkward to carry [shape, weight distribution].
    Context: Physical discomfort in carrying. The box may not be heavy, just unwieldy.
    その話はなしは言いいにくいんだけど… That's a hard thing to say, but…
    Context: Social difficulty in expressing something. The んだけど softens the admission.
    この仕事しごとはしやすい。 This work is easy to do.
    Context: する → しやすい. Describing workflow, environment, or task design.
    生いきにくい世よの中なかだ。 It's a world that's hard to live in.
    Context: Social commentary. 生いきにくい is a loaded term in Japanese discourse about society and mental health.

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Confusing 〜にくい with personal inability¶

    Attribution error

    私わたしは泳およぎにくい。

    ~This sounds like "I am hard to swim" — a property of your body, not your skill. For personal inability, use 泳およぐのが下手へただ or 泳およぎ方かたがわからない.~

    Correct

    このプールは泳およぎにくい。 (The pool is hard to swim in.) 私わたしは泳およぐのが下手へただ。 (I'm bad at swimming.)

    2. Using 難むずかしい where 〜にくい is more natural¶

    Register mismatch

    このボタンは押おすのが難むずかしい。

    ~難むずかしい describes complexity or high skill. A button is not intellectually demanding — it is physically resistant or poorly placed. Use 押おしにくい.~

    Correct

    このボタンは押おしにくい。

    3. Overusing 〜がたい for ordinary difficulty¶

    Emotional overkill

    この漢字かんじは読よみがたい。

    ~がたい carries emotional or moral weight. For ordinary reading difficulty, use 読よみにくい or 難むずかしい.~

    Correct

    この漢字かんじは読よみにくい。

    4. Wrong stem formation with godan verbs¶

    Stem error

    書かくやすい。

    ~Godan verbs require the i-stem (書かき), not the dictionary form.~

    Correct

    書かきやすい。

    Collocation Notes¶

    Collocation Meaning
    分わかりやすい Easy to understand — the gold standard of explanations
    分わかりにくい Hard to understand — common complaint
    読よみやすい Easy to read — font, handwriting, prose style
    読よみにくい Hard to read — poor design, messy writing
    飲のみやすい Easy to drink — smooth, mild flavor
    飲のみにくい Hard to drink — bitter, strong, awkward container
    使つかいやすい Easy to use — UI, tools, appliances
    使つかいにくい Hard to use — confusing design
    書かきやすい Easy to write with — pen, paper, interface
    書かきにくい Hard to write with — poor grip, laggy stylus

    Hidden Points¶

    • 分わかりやすい is often used as praise for people, not just documents. A teacher is 分わかりやすい. A presenter is 分わかりやすい. This extends the "object property" metaphor to people — their explanations have the quality of clarity.
    • 〜やすい can imply vulnerability. 病気びょうきになりやすい means "prone to illness" — easy for disease to take hold. 間違まちがえやすい means "prone to mistakes." The "ease" is not always positive.
    • 〜にくい can imply resilience positively. 腐くさりにくい means "resistant to rotting" — a good property for food. 壊こわれにくい means "durable." The "difficulty" can be a benefit.
    • The さ nominalization is common in tech and design. 読よみやすさ (readability), 使つかいやすさ (usability), 書かきやすさ (writability) are standard professional terms. They turn subjective experience into measurable properties.
    • 生いきにくい carries social weight. In discussions of mental health, social pressure, or economic hardship, 生いきにくい is the standard grammar for "hard to live [in this society]." It is stronger than 大変たいへん because it locates the difficulty in the structure of life itself.
    • 言いいにくい is the grammar of taboo. When a topic is embarrassing, socially risky, or emotionally loaded, Japanese speakers say 言いいにくいんだけど… before broaching it. The form functions as a social warning signal.
    • 〜やすい / 〜にくい can be used adverbially. やすく書かける ("can write easily") and にくく壊こわれる ("breaks with difficulty / is hard to break") are standard. The adverbial form modifies the verb that follows.

    Learner Stage¶

    Stage What It Looks Like
    Now — N4 Produce 〜やすい and 〜にくい correctly from verb stems; distinguish them from 難むずかしい / 簡単かんたんだ; use common collocations (分わかりやすい, 使つかいやすい) naturally; avoid attributing them to persons
    Developing — N4→N3 Deploy 〜づらい for physical/social discomfort; use 〜がたい for emotional resistance; choose between にくい and づらい based on type of friction; nominalize with 〜やすさ / にくさ
    Automatic — N3→N2 The difficulty spectrum becomes intuitive — you feel why 食たべにくい is about food design, 食たべづらい is about social context, and 食たべがたい is about moral revulsion. You no longer translate "difficult" but select the right Japanese friction type.
    Mastery You control the full ease/difficulty architecture: やすい (smooth), にくい (resistant), づらい (awkward), がたい (unthinkable), かねる (hesitant). You know when to praise with 分わかりやすい, when to warn with 壊こわれにくい, and when to mourn with 生いきにくい.
