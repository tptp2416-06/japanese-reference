    N4-Upper keigo respectful-language sonkeigo

    敬けい意いあるあ依い頼 — お/ご〜下ください¶

    ご覧らん下ください。 / お座すわり下ください。¶

    JLPT Levelレベル¶

    JLPT Levelレベル — Upper N4 / Lower N3

    The pattern itself is N4 — a core 尊敬語そんけいご (sonkeigoそんけいご / respectful language) structure. But the calibration of when to use it versus neutral 〜て下ください or directive 〜なさいなさい, and the wago/kango prefix distinction, push it into lower-N3 territory for active production. Service-industry fluency depends on this pattern.

    Tier Pattern Description Example
    N5 — Foundation 〜て下ください Neutral request — "please do X" 座すわって下ください。
    N4 — Foundation 〜なさいなさい Directive — "do X" (authority to subordinate) 座すわりなさいなさい。
    N4 — Core ← You are here お/ご〜下ください Respectful request — elevates the listener's action お座すわり下ください。
    N4 — Foundation お/ご〜するする Humble — lowers speaker's action お座すわりしますします。
    N3 — Extended お/ご〜いただけますか Humble request for listener's action お座すわりいただけますか。
    N2 — Awareness お/ご〜になられる Double keigo (often avoided) お座すわりになられる。

    Quickクイック Summaryサマリー¶

    At a glance

    • お/ご〜下ください is a 尊敬語そんけいご (sonkeigoそんけいご) pattern: it elevates the listener's action while politely requesting it. It does not mean "please" in a casual sense — it means "I humbly ask that you, who are above me, do this elevated action."
    • お attaches to 和語わご (wagoわご / native Japaneseじゃぱにーず) verb stems: 座すわる → お座すわり下ください, 休やすむ → お休やすみ下ください.
    • ご attaches to 漢語かんご (kangoかんご / Sinoしの-Japaneseじゃぱにーず) verb stems or nouns: 利用りようする → ご利用りよう下ください, 覧らんする → ご覧らん下ください.
    • This pattern is customer-facing, service-industry, and upward-directed. You use it when speaking to guests, clients, superiors, or anyone whose status you want to honor.
    • It is not the humble form. お座すわりしますします (I will sit — humble) and お座すわり下ください (Please sit — respectful) are opposite social directions. Confusing them is a major keigo error.

    Coreこあ Meaningいみ¶

    Core Rule

    Use お/ご〜下ください when you want to request that someone of higher status or someone you are serving perform an action, while grammatically elevating that action. The request is polite but structurally framed as an honor being granted to you by their action.

    お座すわり下ください。 Please be seated [respectful, to a guest or superior].
    Literalりたてらる: Humbleへんきん-prefix + sit-stem + please-do [respectful].
    Contextこんてくすと: Restaurantれすとらん host to customer. Doctorどくたー to patient. Receptionistれせぷしょにすと to visitor. The host elevates the act of sitting.
    ご覧らん下ください。 Please take a look [respectful, to a client or superior].
    Literalりたてらる: Humbleへんきん-prefix + see-stem + please-do [respectful].
    Contextこんてくすと: Shopしょっぷ staff showing merchandise. Presenterぷれぜんたー indicating a slide. The act of looking is elevated.
    ご確認かくにん下ください。 Please confirm [respectful, business correspondence].
    Literalりたてらる: Humbleへんきん-prefix + confirm-stem + please-do [respectful].
    Contextこんてくすと: Emailいーめーる to a client. The writer elevates the client's act of checking.
    お召めし上あがり下ください。 Please partake / Please eat [ultra-respectful, service].
    Literalりたてらる: Humbleへんきん-prefix + eat-stem + please-do [respectful].
    Contextこんてくすと: Highはい-end restaurantれすとらん. 召めし上あがる is the respectful verb for "eat"; adding お/ご〜下ください double-elevates.

    In Realりある Lifeらいふ¶

    In Real Life

    Situationしちゅえーしょん: ユミ works at the front desk of a hotel. A guest has just completed check-in. She gestures toward the elevator bank and speaks.

    > エレベーターはあちらでございます。どうぞ、お掛かけになりまして、お休やすみ下ください。

    "The elevator is over there. Please make yourself comfortable and rest."

    Why お休やすみ下ください fits here: Yumi could have said 休やすんで下ください — grammatically correct, neutrally polite. But that would treat the guest as a peer. お休やすみ下ください elevates the guest's rest into an honored action. The お marks the verb as wago; the 下ください frames it as a respectful request.

    What makes this natural: The pattern appears in the standard hospitality script. It is not a spontaneous sentence — it is a social ritual encoded in grammar. The guest expects this level of elevation; anything less feels like a downgrade.

    The どうぞ that precedes: どうぞ ("by all means") is a common partner for お/ご〜下ください. It adds warm invitation to the respectful structure. どうぞお座すわり下ください is a standard pairing.

    How It Worksわーくす¶

    The pattern is built from three layers: prefix + stem + request marker. Each layer carries social meaning.

    Layerれいやー 1 — Prefix (お vs ご): The prefix elevates the following word. お is for native Japaneseじゃぱにーず vocabulary; ご is for Sinoしの-Japaneseじゃぱにーず. This is not optional — using the wrong prefix is immediately recognizable as incorrect.

    Layerれいやー 2 — Stem: The verb is reduced to its stem form (the form used before ます). For する verbs, the noun/stem is used (利用りようする → 利用りよう). This stripped form is then "wrapped" in the honorific prefix.

    Layerれいやー 3 — 下ください: This is the imperative form of 下くださる, the respectful verb "to give (to me/someone below)." By using 下ください, the speaker frames the action as something the honored listener will "give" by performing it. The social geometry is: "You will do this for me, and I am grateful."

    座すわって下ください。 Please sit [neutral request].
    The listener is a peer or the situation is casual. No elevation.
    お座すわり下ください。 Please be seated [respectful request].
    The listener is a guest or superior. Their sitting is elevated.
    お座すわりしますします。 I will be seated [humble].
    The speaker lowers their own action. Social direction is reversed.

    Realりある Speechすぴーち vs. Textbookてくすとぶっく Formふぉーむ¶

    Textbooksてくすとぶっくす often present お/ご〜下ください as "please do X." In real speech, it is a service-industry default and an upward-deference marker. It is less common in peer conversation than in structured service contexts.

    Form Example Note
    Textbookてくすとぶっく (neutral) 窓まどを開あけて下ください。 "Please open the window" — classroom example
    Realりある speech (respectful) 窓まどをお開あけ下ください。 "Please open the window" — hotel staff to guest
    Realりある speech (humble request) 窓まどをお開あけいただけますか。 "Could you open the window for me?" — even more deferential
    Realりある speech (directive) 窓まどを開あけなさいなさい。 "Open the window" — parent to child

    Grammarぐらまー Analysisあなりしす¶

    Functionふんくしょん in Contextこんてくすと¶

    • お座すわり下ください: 座すわる (to sit) is wago, so it takes お. The stem is 座すわり. 下ください elevates the imperative.
    • ご覧らん下ください: 覧らんする (to view) is kango, so it takes ご. The stem/noun is 覧らん. 下ください elevates the imperative.
    • ご利用りよう下ください: 利用りようする (to use) is kango, so it takes ご. The stem/noun is 利用りよう.
    • お召めし上あがり下ください: 召めし上あがる (to eat — respectful verb) is wago, so it takes お. The stem is 召めし上あがり. This is already a respectful verb; adding お/ご〜下ください makes it ultra-respectful.
    • The subject of these sentences is always the listener (you), but it is never stated. The grammar of 尊敬語そんけいご implies "you, the honored person."
    • Without the prefix: 座すわり下ください is ungrammatical. The お/ご is required for the respectful frame.
    お掛かけ下ください。 Please sit down [hanging / seated position].
    Contextこんてくすと: 掛かける (to hang / to set) in お掛かけになる means "to seat oneself." お掛かけ下ください is a slightly more formal variant of お座すわり下ください in some service contexts.

    The wago/kango boundary

    Most errors involve applying お to kango or ご to wago. If the word can be written in pure hiragana or has native Japaneseじゃぱにーず etymology, it likely takes お. If it is a two-kanjiかんじ compound or clearly Sinoしの-Japaneseじゃぱにーず, it takes ご.

    Some words are lexicalized chunks

    ご覧らん, ご注意ちゅうい, お待まち are so common they should be learned as complete units rather than assembled from rules each time.

    Do not use お/ご〜下ください for your own actions

    This is 尊敬語そんけいご (respectful), not 謙譲語けんじょうご (humble). It points upward at the listener. Using it for yourself (お座すわり下ください when you are the one sitting) is a social-direction error.

    Japaneseじゃぱにーず Mindsetまいんどせっと¶

    Japaneseじゃぱにーず keigo is not "polite Japaneseじゃぱにーず" — it is a social positioning system. お/ご〜下ください does more than soften a command. It restructures the relationship between speaker and listener.

    When a hotel clerk says お座すわり下ください, they are not merely saying "Sit down." They are saying: "You are a guest of high standing, and your act of sitting is an honor. I am grateful that you will do it." The grammar encodes an entire service philosophy.

    • Elevation as hospitality. The prefix お/ご lifts the listener's action out of the everyday. Sitting becomes "being seated." Looking becomes "viewing." Eating becomes "partaking." The everyday is ceremonialized.
    • Request as gift. 下ください derives from 下くださる (to give from above). The listener is framed as giving the action to the speaker. Even when the listener is clearly following an instruction, the grammar treats them as bestowing a favor.
    • The wago/kango split as cultural taxonomy. The fact that native and Sinoしの-Japaneseじゃぱにーず vocabulary take different prefixes reveals how deeply Japaneseじゃぱにーず grammar encodes the origin of words. The particle system respects etymology.

    〜て下ください "Please do X" [neutral] Neutral request Polite, general Peers, general requests N5
    〜なさいなさい "Do X" [directive] Speakerすぴーかー directs listener Authority to subordinate Parentぱれんと to child, teacherてぃーちゃー to studentすとゅーでんと N4
    お/ご〜するする "I do X" [humble] Lowers speakerすぴーかー Humble Describing one's own actions to superiors N4
    お/ご〜いただけますか "Could you do X?" [humble request] Humble request for listener's action Ultra-polite Asking superiors for permission/action N3
    お/ご〜なさいなさい "Do X" [respectful directive] Elevated directive Formal, slightly old-fashioned Formal instruction, written N4
    〜ていただけますか "Could I receive X?" [humble] Receivingりしーびんぐ frame Polite Requesting someone do something for you N3

    お/ご〜下ください vs 〜て下ください: Both mean "please do." The difference is elevation. 座すわって下ください is a polite request to a peer. お座すわり下ください is a respectful request to a guest. Using 〜て下ください with a hotel guest sounds slightly brusque; using お/ご〜下ください with a close friend sounds oddly ceremonial.

    お/ご〜下ください vs お/ご〜するする: These are opposite directions. お座すわり下ください = "You, please sit" (respectful). お座すわりしますします = "I will sit" (humble). The prefix is the same; the social direction is reversed by the main verb (下ください vs するする).

    Alternativesおるたなてぃぶす¶

    〜て下ください (neutral request)¶

    The standard polite request. No elevation.
    Exampleいぐざんぷる 座すわって下ください。
    Englishいんぐりっしゅ "Please sit."
    Intentいんてんと Polite but neutral. The listener is a peer or the context is casual.
    vs. お/ご〜下ください Removes the ceremonial elevation. Appropriate for colleagues, friends, or everyday service.

    When to use this instead

    Use 〜て下ください when speaking to peers, subordinates in casual contexts, or when the situation does not require service-industry formality.

    〜なさいなさい (directive)¶

    Authority speaking downward. Not polite in the deferential sense — it is a command wrapped in grammar.
    Exampleいぐざんぷる 座すわりなさいなさい。
    Englishいんぐりっしゅ "Sit down."
    Intentいんてんと Directive. The speakerすぴーかー has authority.
    vs. お/ご〜下ください なさいなさい commands; お/ご〜下ください requests. Never use なさいなさい with guests or superiors.

    お/ご〜いただけますか (humble request)¶

    Asks the listener to "give" the action in an even more humble frame.
    Exampleいぐざんぷる お座すわりいただけますか。
    Englishいんぐりっしゅ "Could you please be seated?"
    Intentいんてんと The speakerすぴーかー positions themselves as receiving the listener's action. More humble than お座すわり下ください.
    vs. お/ご〜下ください いただけますか adds a receivingりしーびんぐ frame (謙譲語けんじょうご layer on top of 尊敬語そんけいご). Appropriate for very high-status listeners or formal written requests.

    お/ご〜なさいなさい (respectful directive)¶

    A middle ground between request and directive. Slightly old-fashioned.
    Exampleいぐざんぷる お休やすみなさいなさい。
    Englishいんぐりっしゅ "Do rest." / "Good night."
    Intentいんてんと Elevated directive. Common in set phrases (お休やすみなさいなさい = good night). Less common for spontaneous requests.
    vs. お/ご〜下ください なさいなさい is more decisive; 下ください is more inviting.

    Registerれじすたー and Formalityふぉーまりてぃ¶

    The respectful request is formal by definition, but its warmth can be modulated.

    お座すわり下ください。

    "Please be seated."

    Shift: Used with a close friend. Sounds mock-formal or sarcastic unless the context is genuinely ceremonial.

    どうぞ、お座すわり下ください。

    "Please, be seated."

    Shift: Standardすたんだーど service-industry warmth. The どうぞ adds invitation; the お座すわり下ください provides the elevation.

    ご確認かくにんのほど、よろしくお願ねがいいたします。

    "We kindly ask that you please confirm."

    Shift: ご確認かくにん + のほど (formal periphrasis) + よろしくお願ねがいいたします (humble closing). The core pattern ご確認かくにん下ください is embedded in a more elaborate formal structure.

    どうぞ、お召めし上あがりくださいませ。

    "Please, do partake."

    Shift: 下ください + ませ adds historical/formal flourish. Found in luxury ryokan, traditional restaurants, and theatrical speech.

    2. Confusing 尊敬語そんけいご (respectful) with 謙譲語けんじょうご (humble)¶
