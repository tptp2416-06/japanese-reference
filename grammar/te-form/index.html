    Casual N3 Te-form

    て-form¶

    The te-form is one of the most productive forms in Japanese — it connects clauses, makes requests, signals permission and prohibition, and stacks with dozens of auxiliaries.

    Cards¶

    Claude Sonnet 4.6 · Sample Card

    待まってくれてありがとう

    【Natural Upgrade (2026 / 30s–40s)】

    • ✓ Already Natural

      • Warm, direct gratitude for someone waiting. Natural in casual and semi-polite contexts.

      • The くれて form acknowledges that the waiting was done for the speaker's benefit — a key pragmatic layer.

      ⇒ Nothing to change.

    【Translation】

    • Natural English: Thanks for waiting.
    • Literal English: Having-waited for-me, thank you.
    • The Gap:

      • English "thanks for waiting" is neutral. Japanese 待まってくれて encodes that the wait was done as a favor to me — the speaker is acknowledging a social debt.

      • The te-form chain (って + くれて) packages two grammatical moves — punctual completion + benefactive — into one smooth unit that English collapses into a single word.

    【Grammar Stacking】

    • Chain 1 · Benefactive Gratitude Stack: 待まってくれてありがとう
      ① 待まつ ➝ Base verb "to wait." ⟦Godan · intransitive⟧

      • The actor doing the waiting is the listener, not the speaker.

      ② 待まって ➝ Te-form connector. ⟦te-form⟧

      • Links the waiting action to the next element — here, the benefactive auxiliary.

      ③ 待まってくれて ➝ Benefactive: the listener did the waiting for the speaker. ⟦te-form + くれる benefactive⟧

      • くれる marks an inward favor — something done for me or my in-group.

      • Without くれて, ありがとう would still work but loses the explicit acknowledgment of social debt.

      ④ ありがとう ➝ Gratitude expression. ⟦set phrase⟧

      • The whole te-form chain before it becomes the reason for gratitude.

      📌 Compare: 待まっててくれてありがとう — adding ている (→ てて) shifts from punctual to progressive: "thanks for having kept waiting." Acknowledges sustained, accumulated waiting rather than a single wait.
      Full Assembly
      Logical order: [Listener waited (for me)] → [thank you for that]
      Natural: Thanks for waiting.
      Literal: Having-waited-for-me, thank you.

    Anchor Chunk

    待まってくれてありがとう is a high-frequency anchor chunk worth active recall training. The くれて benefactive pattern recurs across dozens of gratitude expressions.
