    Unattended Continuation — 〜っぱなし¶

    Reference Sentence: 〜っぱなし — "Left Doing" / "Left in the State of Doing"¶

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • 〜っぱなし attaches to the masu-stem of a verb and means "to leave something in an ongoing state" — the action continues (or its result persists) without the attention, intervention, or closure that would normally be expected.
    • It carries a strong nuance of neglect, forgetfulness, or failure to complete the proper cycle of an action. Something is on, open, running, or undone when it should have been stopped, closed, or finished.
    • In the reference sentence — テレビが付つけっぱなしです — the TV has been left on (it is in the "turned-on" state, and someone should have turned it off but didn't).
    • っぱなし is distinct from 〜たまま, which neutrally describes a state persisting, and from 〜ている, which simply states an ongoing action or resultant state without the judgment of neglect.

    Core Meaning — "Left in an Inappropriate State"¶

    Core Rule

    Use 〜っぱなし when you want to say that an action or its result continues without being properly closed or attended to — usually because someone forgot, neglected, or failed to complete the expected follow-up.

    テレビが付つけっぱなしです。 The TV has been left on.
    Literal: TV turned-on-left-alone is.
    Someone should have turned it off; the state of being on is continuing unattended.

    The Reference Sentence — Full Breakdown¶

    The Sentence¶

    テレビが付つけっぱなしです。

    Natural Translation¶

    "The TV has been left on." / "The TV is still on (and shouldn't be)."

    Component Breakdown¶

    Component Form Function
    テレビ Noun Television
    が Subject particle Marks the TV as the thing in the state
    付つけ Masu-stem of 付つける (to turn on) The base to which っぱなし attaches
    っぱなし Suffix "Left in the state of doing" — adds neglect nuance
    です Polite copula Makes the sentence polite

    Why Not 〜ている or 〜たまま?¶

    Form Sentence Nuance
    〜ている テレビが付ついている。 "The TV is on." (neutral state; no judgment)
    〜たまま テレビが付つけたままだ。 "The TV is on (and was left that way)." (persistent state; can be neutral or neglectful depending on context)
    〜っぱなし テレビが付つけっぱなしだ。 "The TV is left on (and someone should have turned it off)." (strong neglect nuance)

    How 〜っぱなし Works¶

    The Grammar¶

    Verb (masu-stem) + っぱなし

    Remove ます from the polite form to get the stem, then attach っぱなし directly. っぱなし functions as a noun (it can be followed by だ/です). It comes from the verb 放はなす (はなす, to let go / to release), with the same root as 放はなし飼がい (free-range) — the idea is that the action has been "released" and left to run.

    Verb Masu-stem + っぱなし Meaning
    付つける (to turn on) 付つけ 付つけっぱなし Left on
    開あける (to open) 開あけ 開あけっぱなし Left open
    出だす (to take out) 出だし 出だしっぱなし Left out (not put away)
    書かく (to write) 書かき 書かきっぱなし Left half-written; left as-is without finishing
    言いう (to say) 言いい 言いいっぱなし Left as just words; said and then abandoned without follow-up
    立たつ (to stand) 立たち 立たちっぱなし Left standing (without sitting)
    座すわる (to sit) 座すわり 座すわりっぱなし Left sitting (without moving)

    Only attaches to the masu-stem

    You cannot attach っぱなし to the dictionary form, te-form, or any other conjugation. Only the masu-stem (the part before ます) is used.

    The Neglect Nuance — The Core of っぱなし¶

    っぱなし always implies that something should have been done but wasn't — the state is not just continuing; it's continuing inappropriately. This is what distinguishes it from neutral state descriptions.

    Sentence Nuance
    窓まどが開あいている。 The window is open. (No judgment — maybe it's open for fresh air.)
    窓まどが開あけっぱなしだ。 The window has been left open. (Someone should have closed it — it's cold, it's insecure, etc.)
    扇風機せんぷうきが回まわっている。 The fan is running. (Neutral.)
    扇風機せんぷうきが付つけっぱなしだ。 The fan has been left on. (Someone forgot to turn it off; waste of electricity.)

    The speaker using っぱなし is criticising the situation, even if mildly. It's not just observation — it's observation with a "this shouldn't be the case" judgment.

    〜っぱなし vs. 〜たまま — A Critical Distinction¶

    Both describe a state continuing, but the nuance differs sharply.

    〜っぱなし 〜たまま
    Nuance Neglect, oversight, inappropriateness — the state should have been resolved. Persistence — the state simply hasn't changed yet; neutral or context-dependent.
    Judgment Inherently critical or regretful. Can be neutral, practical, or sometimes critical if context adds it.
    Typical use Complaints, noticing forgotten things. Describing posture, position, or unchanged situation.
    Example 電気でんきが付つけっぱなしだ。 "The light has been left on." (Wasteful; someone forgot.) 電気でんきが付ついたままだ。 "The light is still on." (Just stating; maybe it's intentional.)
    Example 靴くつを脱ぬぎっぱなしだ。 "The shoes have been left lying around." (Tidy them up!) 靴くつを脱ぬいだままだ。 "The shoes are still off." (Maybe the person is still inside; no criticism implied.)

    Rule of thumb

    If you're annoyed that something hasn't been put right, use っぱなし. If you're simply describing that a state hasn't changed, use まま.

    Extended Examples — っぱなし in Real Life¶

    Household Forgetfulness¶

    Japanese English
    冷蔵庫れいぞうこが開あけっぱなしだよ。 The fridge has been left open.
    Someone didn't close it properly; food will spoil.
    部屋へやの電気でんきが付つけっぱなしだった。 The room light had been left on.
    I found it on when it shouldn't have been.
    水みずを出だしっぱなしにしないで。 Don't leave the water running.
    Turn it off when you're done.
    お菓子かしを食たべっぱなしで片付かたづけてない。 You left the snacks out after eating — didn't tidy up.
    The eating is done; the remains are still there.

    Undone Actions / Half-Finished States¶

    Japanese English
    書類しょるいを書かきっぱなしで提出ていしゅつしていない。 I left the documents half-written and haven't submitted them.
    The writing was interrupted; the task is incomplete.
    彼かれは言いいっぱなしで行動こうどうしない。 He just talks and never follows through.
    The words are "left out there" without action.
    仕事しごとをやりっぱなしにして帰かえった。 He left work half-done and went home.
    Tasks were started but not finished or put away.

    Personal State — Standing, Sitting, Wearing¶

    Japanese English
    電車でんしゃで一時間いちじかんも立たちっぱなしだった。 I was left standing on the train for a whole hour.
    No seat; standing continued without relief.
    一日中いちにちじゅう座すわりっぱなしで腰こしが痛いたい。 I've been sitting all day and my back hurts.
    The sitting continued unbroken, to a negative degree.
    靴くつを履はきっぱなしで家いえに上あがらないで。 Don't come into the house with your shoes still on.
    The shoes should have been removed at the entrance.

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Using っぱなし for neutral or positive continuation¶

    っぱなし is inherently negative — it implies neglect or something left undone. Using it for a neutral or intentional state sounds accusatory or odd.

    Incorrect nuance

    音楽おんがくをかけっぱなしで勉強べんきょうするのが好すきです。

    ~"I like studying with the music left on." Sounds like you're complaining about the music being on, not enjoying it.~

    More natural

    音楽おんがくをかけたまま勉強べんきょうするのが好すきです。

    ~"I like studying with the music playing." まま is neutral; no neglect nuance.~

    2. Attaching っぱなし to the wrong form¶

    っぱなし only attaches to the masu-stem. Using the dictionary form, te-form, or past tense is incorrect.

    Incorrect

    付つけるっぱなし ❌ 付つけったっぱなし ❌

    Correct

    付つけっぱなし ✅ (masu-stem of 付つける)

    3. Confusing っぱなし with 〜たまま for all contexts¶

    Though they overlap, っぱなし adds judgment. Using it when simply describing a persistent state without criticism can make the speaker sound unfairly harsh.

    Overly critical

    彼かれは若わかいままだ。 → 彼かれは若わかいっぱなしだ ❌

    ~"He remains young." っぱなし with i-adjectives isn't standard; "left young" makes no sense. まま is correct.~

    Correct

    彼かれは若わかいままだ。

    Quick Reference Table¶

    Pattern Meaning Example
    Masu-stem + っぱなし Left doing / left in a state (neglect) 付つけっぱなし "left on"
    Masu-stem + っぱなし + だ／です Polite/Negative statement about the state 付つけっぱなしです
    〜っぱなしにする To leave something in that state (deliberate or causal) 開あけっぱなしにする "leave it open"
    〜っぱなしにしないで Don't leave it in that state 出だしっぱなしにしないで "Don't leave it out"

    See Also¶

    Similar Patterns¶

    • 〜たまま — Persistent state, neutral. "The window is still open (as it was)." No neglect nuance.
    • 〜ている — Ongoing action or resultant state. "The TV is on." Pure state description.
    • 〜ずにいる — "To remain without doing." Negative action remaining undone, but without the "left" judgment.
    • 〜放題ほうだい（ほうだい） — "All you can / as much as you like." Different nuance — freedom, not neglect. 食たべ放題ほうだい, 言いいたい放題ほうだい.

    Contrast With¶

    • 〜っぱなし vs. 〜たまま — Neglect vs. neutral persistence. See the comparison section above.
