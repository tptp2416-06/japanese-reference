    〜ちゃいなよ¶

    〜ちゃいなよ — “Go ahead and do it already / just do it”¶

    JLPT Level¶

    JLPT Level — Lower N3

    The pieces are familiar earlier — てしまう, casual contraction ちゃう, and なよ — but the combined spoken pattern 〜ちゃいなよ is best treated as Lower N3 because its real difficulty is tone: friendly, pushy, caring, teasing, or mildly impatient depending on context.

    Tier Pattern Description Example
    N4 — Foundation 〜てしまう Completion / “end up doing” 食たべてしまう。"I eat it all / end up eating it."
    N4 — Spoken 〜ちゃう Casual contraction of 〜てしまう 食たべちゃう。"I’ll eat it / I end up eating it."
    N3 — Core ← You are here 〜ちゃいなよ Friendly push: “go ahead and do it already” 先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。"Go ahead and get in first."
    N3 — Extended 〜ちゃえば？ / 〜ちゃったら？ Softer suggestion variants 先さきに入はいっちゃえば？"Why don’t you just go in first?"

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • 〜ちゃいなよ is a casual spoken pattern meaning “go ahead and do it,” “just do it already,” or “you might as well do it.”
    • It comes from 〜てしまう → 〜ちゃう, but here the main nuance is usually completion / moving forward, not regret.
    • It often sounds caring or encouraging when used with close people.
    • It can sound pushy if used with strangers, superiors, or people you are not close to.
    • In the example sentence, 入っちゃいなよ means “go ahead and get in already” — the speaker is warmly encouraging the listener to take the bath first.

    Core Meaning¶

    Core Rule

    Attach 〜ちゃいなよ to a verb to casually encourage someone to go ahead and do the action, usually because the speaker thinks it is fine, convenient, or better to get it done now.

    先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。 Go ahead and get in first.
    Literal: Go ahead and enter first already.

    ~Context: Friendly encouragement. The speaker is not ordering harshly; they are making the action feel easy and acceptable.~

    お風呂ふろ準備じゅんびできてるから、もう先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。 The bath is ready, so go ahead and get in first.
    Literal: Since the bath preparation is done, already go ahead and enter first.

    ~Context: The input sentence. The speaker has prepared the bath and is gently pushing the listener to use it first. The tone is close, casual, and considerate.~

    In Real Life¶

    In Real Life

    Situation: One person is staying over. The host has already prepared the bath and wants the guest or partner to relax first.

    お風呂ふろ準備じゅんびできてるから、もう先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。

    "The bath is ready, so go ahead and get in first."

    Why this form fits here: 入っちゃいなよ makes the suggestion feel casual and warm. The speaker is not just saying "you may enter" or "please enter"; they are lightly nudging the listener to stop hesitating and take the bath.

    What makes this natural: もう adds “at this point / already,” 先に sets up “before me / first,” and ちゃいなよ makes the action feel like something the listener can simply go ahead and do. This is close-relationship speech, not polite service speech.

    How It Works¶

    The pattern is built from three layers:

    てしまう¶

    • completion
    • “do it fully”
    • sometimes regret, but not always

    ちゃう¶

    • casual contraction of てしまう
    • very common in spoken Japanese

    ちゃいなよ¶

    • casual encouraging command / suggestion
    • “go ahead and do it”
    • often used when the speaker thinks the listener is hesitating unnecessarily
    Layer Form Function
    Base verb 入はいる enter / get in
    て-form 入はいって connective form
    Contracted てしまう 入はいっちゃう go ahead and get in / end up getting in
    Suggestive push 入はいっちゃいなよ go ahead and get in already

    Why It Does Not Mean Regret Here¶

    In textbooks, てしまう is often taught as “unfortunately did X.” That is only one use. In casual speech, ちゃう often means the action is completed, done decisively, or moved forward.

    Sentence Natural Meaning Regret?
    先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。 Go ahead and get in first. No
    全部ぜんぶ食たべちゃった。 I ate it all. Maybe
    財布さいふ忘わすれちゃった。 I forgot my wallet. Yes

    Key point

    ちゃう does not automatically mean regret. It often means completion, decisiveness, or “it happened / got done.” The emotional meaning comes from the verb and situation.

    Grammar Analysis¶

    Function in Context¶

    • お風呂準備できてるから gives the reason: the bath is already ready.
    • もう adds a light “now / already / at this point” feeling.
    • 先に means “before me / first.”
    • 入っちゃいなよ is the friendly push: “go ahead and get in.”
    • よ adds warmth and makes the suggestion feel directed at the listener.
    Segment Role
    お風呂ふろ準備じゅんびできてるから reason / setup
    もう “already / now”
    先さきに first / before me
    入はいっちゃいなよ go ahead and get in

    Construction¶

    Base Verb て-form ちゃう Form ちゃいなよ Form
    入はいる 入はいって 入はいっちゃう 入はいっちゃいなよ
    食たべる 食たべて 食たべちゃう 食たべちゃいなよ
    飲のむ 飲のんで 飲のんじゃう 飲のんじゃいなよ
    帰かえる 帰かえって 帰かえっちゃう 帰かえっちゃいなよ
    見みる 見みて 見みちゃう 見みちゃいなよ
    する して しちゃう しちゃいなよ
    来くる 来きて 来きちゃう 来きちゃいなよ

    ちゃう vs じゃう

    If the て-form ends in で, the contraction becomes じゃう, not ちゃう.
    飲のんで → 飲のんじゃう → 飲のんじゃいなよ.

    What Is 〜いなよ?¶

    The いなよ part comes from a casual command-like pattern related to なさい, but it is much softer and more colloquial than a direct command in many friendly contexts.

    Form Feeling
    入はいって simple request / command
    入はいりなさい parent / teacher command
    入はいりなよ casual suggestion / light command
    入はいっちゃいなよ go ahead and get in already; friendly push

    Not for everyone

    〜ちゃいなよ assumes closeness. It is natural with friends, partners, family, or someone you are caring for casually. It can sound too casual or bossy with strangers, seniors, customers, or teachers.

    Japanese Mindset¶

    English often separates permission, advice, and command very clearly: “You can go in,” “You should go in,” “Go in.” Japanese often uses softer spoken packaging to place the action inside the relationship. 〜ちゃいなよ does not simply tell someone what to do — it says, “It’s okay, the situation is ready, so just go ahead.”

    The key is that the speaker is reducing hesitation. In the bath sentence, the listener may be waiting politely, unsure whether to go first, or not wanting to impose. 入っちゃいなよ removes that hesitation and makes the action socially easy.

    • Social easing: The pattern helps the listener move forward without feeling selfish.
    • Completion framing: ちゃう packages the action as something to just get done.
    • Relationship signal: The speaker sounds close enough to nudge the listener casually.
    • Care through pushiness: The form can feel caring because the speaker is actively clearing the listener’s hesitation.

    Nuance Comparison¶

    Pattern Core Meaning Emotional / Pragmatic Nuance Example JLPT
    〜て Do X Plain request or command depending on tone 先さきに入はいって。"Go in first." N5
    〜ていいよ You may do X Permission; softer and less pushy 先さきに入はいっていいよ。"You can go in first." N5
    〜なよ Do X / you should X Casual suggestion; can sound slightly directive 先さきに入はいりなよ。"You should go in first." N4
    〜ちゃいなよ Go ahead and do X Friendly push toward completion; “just do it already” 先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。"Go ahead and get in first." N3
    〜ちゃえば？ Why not just do X? Suggestion framed as an option 先さきに入はいっちゃえば？"Why don’t you just go in first?" N3
    〜てしまいなさい Go ahead and do X Formal/written or parent-like full form; stronger 先さきに入はいってしまいなさい。"Go ahead and get in first." N3

    Micro Contrast¶

    Sentence Natural English Feeling
    先さきに入はいっていいよ。 You can go in first. permission
    先さきに入はいりなよ。 You should go in first. suggestion / light command
    先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。 Go ahead and get in first already. friendly push / completion
    先さきに入はいっちゃえば？ Why don’t you just go in first? softer option

    Alternatives¶

    〜ていいよ¶

    A softer permission-based alternative. Use this when you want to avoid sounding pushy.
    Example もう先さきに入はいっていいよ。
    English "You can go in first now."
    Intent Permission, not a push
    vs. original Softer but less warm/active. It allows the action but does not strongly encourage it.

    〜なよ¶

    A direct casual suggestion. It keeps the friendly tone but loses the “go ahead and finish/do it” nuance from ちゃう.
    Example もう先さきに入はいりなよ。
    English "Go in first already."
    Intent Casual suggestion / light command
    vs. original More direct. Less sense of “the situation is ready, so just go ahead.”

    〜ちゃえば？¶

    A softer, option-like version. It sounds more like “why don’t you just…?”
    Example もう先さきに入はいっちゃえば？
    English "Why don’t you just go in first?"
    Intent Suggestion framed as an option
    vs. original Softer and less directive. Good when you do not want to sound like you are pushing.

    Choosing between them

    Use 〜ていいよ when giving permission, 〜なよ when casually recommending, 〜ちゃいなよ when warmly pushing someone to just go ahead and do it.

    Register and Formality¶

    お風呂ふろ準備じゅんびできてるから、もう先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。

    "The bath is ready, so go ahead and get in first."

    Shift: Input sentence as-is. Natural close-relationship speech. Friendly, lightly pushy, caring.

    お風呂ふろの準備じゅんびができていますので、よかったら先さきに入はいってください。

    "The bath is ready, so please feel free to go in first if you'd like."

    Shift: 〜ちゃいなよ is removed because it is too casual. よかったら and ください preserve the considerate intent without the casual push.

    お風呂ふろの準備じゅんびができておりますので、よろしければお先さきにお入はいりください。

    "The bath is ready, so please go ahead and use it first if you would like."

    Shift: More formal and hosted. Natural in a polite household, lodging, or service-like context. The warmth becomes courtesy rather than intimacy.

    No true humble equivalent

    〜ちゃいなよ is directed at someone else, so there is no direct humble version. Humble speech describes your own actions toward someone above you; here, the speaker is inviting the listener to act.

    Grammar Lineage¶

    Step Pattern What It Adds Example
    N5 て-form Connects verbs; basis for requests 入はいって
    N4 〜てしまう Completion / regret / finality 入はいってしまう
    N4 Spoken 〜ちゃう Casual contraction of 〜てしまう 入はいっちゃう
    N3 — Core ← You are here 〜ちゃいなよ Friendly push to go ahead and do it 入はいっちゃいなよ
    N3 Extended 〜ちゃえば？ Softer suggestion / option 入はいっちゃえば？
    N2 Awareness 〜てしまいなさい Full, formalized version; stronger 入はいってしまいなさい

    Examples¶

    冷さめちゃうから、先さきに食たべちゃいなよ。 It’ll get cold, so go ahead and eat first.
    Literal: Because it will end up getting cold, go ahead and eat first.

    ~Context: Close friend or partner. Caring push. The speaker is removing hesitation.~

    疲つかれてるなら、もう寝ねちゃいなよ。 If you're tired, just go to bed already.
    Literal: If you're tired, go ahead and sleep already.

    ~Context: Caring but slightly directive. Natural with someone close.~

    気きになるなら、買かっちゃいなよ。 If you want it, just buy it already.
    Literal: If it bothers/interests you, go ahead and buy it.

    ~Context: Friend encouraging someone who keeps hesitating. Light, casual push.~

    時間じかんあるうちに、先さきにやっちゃいなよ。 While you still have time, go ahead and get it done first.
    Literal: While there is time, go ahead and do it first.

    ~Context: Practical advice. Completion nuance is strong.~

    読よみたいなら、もう読よんじゃいなよ。 If you want to read it, just go ahead and read it.
    Literal: If you want to read it, go ahead and read it already.

    ~Context: Friendly encouragement. んじゃいなよ appears because 読む → 読んで → 読んじゃう.~

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Treating ちゃう as always regret¶

    Incorrect assumption

    入はいっちゃいなよ = "Unfortunately go in."

    ~This is not how the sentence works. Here ちゃう expresses completion / going ahead, not regret.~

    Correct understanding

    入はいっちゃいなよ = "Go ahead and get in."

    ~The speaker is encouraging action, not expressing regret.~

    2. Using it with people you are not close to¶

    Too casual

    先生せんせい、もう先さきに入はいっちゃいなよ。

    ~Too casual and too directive toward a teacher. It sounds socially inappropriate unless the relationship is extremely unusual.~

    Safer polite version

    先生せんせい、よろしければお先さきにお入はいりください。

    ~Polite and respectful. The invitation remains, but the casual push disappears.~

    3. Forgetting じゃう after で-ending て-forms¶

    Incorrect

    飲のんちゃいなよ。

    ~飲む → 飲んで, so the contraction is じゃう, not ちゃう.~

    Correct

    飲のんじゃいなよ。

    ~で → じゃう. 読む also works this way: 読んで → 読んじゃう → 読んじゃいなよ.~

    Collocation Notes¶

    Common Chunk Meaning Note
    食たべちゃいなよ Go ahead and eat it Caring / practical
    寝ねちゃいなよ Just go to bed Caring, sometimes slightly parental
    買かっちゃいなよ Just buy it already Encouraging someone hesitating
    やっちゃいなよ Just get it done Common, practical, direct
    読よんじゃいなよ Just read it already で → じゃう contraction
    使つかっちゃいなよ Go ahead and use it Permission + encouragement

    ~These are best learned as spoken chunks. The social meaning comes from the whole phrase, not just the grammar pieces.~

    Hidden Points¶

    • ちゃいなよ contains a mild push. It is not neutral permission. The speaker is actively encouraging the listener to stop waiting, stop worrying, or just move forward.
    • It often carries care. With close people, this pattern can sound warm because the speaker is removing the listener’s hesitation.
    • It can sound parental. 寝ちゃいなよ or 食べちゃいなよ can sound like someone gently taking care of another person.
    • The regret meaning of ちゃう is not automatic. In this pattern, completion and decisiveness are usually more important than regret.
    • The relationship matters. The same phrase can sound affectionate with a partner, friendly with a friend, and rude with a stranger.
    • よ is doing social work. It makes the suggestion feel addressed and supportive rather than cold or abrupt.

    Coming Soon¶

    〜ちゃえば？

    A softer suggestion pattern meaning “why don’t you just…?” It is often less pushy than 〜ちゃいなよ and useful when you want to make the action feel like the listener’s choice.

    〜ちゃった

    A very high-frequency spoken contraction of 〜てしまった. It can mean regret, completion, accident, or emotional confession depending on context.

    〜なよ

    A casual suggestion / light command pattern. It is simpler than 〜ちゃいなよ but can sound more direct because it lacks the “go ahead and finish/do it” nuance from ちゃう.

    Learner Stage¶

    Stage What It Looks Like
    Now — Lower N3 You can recognize 〜ちゃいなよ as a casual spoken suggestion and understand that it usually means “go ahead and do it,” not regret
    Developing — N3 You can choose between 〜ていいよ, 〜なよ, 〜ちゃいなよ, and 〜ちゃえば？ based on how pushy or soft you want to sound
    Automatic — N3→N2 You feel the social temperature instantly: caring, teasing, pushy, parental, or inappropriate depending on relationship
    Mastery You can use 〜ちゃいなよ naturally to reduce hesitation without sounding bossy, and you can avoid it when the relationship does not support that casual push
