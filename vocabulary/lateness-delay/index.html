    Kimi2.6 N3-Mid delay lateness verb-distinction

    Lateness & Delay — Four Ways to Be Late¶

    遅おくれる・遅おそくなる・遅刻ちこくする・遅延ちえんする¶

    JLPT Level¶

    JLPT Level — Mid N3

    The individual verbs are N4–N5 vocabulary, but distinguishing them pragmatically — knowing which to use for personal tardiness, schedule shifts, formal appointments, or systemic delays — is solidly N3. Native speakers never confuse these; learners often reach for the wrong one and reveal non-native intuition.

    Tier Pattern Description Example
    N5 — Foundation 遅おそい (i-adjective) "Late" — descriptive state 電車でんしゃが遅おそい。"The train is late."
    N4 — Foundation 遅おくれる Intransitive verb — "to be delayed / to run late" 寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれた。"I overslept and was late."
    N4 — Foundation 遅おそくなる "Become late" — state change, often scheduled 会議かいぎが遅おそくなる。"The meeting will run late."
    N3 — Core ← You are here 遅刻ちこくする vs. 遅延ちえんする Formal lateness (personal) vs. systemic delay (organizational) 遅刻ちこくしました。"I was tardy." / 遅延ちえんしています。"There is a delay."
    N2 — Awareness 延のびる / 伸のびる "To extend / stretch" — duration expansion 会議かいぎが延のびた。"The meeting ran over."

    Quick Summary¶

    At a glance

    • 遅おくれる — Personal, intransitive, often fault-implied. You missed the mark. "I was delayed / I ran late."
    • 遅おそくなる — Scheduled or predicted shift. The time changes, not necessarily anyone's fault. "It will be late / run late."
    • 遅刻ちこくする — Formal, institutional, personal tardiness. Used for appointments, school, work. "To be tardy / arrive late."
    • 遅延ちえんする — Systemic, organizational, mechanical delay. Trains, flights, projects. "To be delayed [system-wide]."
    • The key distinction: 遅おくれる is about failing to arrive on time; 遅おそくなる is about a schedule shifting later; 遅刻ちこくする is the formal institutional word for personal tardiness; 遅延ちえんする is the formal word for systemic delay.

    Core Meaning¶

    Core Rule

    Choose your lateness verb based on agency (whose fault?), formality (institutional or personal?), and system scope (individual or organizational?).

    寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれた。 I overslept and was late [personal, my fault].
    Literal: Overslept did [te-form] was-delayed.
    Context: Personal explanation. 遅おくれる is intransitive — the lateness happened to me, but the oversleeping implies my agency. Common for daily life, friends, informal workplace.
    会議かいぎが遅おそくなる。 The meeting will run late [schedule shift].
    Literal: Meeting late become.
    Context: Announcing a schedule change. No fault implied — the meeting simply extends. Used for plans, appointments, events that shift later.
    電車でんしゃに遅刻ちこくした。 I was late for the train [formal, institutional].
    Literal: Train-to tardiness did.
    Context: Formal apology or report. 遅刻ちこくする is the institutional word for failing to arrive by a designated time. School, work, appointments.
    飛行機ひこうきが遅延ちえんしています。 The flight is delayed [systemic, organizational].
    Literal: Flight delay doing [polite progressive].
    Context: Airport announcement. 遅延ちえんする is never personal — it describes mechanical, operational, or systemic delays.

    The Four-Way Distinction¶

    1. 遅おくれる — Personal Delay (Intransitive)¶

    Attribute Value
    Transitivity Intransitive (自動じどう詞し)
    Agency Often implied fault, but grammatically the subject is affected
    Formality Casual to standard
    Scope Individual
    Collocations 電車でんしゃに遅おくれる, 寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれる, 少すこし遅おくれる

    The grammar of 遅おくれる: - Takes に for the missed target (電車でんしゃに遅おくれる — "late for the train") - Often paired with reasons in て-form (寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれる — "oversleep and be late") - Past tense 遅おくれた is a common apology opener

    電車でんしゃに遅おくれそう。 I think I'm going to miss the train [personal prediction].
    The そう adds prediction. The subject is "I" (implied), not the train.
    雨あめで遅おくれた。 I was delayed by rain [external cause, but still personal].
    The で marks external cause, but the subject is still the person who failed to arrive on time.

    遅おくれる vs. 遅刻ちこくする

    Both describe personal lateness, but 遅おくれる is the everyday word while 遅刻ちこくする is the institutional/formal one. You 遅おくれる to meet a friend; you 遅刻ちこくする to class or work.

    2. 遅おそくなる — Schedule Shift (Adjective + なる)¶

    Attribute Value
    Structure i-adjective 遅おそい + なる (to become)
    Agency Neutral — no fault implied
    Formality Casual to standard
    Scope Event, schedule, plan
    Collocations 会議かいぎが遅おそくなる, 到着とうちゃくが遅おそくなる, 終おわりが遅おそくなる

    The grammar of 遅おそくなる: - The subject is the event/time, not the person - Often used for future predictions (〜になる) - Can be modified with extent: 30分ふん遅おそくなる ("become 30 minutes late")

    今日きょうの終業しゅうぎょうは遅おそくなります。 Today's closing will be delayed [schedule announcement].
    The subject is 終業しゅうぎょう (closing time), not a person. This is a neutral schedule shift.
    到着とうちゃくが一時間いちじかん遅おそくなる。 Arrival will be one hour late.
    The 一いち時間じかん specifies the extent. The subject is 到着とうちゃく (arrival), an event.

    遅おそくなる vs. 遅おくれる

    遅おそくなる is about the clock shifting. 遅おくれる is about failing to meet the clock. A meeting 遅おそくなる (starts later); a person 遅おくれる (arrives after the start).

    3. 遅刻ちこくする — Institutional Tardiness (Formal Personal)¶

    Attribute Value
    Register Formal, institutional
    Agency Personal fault (usually)
    Scope Individual within an institution
    Collocations 遅刻ちこくする, 遅刻ちこくしました (apology), 遅刻ちこく扱あつかい (treated as tardy), 無断むだん遅刻ちこく (unauthorized tardiness)

    The grammar of 遅刻ちこくする: - Suru-verb (する verb) — formal and countable - Often used in institutional apologies (遅刻ちこくしました) - Can be nominalized: 遅刻ちこくは禁止きんしです ("Tardiness is prohibited")

    朝礼ちょうれいに遅刻ちこくしました。 I was late for the morning assembly [formal apology].
    Standard workplace/school apology. Using 遅おくれる here would sound too casual.
    三回さんかいの遅刻ちこくで警告けいこくです。 Three instances of tardiness result in a warning.
    Nominalized usage. 遅刻ちこく becomes a countable institutional offense.

    遅刻ちこくする is never systemic

    You cannot say 電車でんしゃが遅刻ちこくした for a train delay. 遅刻ちこくする requires a person who has failed to meet an institutional schedule.

    4. 遅延ちえんする — Systemic Delay (Organizational)¶

    Attribute Value
    Register Formal, technical, public
    Agency Organizational/systemic — no individual fault
    Scope Trains, flights, projects, services
    Collocations 遅延ちえんする, 遅延ちえん情報じょうほう (delay info), 大幅おおはば遅延ちえん (major delay), 遅延ちえん証明しょうめい書しょ (delay certificate)

    The grammar of 遅延ちえんする: - Suru-verb — formal and technical - Often progressive (遅延ちえんしています) for announcements - Used in public announcements, news, official reports

    本日ほんじつの新幹線しんかんせんは大雪おおゆきの影響えいきょうで遅延ちえんしています。 Today's Shinkansen is delayed due to heavy snow [announcement].
    Standard JR announcement. 遅延ちえんしています is the fixed public formula.
    遅延ちえん証明書しょうめいしょを発行はっこうします。 We will issue a delay certificate.
    The 遅延ちえん証明しょうめい書しょ is a formal document from train companies to excuse workplace tardiness.

    遅延ちえんする is never personal

    You cannot use 遅延ちえんする for yourself. It is exclusively for systems, organizations, and mechanical operations.

    Comparative Matrix¶

    Scenario 遅おくれる 遅おそくなる 遅刻ちこくする 遅延ちえんする
    I missed my train ✓ ✗ ✗ ✗
    The train is running late ✗ ✗ ✗ ✓
    The meeting will end later ✗ ✓ ✗ ✗
    I was late to work (informal) ✓ ✗ ✗ ✗
    I was late to work (formal apology) ✗ ✗ ✓ ✗
    The flight is postponed ✗ ✗ ✗ ✓
    My arrival time shifted later ✗ ✓ ✗ ✗
    I overslept and was late ✓ ✗ ✗ ✗
    Three tardies = warning ✗ ✗ ✓ (nominalized) ✗
    Project timeline extended ✗ ✗ ✗ ✓

    In Real Life¶

    Morning rush, informal

    Situation: タクヤ texts his friend シュン.

    寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれた。10分ぷんくらい待まってて。

    "I overslept and was late. Wait about 10 minutes."

    Why 遅おくれた: Personal, informal, fault-implied. 遅刻ちこくした would sound oddly formal for a friend. 遅延ちえん or 遅おそくなる don't fit — this is about his personal failure to arrive, not a schedule shift or system delay.

    Workplace announcement

    Situation: Manager emails team about a meeting.

    会議かいぎが30分ぷん遅おそくなります。

    "The meeting will be 30 minutes late."

    Why 遅おそくなる: The meeting time shifts. No fault, no person — just a schedule change. 遅おくれる would imply someone failed; 遅刻ちこくする or 遅延ちえんする are wrong register and scope.

    Formal apology to teacher

    Situation: Student arrives late to class.

    電車でんしゃが止とまって、遅刻ちこくしました。

    "The train stopped, and I was tardy."

    Why 遅刻ちこくしました: Institutional context requires institutional language. Even though the cause was the train, the student takes responsibility for the tardiness. 遅おくれた would be too casual for a teacher.

    Station announcement

    Situation: JR East station PA system.

    上野うえの方面ほうめんの電車でんしゃは人身事故じんしんじこの影響えいきょうで遅延ちえんしています。

    "Trains toward Ueno are delayed due to a passenger injury incident."

    Why 遅延ちえんしています: Public, systemic, organizational. The train company uses 遅延ちえんする as its technical term. 遅おくれる would sound personal and inappropriate; 遅刻ちこくする is for people; 遅おそくなる is too casual for public announcement.

    Nuance Comparison¶

    Grammar Point Core Meaning Agency / Fault Register Scope JLPT
    遅おくれる To be delayed / run late Often personal; fault implied Casual–standard Individual N4
    遅おそくなる To become late Neutral; schedule shift Casual–standard Event/time N4
    遅刻ちこくする To be tardy / arrive late Personal; institutional fault Formal Individual within institution N4
    遅延ちえんする To be delayed [systemic] Organizational; no personal fault Formal–technical System/organization N3
    延のびる To extend / stretch Neutral; duration expands Standard Time period N3

    Hidden Points¶

    • 遅おくれる can be used for things, not just people. 電車でんしゃが遅おくれた is acceptable in casual speech, though 遅延ちえんした is more formal. The boundary: personal perspective vs. public announcement.
    • 遅刻ちこくする can be nominalized and counted. 遅刻ちこくは3回かいまで ("Tardiness is limited to 3 times"). This institutional countability is unique to 遅刻ちこく.
    • 遅延ちえん証明しょうめい書しょ is a cultural institution. Japanese train companies issue these to excuse workplace tardiness. The existence of this document reveals how 遅延ちえん (systemic) and 遅刻ちこく (personal) are socially separated — the system acknowledges its fault to protect the individual from institutional penalty.
    • 遅おそくなる often pairs with specific times. 30分ふん遅おそくなる, 1時間じかん遅おそくなる. The extent specification is common because the shift is measurable and predictable.
    • 遅おくれる + そう = anxious prediction. 遅おくれそう ("I think I'll be late") is a common anxious self-talk pattern. 遅刻ちこくしそう is more formal prediction; 遅延ちえんしそう is systemic prediction.
    • The rain exception. 雨あめで遅おくれた (I was delayed by rain) uses 遅おくれる despite external cause. But 雨うで遅刻ちこくした sounds like you're blaming the rain for your institutional tardiness — slightly less acceptable. 遅延ちえん is never used for rain affecting a single person.

    Common Mistakes¶

    1. Using 遅刻ちこくする for systemic delays¶

    Scope error

    電車でんしゃが遅刻ちこくした。

    "The train was tardy." — Trains cannot be tardy; they are delayed. 遅刻ちこくする requires a person with institutional obligations.

    Correct

    電車でんしゃが遅延ちえんした。

    "The train was delayed." — Systemic, appropriate.

    2. Using 遅延ちえんする for personal lateness¶

    Register/scope error

    寝坊ねぼうして遅延ちえんした。

    "I overslept and was systemically delayed." — Bizarre. You are not a train.

    Correct

    寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれた。

    "I overslept and was late." — Personal, appropriate.

    3. Using 遅おそくなる for personal tardiness¶

    Category error

    私わたしが遅おそくなる。

    "I will become late." — Grammatically possible but pragmatically odd. People don't "become late" like schedules do; they "are delayed" or "are tardy."

    Correct

    私わたしは遅おくれる。

    "I will be late." — Personal delay, standard expression.

    Collocation Notes¶

    Collocation Meaning
    寝坊ねぼうして遅おくれる "Oversleep and be late" — classic personal delay
    電車でんしゃに遅おくれる "Miss the train / be late for the train" — personal
    会議かいぎが遅おそくなる "The meeting runs late" — schedule shift
    到着とうちゃくが遅おそくなる "Arrival becomes later" — predicted shift
    遅刻ちこくしました "I was tardy" — formal institutional apology
    無断むだん遅刻ちこく "Unauthorized tardiness" — institutional offense
    遅延ちえんしています "Is delayed" — public announcement formula
    大幅おおはば遅延ちえん "Major delay" — news/announcement
    遅延ちえん証明書しょうめいしょ "Delay certificate" — institutional excuse document
    遅刻ちこく扱あつかい "Treated as tardy" — institutional judgment

    Learner Stage¶

    Stage What It Looks Like
    Now — N4 Use 遅おくれる for personal lateness; recognize 遅刻ちこくする and 遅延ちえんする in context; avoid mixing personal and systemic scopes
    Developing — N4→N3 Deploy 遅おそくなる naturally for schedule shifts; choose 遅刻ちこくする vs. 遅おくれる based on institutional context; read 遅延ちえんしています without conscious decoding
    Automatic — N3→N2 The four-way distinction becomes intuitive — you feel the wrongness of 電車でんしゃが遅刻ちこくした before analyzing why. You automatically match register to context.
    Mastery You control the full lateness spectrum with precision — using 遅おくれる for personal fault, 遅おそくなる for neutral shifts, 遅刻ちこくする for institutional apologies, and 遅延ちえんする for public announcements. You also know when to use 延のびる for duration expansion and when 遅おそい as adjective is sufficient.

    See Also¶

    Same Family¶

    • 遅おそい (i-adjective) — "Late" — descriptive state
    • 早はやい / 速はやい — "Early / Fast" — the temporal opposites
    • 間まに合あう — "To be in time" — the positive outcome
    • 間まに合あわない — "To not be in time" — functional equivalent to 遅おくれる

    Commonly Confused With¶

    • 延のびる — "To extend" — duration expansion, not lateness
    • 伸のびる — "To stretch / extend" — physical or temporal expansion
    • 後あとれる — "To lag behind" — falling behind, not time-specific
    • 遅おくらせる — Causative of 遅おくれる — "To make late / delay [something]"

    Builds From¶

    • 遅おそい (N5) — Base adjective
    • Intransitive verbs (N5) — 遅おくれる as 自動じどう詞し
    • 〜なる (N5) — State change pattern
    • Suru-verbs (N4) — 遅刻ちこくする, 遅延ちえんする as formal verbs

    Unlocks Next¶

    • 延のびる / 伸のびる (N3) — Duration expansion beyond lateness
    • 後あとれる (N3) — Falling behind in sequence
    • 遅おくらせる (causative) (N4) — Making something late
